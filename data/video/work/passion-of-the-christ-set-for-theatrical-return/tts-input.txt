One of the most controversial films ever made is headed back to theaters. That's The Passion of the Christ. Mel Gibson's graphic biblical epic is reportedly prepping for a re-release. It would bring the film's intense depiction of Jesus's final hours back to the big screen. The report doesn't name dates or theaters, that's per The Washington Post. But Easter would be the obvious fit. A re-release would reignite all the old debates. It was hailed for its devotion and criticized for its violence. So the film's return could be a major cultural moment. Will you see it in theaters this time?
