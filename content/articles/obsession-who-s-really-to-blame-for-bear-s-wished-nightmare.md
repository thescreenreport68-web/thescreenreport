In the horror film *Obsession*, a shy, down-on-his-luck man named Bear makes a desperate wish on a magical willow branch for the woman of his dreams to love him. The wish works, but it spirals into a nightmare of possession and tragedy, leaving viewers fiercely divided over a central question: who, or what, is truly to blame?

## The Spine of the Debate: Bear's 'Inherently Evil' Wish

The overwhelming reaction online pins the blame squarely on Bear himself. Many argue his wish wasn't just poorly worded; it was morally corrupt from the start, seeking to override another person's free will.

One user wrote, "I think a MAJOR flaw in his wish was including ‘more than anything else in the world’."

This sentiment is echoed by others who see the act itself as the core sin. "His wish blew up in his face because it was straight up just a really bad wish," another viewer posted.

This critical lens extends to Bear's character, with viewers finding his awkwardness and specific choices revealing. A moment that particularly rankled audiences was his attempt at a pet name. "Bear calling her ‘Freaky Nikki’ made me cringe so hard the first time I saw this in theaters," one fan said. "It’s the fact that he thought he was being cute about it, and that their friendship was fond enough to say something like that. Super embarrassing."

## Sympathy for the Devil (and His Victim)

A smaller, but notable, contingent of viewers approaches the story with more pity, seeing Bear as a tragic figure whose loneliness led to a cataclysmic error. This group often pairs their sympathy for Bear with deep sadness for Nikki, the true victim of his wish.

"Poor Nikki, I felt really sad for her when she was saying how unhappy she was at her job and had no one to speak to. That's a very bad feeling," one viewer expressed. Another highlighted the haunting nature of her trapped existence: "It’s so sad hearing Nikki say that she just wants to go home and go to bed, but then all this happens."

Some even trace a sympathetic arc for Bear at the outset. "Personally at the start I was rooting for bear, he was a down on luck guy who resorted to cheap, magical gag gift on a whim not thinking it would work," a fan explained, acknowledging the tragic turn. The overarching feeling from this side is one of profound sadness for all involved. "God it’s just so sad how everyone was having a good time and bear made the absolute worst decision of his life that destroyed them all."

## Foreshadowing, Performance, and the Death of Media Literacy

Beyond the moral debate, fans are deeply appreciating the film's craft. The acting, particularly from Inde Naverette as the possessed Nikki, is receiving widespread praise. "The way she says yeah twice in the same expression and tone is creepy wtff Inde Naverette is so gooooodddddd," one viewer raved. Others noted, "her first realisation is so creepy and well acted."

The film's score and clever details are also getting attention. "i love the score when he broke the one wish willow its so haunting," posted a fan. Another spotted brilliant foreshadowing: "one cool detail in the start of the movie - in the bar scene before it cuts to them walking outside, the first question for trivia was 'What is one wish the blue genie from Aladdin can't make?' and the answer is to make someone."

A fascinating meta-reading has also emerged from the *Obsession* movie debate, commenting on the reactions themselves. One astute viewer posted, "it's funny reading up on what people are saying about obsession because even beyond the misogynist reactions there's also just the complete death of media literacy that is 'is nikki possessed by a demon/witch?' no how do you think anything beyond bear's wish is doing." This points to a deeper layer in the audience response, where some interpretations overlook the film's clear metaphor about possessive, selfish love.

## The Symbol at the Heart of Selfish Love

Perhaps the most chilling analysis from viewers concerns a seemingly minor detail: Bear's treatment of his cat. Several fans see it as the ultimate foreshadowing of his character flaw.

"I figure his treatment of the cat is symbolic of how he loves in general?" one user theorized. "It’s a very specific thing to foreshadow, the way he just disposed of something he should have loved once it no longer serves a purpose. Selfish love? Neglectful love?"

This reading reframes the entire story. It suggests Bear's wish for Nikki wasn't a one-off mistake, but the culmination of a pattern of seeing love as something that exists solely for his own benefit and comfort. The cat, and then Nikki, become objects to be cherished only while they serve his emotional needs, foreshadowing the horror that his 'granted' love would inevitably become.

As the conversation continues, it's clear *Obsession* has hit a nerve. The debate is less about supernatural mechanics and more about a very human horror: the toxicity of a love that demands possession over partnership, and the tragic ruins it leaves behind.
