A Spielberg Sci-Fi Epic

Steven Spielberg returns to the genre he helped define with '[Disclosure Day](/movies/disclosure-day-box-office-day-34-domestic-total-climbs-to-112-9-million/),' a high-stakes science fiction thriller. The film follows a cybersecurity expert who becomes a whistleblower after uncovering dangerous secrets about extraterrestrial life, forcing him on the run from a powerful corporation. His path collides with that of a meteorologist experiencing inexplicable phenomena, and together they must prove the existence of life beyond our understanding. The 146-minute film is a tense fusion of corporate conspiracy thriller and classic Spielbergian wonder.

## An All-Star Ensemble Cast

The film boasts a formidable cast led by Emily Blunt as Margaret Fairchild, the meteorologist drawn into the mystery. Josh O'Connor plays Daniel Kellner, the cybersecurity expert turned fugitive whistleblower. Colin Firth appears as Noah Scanlon, with Colman Domingo as Hugo Wakefield and Eve Hewson as Jane Blakenship. The supporting roles are filled by Wyatt Russell as Jackson, Elizabeth Marvel as Sister Maura, and Henry Lloyd-Hughes as Casper Boyd, creating a rich tapestry of characters caught in the escalating conspiracy.

'Disclosure Day' represents a major cinematic event, bringing together one of cinema's greatest directors with a top-tier cast for a story that probes the intersection of corporate power and cosmic truth.

## At the Box Office

Disclosure Day has grossed $112,925,320 at the domestic box office, 34 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 2,204 theaters. The film added $452,270 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $230 million. It carries a reported production budget of $115 million.
