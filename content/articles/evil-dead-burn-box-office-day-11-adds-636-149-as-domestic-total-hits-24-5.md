A Fiery Franchise Falters at the Box Office

The debut marks a steep underperformance compared to recent series entries, signaling a potential disconnect with audiences despite the property's enduring cult status.

Directed by Sébastien Vaniček, *[Evil Dead Burn](/movies/evil-dead-burn-box-office-day-10-adds-1-5-million-as-domestic-total-hits-24-2/)* is a 110-minute horror-thriller that follows Alice (Souheila Yacoub), who seeks solace with her late husband's family after his sudden death. The family are descendants of a leading researcher on demonic possession, and as Alice's new in-laws begin transforming into creatures that feed on fear, she discovers that the vows she took in life survive even in death. The film continues the series' tradition of visceral, supernatural terror.

The cast is led by Souheila Yacoub as the grieving widow, Alice. She is joined by Tandi Wright as Susan, Hunter Doohan as Joseph, and Luciane Buchanan as Thya. Erroll Shand portrays Edgar, with Maude Davey as Polly, George Pullar as Will, and Victory Ndukwe rounding out the ensemble as Leo. The film focuses on Alice's nightmarish introduction to this possessed family lineage.

This **Evil Dead Burn box office performance** places it at the bottom of the recent series revivals. The film played in 3,004 theaters domestically.

This is a notable result for a series where all six films have maintained positive Rotten Tomatoes scores, indicating that critical approval did not translate to a strong commercial opening this time.

## At the Box Office

Evil Dead Burn has grossed $24,520,594 at the domestic box office, 11 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,004 theaters. The film added $636,149 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $35 million. It carries a reported production budget of $20 million.
