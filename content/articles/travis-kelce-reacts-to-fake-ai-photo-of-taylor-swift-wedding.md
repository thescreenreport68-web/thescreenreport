What do you do when a fake photo of your wedding goes viral? If you're Travis Kelce, you laugh it off.

Ahead of the Kansas City Chiefs tight end's rumored wedding to Taylor Swift, comedian Bert Kreischer shared an AI-generated picture on Instagram on July 2. The fake photo showed himself standing alongside a smiling, AI-generated Swift and Kelce in wedding attire. Kreischer, however, is looking away from the camera. He explained the moment in his caption: "The moment @jason.kelce called me over to the bar for a shot."

Instead of getting upset, Kelce took the joke in stride. He responded in the comment section with a collection of crying laughing emojis, according to E! News.

This lighthearted reaction comes amid a whirlwind of speculation about the couple's nuptials. Reports from outlets like Page Six suggest a wedding could be imminent, with a rumored July 3 ceremony at New York City's Madison Square Garden. The couple has not confirmed or denied these reports.

Guest sightings have only added fuel to the fire. Swift's friends, including Ed Sheeran, Sabrina Carpenter, and Camila Cabello, were reportedly seen in New York City on July 1, the day before a rumored rehearsal dinner. Bestie Selena Gomez also hinted at attending, writing on her Instagram Stories on July 2, "I wish benny was here rn."

Swift's soon-to-be mother-in-law, Donna Kelce, was seen arriving at Madison Square Garden for the rumored rehearsal dinner after landing in New York City.

While the internet buzzes with wedding plans, the actual details remain firmly in the realm of rumor. The bride and groom have stayed silent, letting the speculation—and the occasional AI gag—run wild.
