A new trailer has arrived for the upcoming 4K restoration of René Laloux's final feature, the cult animation classic *Gandahar*. According to The Film Stage, Janus Films has acquired the restoration and will begin rolling it out in theaters starting August 28 at New York's IFC Center.

The release will feature the original, uncut French edition of the film. This marks a significant departure from the heavily altered English-language dub that was previously released in the United States. With this acquisition, Janus Films now holds the rights to all three of the visionary French animator's features.

## What is the story of 'Gandahar'?

The film is set on the peaceful planet of Gandahar, a utopia suddenly threatened when people on its frontiers are turned to stone. A council of matriarchs sends the inexperienced warrior Sylvain, voiced by Pierre-Marie Escourrou, to investigate. His journey leads him to team up with a race of mutants called the Deformed and discover that a gargantuan brain named Metamorphis (Georges Wilson) is behind the invasion. Sylvain must then travel through time with the warrior Airelle (Catherine Chevallier) to save their world.

## Who made 'Gandahar'?

Directed by René Laloux, *Gandahar* was his final film. It features surreal creature and environment designs by comic-book artist Caza, contributing to its reputation as a visually poetic and mind-bending work. The film blends animation, science fiction, and fantasy, exploring themes of utopia and invasion.

## Where can I watch 'Gandahar'?

As of July 2026, the 1987 film is currently available to stream on Amazon Prime Video. The new 4K restoration will begin its theatrical life in late August, offering fans a chance to see Laloux's original vision on the big screen.
