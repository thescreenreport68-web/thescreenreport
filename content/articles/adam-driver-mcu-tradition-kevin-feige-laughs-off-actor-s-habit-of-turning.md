Marvel Studios president Kevin Feige confirmed Adam Driver has a tradition of turning down roles in the [Marvel](/movies/avengers-doomsday-trailer-sent-fans-into-a-frenzy-and-the-skeptics-came-out/) Cinematic Universe, according to TheWrap. Feige made the remarks while promoting the upcoming film "Spider-Man: Brand New Day."

He described a recurring pattern of approaching the actor for potential parts.

## What did Kevin Feige say about Adam Driver?

"It’s a tradition to Zoom with him and then he passes," Feige said. "That’s a long-lived tradition." The Marvel president laughed about the habit but clarified his interest in casting Driver remains undeterred. "It’s no secret we’d like to," Feige replied when asked about getting Driver into the MCU.

This latest comment follows recent rumors linking Driver to roles within the emerging [X-Men](/movies/tramell-tillman-reportedly-reprises-william-metzger-as-first-x-men-movie/) franchise, such as Magneto or Mister Sinister. Feige expressed confidence that Marvel will eventually find the right role for the actor, despite the repeated passes.

## What else did Feige discuss?

The interview proved candid on other topics. Feige also touched on the production difficulties facing Mahershala Ali's "Blade" movie, which was teased in a post-credit scene for "Eternals." "I am feeling like a gigantic loser and failure that we didn’t get off the ground with Mahershala," Feige admitted.

The film stars Tom Holland, Zendaya, and Jon Bernthal, and is directed by [Destin Daniel Cretton](/movies/destin-daniel-cretton-discusses-the-personal-stakes-of-spider-man-brand-new/).
