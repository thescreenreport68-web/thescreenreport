Taylor Ortega, Tim Meadows, and Dan Bucatinsky have joined the cast of Netflix's upcoming reboot of the 2004 film *13 Going on 30*. According to Deadline, the trio have come aboard for undisclosed roles in the sequel.

They join the previously announced cast members Emily Bader, Logan Lerman, Adeline Rudolph, and Jessica Alba. Details about the plot and their specific characters have not been revealed.


Production on the project kicked off in Los Angeles last month. The original film's star, Jennifer Garner, is onboard as an executive producer.

## Who is behind the camera?

Brett Haley is directing from a script by Hannah Marks, with revisions by Flora Greeson. Joe Roth and Jeff Kirschenbaum are producing for RK Films, with Garner executive producing alongside Donna Roth, Susan Arnold, Scott Hemming, Alyssa Altman, Gina Matthews, and Ted Gidlow.

The 2004 romantic comedy starred Jennifer Garner as a 13-year-old girl who magically wakes up as her 30-year-old self, navigating adulthood while rediscovering her true identity. The film grossed roughly $96 million in theaters and has remained a cultural touchstone, leading to this new adaptation.
