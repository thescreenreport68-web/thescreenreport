After nine months away from the streaming spotlight, Kai Cenat is officially back in the game. The 24-year-old YouTuber and streamer announced his return to content creation on Instagram and X/Twitter, setting a date for his first stream back.

"Streamer University official reveal stream July 6th at 8pm EST be there," he wrote in all caps on Instagram, according to Just Jared. He followed up on X with a simple, "Missed yall."

According to Just Jared, he said, "A lot of the times I am boxed into this box of being a streamer. So, this is one of the first times where I’m going to display something outside of my primary category." He cited a desire to focus on other projects, like his clothing brand *Vivet*, and to take a break from a grueling streaming schedule.

Cenat also reportedly shared his feelings of self-doubt about the pivot. According to the outlet, he explained, "Usually, the times I’ve achieved so much, and I’ve been able to knock down goals I always wanted to do and my mindset in my head, it’s like a lot of self-doubt." He added, "I’m not even depressed. I’m not sad. It’s just for some reason I’m having like a lot of self-doubt because it’s out of fear of stepping back from what I’m primarily known for, which is content creation and pursuing in another goal."

The return is framed around the revival of "Streamer University," an event he first launched last year. The original event selected 120 content creators to, as Just Jared reports, "have the opportunity to showcase their personalities and brand and meet other creators." It featured other creators acting as professors and mentors. His July 6th stream is billed as the "official reveal" for the event's return, though specific details about this year's format or participants have not yet been announced.

Fans who have been waiting since his early June teasers now have a concrete time to tune in. The comeback signals Cenat's attempt to balance his established streaming empire with his ventures outside the digital box he once described.
