Malta Film Commissioner Johann Grech has outlined ambitious plans to build a new soundstage designed to attract major Hollywood productions to the island nation, according to an interview with The Hollywood Reporter.

Grech, the chief of Malta’s film operations, discussed the developments at the recent Mediterrane Film Festival. He confirmed the government has now approved the project, which he calls the Land Sea Super Stage.

## What is the Land Sea Super Stage?

The project is more than a standard soundstage. It will feature a massive water tank inside the facility that can be used dry or wet. The tank will be six meters deep and the size of a football pitch, with a height of 20 meters. Grech stated the structure would be strong enough to hold two Black Hawk helicopters, a feature intended to make it unique on the global market.

"We want to create something unique — and unique is the first global Land Sea Super Stage," Grech told THR.

## When will it be built?

Grech said the project is greenlit and that officials are finalizing paperwork to issue an international call for financing, construction, and operation. The chosen operator will run the studios under a 65-year concession. Building the entire facility, which will also include workshops, offices, and warehouses, is expected to take two years.

## Why is this stage needed?

The commissioner said Malta has lost projects because it lacked adequate soundstage infrastructure. He cited the *Jurassic World* films as examples where having a stage would have increased shoot days and local employment. "Completing the Land Sea Super Stage will allow Malta to host the big franchise blockbusters and more high-end TV shows," Grech said, framing it as an investment to "win the future."

## What else is Malta doing to grow its industry?

Alongside the physical infrastructure, Grech highlighted efforts to build a sustainable, year-round industry. This includes a recently launched €2 million scholarship fund to support and upskill local crews. As of July 2026, Malta has hosted 17 projects this year, including TV series, six feature films, and animation projects, with the majority coming from the U.K. and U.S. markets.

The Mediterrane Film Festival, now in its fourth year, is a key part of the strategy to bring international decision-makers to the island to see its offerings firsthand.
