[Shipwrecked: Nightmare at Sea](/streaming/shipwrecked-nightmare-at-sea-debuts-at-2-on-netflix-with-14-million-hours-viewed/) is a gripping documentary that immerses viewers in the harrowing 2012 shipwreck of the luxury cruise liner Costa Concordia. The film traces the disaster that unfolded when the ship struck rocks off the coast of Italy, resulting in 32 fatalities. Through a combination of never-before-seen footage and firsthand survivor accounts, the documentary offers an intense, real-time experience of the tragedy.

Directed by Chiara Messineo, the 87-minute documentary features survivors playing themselves, including John and Meghan Scimone, Patricia Sandoval, Nicholas Taliaferro, Stefania Vincenzi, Manrico Giampedroni, and Rose Metcalf. The film also includes footage of Francesco Schettino, the ship’s captain, whose decisions led to the disaster.

Currently streaming on Netflix, Shipwrecked: Nightmare at Sea has amassed 17 million hours viewed this week, earning the #1 spot on Netflix’s Top 10.

Audiences are drawn to the documentary’s emotional depth and immersive storytelling. The use of never-before-seen cell phone footage and black box recordings creates a vivid, unsettling atmosphere that brings the disaster to life. Survivor interviews reveal the trauma and resilience of those who lived through the sinking, making the story resonate deeply. This combination of raw footage and personal testimony explains why Shipwrecked: Nightmare at Sea continues to dominate Netflix’s charts.
