A new collection of Destiny's Child remixes is on the way. According to Rolling Stone, the group's former manager and Beyoncé's father, Mathew Knowles, revealed the project during an interview with SiriusXM's Page Six Radio on July 22.

Knowles stated the remixes would arrive within the next month. "We have a number of remixes that have never been released, and we’ll be putting those out soon," he said. The new tracks will feature a mix of styles, including dance mixes and urban mixes, all built around the vocals of the iconic trio.

## Who is featured on the new Destiny's Child remixes?

The project will include guest appearances from other artists. Knowles specifically teased that Missy Elliott appears on one of the tracks. He indicated there are other featured rappers but did not name them. It is not yet clear if the remixes will be packaged as a new album or released as individual singles.

## What is the context for this news?

The announcement comes as Destiny's Child recently marked the anniversary of their 1999 album, *The Writing's on the Wall*, on social media. The group, which last released an album in 2004, has a deep catalog of hits and remains one of the best-selling girl groups of all time. This project taps into a vault of unreleased material, offering fans new takes on classic sounds.

Knowles, who managed the group during their peak, is spearheading the release. He emphasized the volume of unheard music, saying, "We have so much music that hadn't been put out yet on Destiny's Child."
