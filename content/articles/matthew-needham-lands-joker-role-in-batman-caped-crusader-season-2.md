Matthew Needham will voice the Joker in the second season of the animated series *Batman: Caped Crusader*, according to Variety. The news was revealed at San Diego Comic-Con, where the actor joined the show's cast and producers for a panel.

Needham, known for his role in *[House of the Dragon](/tv/house-of-the-dragon-s-steve-toussaint-breaks-down-corlys-s-dangerous-defian/)*, joins a long line of actors who have lent their voice to the Clown Prince of Crime. His character was teased at the end of the show's first season, which premiered in 2024.

## When does Season 2 come out?

The 10-episode second season will be available to stream on Amazon Prime Video on July 31. It will be released as a binge drop in more than 240 countries and territories worldwide. The Comic-Con panel followed a screening of the Season 2 premiere episode.

## Who else is in the cast?

Needham joins a large ensemble voice cast led by Hamish Linklater as Batman and Bruce Wayne. Jamie Chung returns as Harley Quinn, with other returning cast members including John DiMaggio, Michelle C. Bonilla, and Krystal Joy Brown. The full cast list also features Eric Morgan Stuart, Jason Watkins, Gary Anthony Williams, Wunmi Mosaku, and Tom Kenny, among others.

The series is a hard-boiled reimagining of Batman's early years, forged from tragedy as Bruce Wayne wages a one-man war on crime in Gotham City. It is executive produced by J.J. Abrams, Matt Reeves, and Bruce Timm, alongside James Tucker, Geoffrey Thorne, and Sam Register.

## What about the video game?

Amazon's gaming platform, Luna, also screened a trailer for an upcoming video game tie-in titled *Batman: Caped Crusader – Chronicles*. The game is set to launch on July 31, the same day as the new season. Needham will also portray the Joker in this game, alongside Linklater and Michelle C. Bonilla.

Season 1 of *Batman: Caped Crusader* is currently streaming on Amazon Prime Video.
