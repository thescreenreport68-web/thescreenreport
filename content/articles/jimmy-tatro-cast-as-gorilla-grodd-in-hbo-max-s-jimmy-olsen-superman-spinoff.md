Jimmy Tatro is reportedly joining the DC Universe as the villain Gorilla Grodd in an upcoming HBO Max series.

According to Deadline, Tatro will officially bring to life the hyper-intelligent telepathic ape in a planned Superman spinoff series centered on Daily Planet photographer Jimmy Olsen. The project, which sources say has not yet been officially greenlit by HBO, will be a true-crime docuseries with Jimmy Olsen hosting.

## What is the series about?

The spinoff is billed as being akin to a true-crime docuseries with Jimmy Olsen from *Superman* hosting. Its first season will reportedly be centered around the DC bad guy Gorilla Grodd, a character who often wrestles The Flash and has sought to rule both the simian and human worlds. Grodd gained psychic powers and other skills after an encounter with an alien spaceship. The character was created in 1959 by John Broome and Carmine Infantino.

## Who else is involved?

Skyler Gisondo, who played Jimmy Olsen in the summer blockbuster *Superman* directed by James Gunn, is set to star in the series. The show comes from Tony Yacenda and Dan Perrault, the creators of Netflix's satirical true-crime series *American Vandal*. They will write, executive produce, and showrun the series. DC Studios bosses James Gunn and Peter Safran are executive producers, with Galen Vaisman overseeing production. Warner Bros. Television is also producing.

Tatro's casting arrives as the actor promotes his next project, the Will Ferrell Netflix golf comedy series *The Hawk*. HBO Max declined to comment on the report.
