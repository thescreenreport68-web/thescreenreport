Vince Marcello is set to direct and produce an adaptation of the YA novel *Son of the Mob*, according to Deadline. The filmmaker, known for helming Netflix's *The Kissing Booth* trilogy, will work from a script he co-wrote with his frequent collaborator Jay Arnold.

Red Sage Studios and Sun Distribution have added the romantic comedy to their development slate. The story follows Vince, a teenager desperate for a normal life despite being the son of a mob boss. His world collides with comedic consequences when he falls for the daughter of the FBI agent investigating his family.

Marcello expressed his immediate connection to the material. **“I was laughing from the opening scene,”** he told Deadline. “Being Italian American myself, I could immediately visualize these characters, and I was in from the moment James shared the project with me.”

## Who is adapting 'Son of the Mob'?

Marcello is directing and producing, reuniting with screenwriter Jay Arnold. The pair previously collaborated on the second and third installments of the *The Kissing Booth* film series for Netflix. While those films debuted before Netflix's current viewership reporting standards, available data indicated they were significant hits for the streamer.

## What is the production status?

Filming is slated to take place in Canada with Vancouver-based Brightlight Pictures. The project joins other adaptations on Red Sage Studios' development slate.

The film is based on the novel by Canadian children's and YA author Gordon Korman.
