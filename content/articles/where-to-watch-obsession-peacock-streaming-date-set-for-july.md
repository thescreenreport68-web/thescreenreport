Curry Barker's breakout horror hit *Obsession* will be available to stream on Peacock starting July 17, according to reports from Variety and Deadline. The film, which has grossed over $370 million worldwide, will hit the streaming platform after an extended and highly successful theatrical run that began in mid-May.

The announcement confirms the streaming home for one of the year's biggest cinematic surprises. Its arrival on Peacock marks a 63-day window from its theatrical debut, a timeline extended because the film continued to draw crowds in theaters week after week.

## Is Obsession streaming or still in theaters?

As of July 2026, *Obsession* is available for home viewing. You can currently rent or buy the film on digital storefronts including Amazon Video, Apple TV, Google Play Movies, and YouTube.

The streaming debut on Peacock on July 17 will provide the first opportunity to watch the film with a subscription, no rental fee required.

## When will Obsession hit streaming?

The confirmed streaming date for *Obsession* is July 17, 2026, on Peacock. This date represents a strategic release for the streaming service, capitalizing on the film's sustained cultural buzz. The delay from its initial digital release window was a direct result of its unprecedented box office legs; the film kept earning in theaters, so the studio pushed back its streaming debut.

This kind of holdback is rare, especially for a horror film, and speaks to the movie's extraordinary performance. It opened to a strong domestic debut but then did something almost unheard of—it earned more in its second and third weekends, a clear sign of powerful word-of-mouth.

## What is Obsession about?

The film follows Bear, played by Michael Johnston, a hopeless romantic who is too nervous to confess his feelings for his co-worker, Nikki, portrayed by Inde Navarrette. In a desperate attempt to win her heart, Bear makes a wish on a mysterious object known as the "One Wish Willow," asking for Nikki to love him more than anything else in the world.

He gets exactly what he wished for, but the outcome is a dark and sinister twist. Nikki becomes dangerously obsessed with Bear, leading to fatal consequences for everyone involved. The film blends horror and thriller elements with a story about the perils of getting what you ask for.

## Why was Obsession such a big hit?

*Obsession* became a cultural phenomenon through sheer word-of-mouth. It earned a rare "A-" grade on CinemaScore exit polls, which is exceptional for a horror film, and boasts a 94% critics score on Rotten Tomatoes. Audiences responded to its unique mix of romance and horror, its sharp execution, and star-making performances.

Acquired by Focus Features, its journey from a low-budget indie to a global box office powerhouse is a modern success story. The film's performance has sparked conversations about its potential during the upcoming awards season, with some critics drawing comparisons to the trajectory of Jordan Peele's *Get Out*.
