Brett Gelman, Kevin Nealon, and Rob Morgan have joined the cast of the upcoming feature comedy **"Mini War,"** according to Deadline. The trio will star alongside previously announced leads Luke Wilson, Vanessa Hudgens, and Nick Swardson.

Gelman will play Ned Parker, an eccentric investor from Milwaukee who is eager to purchase the mini-golf course Wilson's character inherits. Morgan takes on the role of Ruben, the manager of that same course who helped build it with the previous owner.

## What is 'Mini War' about?

The film centers on Joe (Wilson), a likable stoner who unexpectedly inherits a miniature golf course from his late uncle. His plans to sell it and move on are thwarted by his neighbor Darren (Swardson), the eccentric owner of a competing go-kart track who wants to bulldoze the golf course to expand his own business. Vanessa Hudgens plays Olivia, who runs a food stand caught in the middle of their escalating feud.

## Who else is involved?

The project reunites key creative talent from the 2006 stoner comedy *Grandma's Boy*. Nick Swardson and Allen Covert wrote the script, with Joel David Moore directing. The film is being produced by Jarod Einsohn, Moore, and others from Level 1 Entertainment and Toric Films. Capture Entertainment has acquired the worldwide film sales rights.

The supporting cast also includes Dave Sheridan, Blake Anderson, John Farley, and Nicholas Turturro, among others.
