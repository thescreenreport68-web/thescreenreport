Disney+ has ordered a pilot for a reboot of the classic variety series *The Mickey Mouse Club*, according to Deadline. Production on the pilot is scheduled to begin this month in Los Angeles.

The project is being developed by Fulwell Entertainment, the production company behind Hulu's *The Kardashians*. The pilot will feature 11 new young performers, aiming to be a "showcase for today’s most talented young performers, where music, innovation, and self-expression take center stage," Disney said.

## Who are the new young performers?

The cast for the pilot includes Hudson Stone, Casey Trotter, and Brooklynn Pitts, who all star in Disney's upcoming *Camp Rock 3*. They are joined by Erianthe Akaata (*Young Rock*), Scarlett London Diviney (*The Lion King*), Michael Cash (*Black Rabbit*), Yonas Kibreab (*Elio*), Varonica Mitchell, Kauani, Scarlett Grace Petty, and Carter Barnes.

## What is the history of the show?

The original *Mickey Mouse Club*, created by Walt Disney, aired on ABC from 1955 to 1959. It was revived as a syndicated series from 1977 to 1979 and again by the Disney Channel from 1989 to 1996. That final iteration became famous for launching the careers of numerous pop culture icons, including Britney Spears, Justin Timberlake, Christina Aguilera, Ryan Gosling, and Keri Russell.

The pilot is executive produced by Fulwell co-founder Ben Winston, Ashley Edens, and Emma Conway and Dave Piendak. The original series is currently available to stream on Disney Plus.
