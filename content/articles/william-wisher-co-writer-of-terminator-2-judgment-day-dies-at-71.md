William Wisher, the co-writer of the iconic film *Terminator 2: Judgment Day*, has died at the age of 71. According to TheWrap, he passed away at his home on July 14.

Wisher’s death was confirmed to the outlet by his brother, John. In a statement, the family remembered him as a kind and imaginative man. “William believed that stories have the power to connect people across generations,” they wrote.

## Who was William Wisher?

Wisher was a boyhood friend of director James Cameron. Their shared love of science fiction led Cameron to recruit him to help develop the concept for *The Terminator* in the early 1980s. Wisher drafted scenes for the film, which Cameron would transcribe over the phone, and he even made a cameo as an LAPD officer.

He later received a co-screenwriting credit for the landmark sequel, *Terminator 2: Judgment Day*. Wisher recalled the creative risk of turning Arnold Schwarzenegger’s T-800 into a hero. “That was a daunting decision at the time,” he said in the biography *The Futurist*.

## What other films did he write?

Following the success of *T2*, Wisher built a screenwriting career through the 1990s and 2000s. He was also an executive producer on *Live Free or Die Hard* (2007).

Born on May 23, 1955, Wisher began his career as an actor, starring in Cameron’s 1978 short film *Xenogenesis*. He is survived by his wife of 22 years, Deborah, and his siblings.
