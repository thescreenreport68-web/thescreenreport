The first trailer for the new [Scary Movie](/movies/scary-movie-2026-the-internet-is-split-between-nostalgic-cheers-and/) sequel has arrived, and the reaction was instant — and loud. For many, the sight of familiar characters and that signature 2000s slapstick felt like a time machine. But as the initial hype settled, a clear split emerged — some fans are losing it over the return, while others are convinced the magic is gone for good.

## The Wayans Are Back — and So Is That Bong Popcorn Bucket

For a big chunk of the audience, the trailer was pure comfort food. The callbacks, the irreverent tone, the Wayans' brand of humor — it all clicked. One user wrote, "Watching this trailer is like bumping into an old classmate 15 years later." The sentiment was shared by many, happy to see a beloved franchise again. Another fan celebrated the return of a legendary dim-witted character, posting, "When the world needed him the most, Doofy returned." The commitment to the roots was a major selling point, with one viewer stating, "The greatest thing about this movie is they're staying true to the franchise and not watering it down I can't wait to see this."

The details mattered too: from the bong-shaped popcorn bucket to the Ghostface licensing. "This is the only Scary Movie out of the two with Ghostface where they call him Ghostface. Means they licensed the costume this time," one fan noted approvingly. The meta-humor also landed, with a viewer praising a moment where the film tells audiences to "just go see the fricken movie!"

## Then the Jokes Stopped Landing

But the Scary Movie 2026 trailer reaction wasn't all celebration. Almost immediately, a counter-narrative of skepticism bubbled up. This was perfectly captured in a single, edited post that went viral for encapsulating the emotional rollercoaster. It began with unbridled excitement: "2000s comedies are BACK BABY!!! 😂😂😂" Then, after a pause for reflection, came the sobering addendum: "Edit: Ok, so I was wrong."

That post became the emblem for viewers who felt the comedy fell flat. Some criticisms were blunt and focused on the craft. One harsh review read, "Nothing about this clip is even a little funny...they lost their magic sadly after the first 2. The writing in this was horrific and all over the place." Others took a more cynical view, with one offering a bleak prediction: "RT rating prediction 10% percent critics 90% audiences Wasn’t far off."

## Why the 2026 Audience Can't Agree

The division makes sense when you consider what Scary Movie represents. For its fans, it's a specific time capsule of early-2000s humor — broad, unapologetic, and deeply silly. Seeing that style return, untouched by modern sensibilities, is the entire appeal. It's comfort food comedy.

But comedy tastes evolve, and the shock-and-awe tactics of two decades ago can feel dated or lazy now. The skepticism isn't just about quality; it's about whether this brand of parody can find its footing in a new era. Interestingly, the film seems aware of this shift. One fan pointed out the new target, posting, "I can’t believe Scary Movie gang are now parodying elevated/prestige horror lol." This nod to the genre's evolution, including a joke about Jordan Peele, shows an attempt to bridge old formula with new targets.

The discourse isn't cooling off any time soon. The core fans are locked in for opening night, ready to relive their youth. The skeptics are waiting for reviews, convinced the best days are behind it. The ultimate test won't be the trailer, but whether the movie can make that 2000s class reunion feel like a party, or just a reminder of how much everyone has changed.
