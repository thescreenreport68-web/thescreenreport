Warner Bros. Discovery U.K. has ordered three new seasons of the long-running panel show **‘Mock the Week’** and a brand-new Top Trumps-themed comedy series. The announcements are part of a comedy slate for the free-to-air channel TLC, according to Variety.

The new show, tentatively titled **‘Top Trumps: The Comedy Pack,’** will be hosted by comedian Katherine Ryan. Team captains Joel Dommett and Jon Richardson will lead rival teams through card face-offs and live studio challenges. The show is set to launch early next year.

“Everyone knows Top Trumps, but no one has ever played it quite like this,” Ryan said. “We’re taking this iconic game and supersizing it into something bigger, louder and much funnier. And with Jon and Joel captaining rival teams, chaos is pretty much guaranteed.”

The three-season order for ‘Mock the Week’ will extend the series to a fifth season in 2027. The show, hosted by Dara Ó Briain, was revived on TLC earlier this year after a hiatus that began in 2022.

Additionally, the network has renewed the comedy format **‘Unacceptable,’** hosted by Ed Gamble, for a second season. The slate represents a concerted effort by the channel, which relaunched earlier this year, to invest in comedy programming.

Graham Lafferty, WBD’s SVP for content strategy and networks in the U.K. and Ireland, said the new Top Trumps format is “perfectly placed for TLC’s growing U.K. comedy line-up.”

## What is the new Top Trumps show?

The new panel show is based on the classic card game and will feature live challenges and bluffing games. It is being produced by TalkbackThames with Naked.

## When does the new ‘Mock the Week’ season air?

The next season of ‘Mock the Week’ is set to premiere soon on TLC. The three-season renewal will carry the show through to 2027.
