Director Na Hong-jin previewed his upcoming alien action film *Hope* at San Diego Comic-Con, according to Variety. The South Korean auteur made his debut at the convention to discuss the project and show exclusive footage.

Na described *Hope* as a continuation of the thematic metaphors in his previous work, but a complete departure in style and content. He advised the audience to abandon any assumptions from films like *The Chaser* or *The Wailing* before seeing this one.

## What is the movie about?

The film is set in the remote village of Hope Harbor, where a mysterious creature wreaks havoc. Local police and hunters set out to track the beast, only to find themselves becoming the hunted. Na emphasized that perceptions are misleading in the story.

## Who stars in the film?

The international cast is led by Hwang Jung-min, with Hoyeon playing a tenacious police officer. Na said casting Hoyeon was one of his best decisions for the project, a ten-year passion project. Hwang had recommended her for the role, telling the director, "He said that if you meet her, you realize how special of a person she is." The cast also includes Michael Fassbender, Alicia Vikander, Taylor Russell, and Cameron Britton.

## What did the Comic-Con footage show?

Hoyeon introduced two exclusive clips via a video message. The first showed Hwang Jung-min and a group of men fighting through their town's wreckage before confronting a gigantic alien. The second was an extended chase scene where a character leaps from a horse into a police car while being pursued down a highway by a fanged creature.

Na revealed the most difficult scene to shoot was a different chase sequence with characters fleeing on horseback through a forest. He also praised Hoyeon's dedication, sharing that she spent six months learning to drive a manual car to perform her own stunts. "After six months, she reached a point where she could perform those drafts at very different, difficult angles too," Na said.

## Will there be a sequel?

During the panel's Q&A, Na teased that "90%" of the characters in the film might perish. The film premiered in competition at this year's Cannes Film Festival, where Na stated he already has a script for a potential sequel. "I think you can readily imagine this sequel. And there’s a script that’s already been done that I’d like to shoot," he said.
