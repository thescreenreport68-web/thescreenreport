A new documentary titled "Disney Worldbuilders" is coming soon to Disney+, according to a report from ABC7 New York. The film will go behind the scenes with some of the company's most influential storytellers.

## Who is featured in the documentary?
The documentary features interviews with a roster of top creative talent. That list includes Marvel Studios president [Kevin Feige](/movies/adam-driver-mcu-tradition-kevin-feige-laughs-off-actor-s-habit-of-turning/), Oscar-winning filmmaker James Cameron, and director-producer Jon Favreau, whose work spans the Marvel Cinematic Universe and the Star Wars franchise.

Also appearing are Pixar Chief Creative Officer Pete Docter, Oscar-winning writer and director of "Frozen" Jennifer Lee, and Jared Bush, Chief Creative Officer of Disney Animation Studios. The Walt Disney Company CEO Josh D'Amaro and former CEO Bob Iger are also featured.

## Who directed 'Disney Worldbuilders'?
The documentary is directed by Leslie Iwerks, who has a personal history with Disney. She is the granddaughter of Ub Iwerks, a Disney Legend and the co-creator of Mickey Mouse alongside Walt Disney. Her previous documentary work includes "The Imagineering Story" and "Disneyland Handcrafted."

## What does the teaser reveal?
A teaser for the film includes on-camera insights from the featured creators. In it, Jon Favreau discusses the fusion of technology and narrative, saying, "Technology with this great piece of storytelling creates this immersive experience."

Pete Docter emphasizes the importance of authenticity in world-building, stating, "If you really want people to believe in it, you've gotta do it right." The documentary aims to explore how the iconic worlds of Marvel, Pixar, Star Wars, and Avatar are conceived and brought to life.

"Disney Worldbuilders" is slated to premiere on Disney+ soon. The report did not specify an exact release date.
