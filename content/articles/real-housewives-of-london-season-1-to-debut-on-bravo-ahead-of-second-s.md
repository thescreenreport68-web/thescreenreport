London's calling, and Bravo is finally answering. The first season of *The Real Housewives of London*, which originally premiered on Hayu in the UK, will debut on Bravo in the US on July 21, and has already been renewed for a second season.

According to Deadline, Bravo has acquired the Hayu Original series, with the premiere set for Tuesday, July 21 at 9 p.m. ET/PT. The network will air back-to-back episodes starting August 4th, with all episodes streaming on Peacock the following day.

The move brings a dose of British glamour and drama to the American reality TV giant. The series follows six women—Juliet Angus, Amanda Cronin, Karen Loderick-Peace, Juliet Mayhew, Panthea Parker, and Nessie Welschinger—navigating life, legacy, and loyalty across London's most prestigious postcodes.

For Bravo fans, one name will ring a bell: Juliet Angus. She's a veteran of the network's own *Ladies of London*, which ran for three seasons starting in 2014. Her return to the Bravo universe adds a layer of continuity and intrigue for stateside viewers.

The US debut comes with the show already boasting momentum. A second season has been renewed and has reportedly wrapped filming.

While the premiere date for Season 2 on Hayu or Bravo has not been confirmed, the cast has teased the finished product. They shared a wrap message, quoted by Just Jared, celebrating the completion of filming.

The arrival of *RHOL* on Bravo marks a significant expansion for the franchise, testing whether the specific brand of British high-society drama can captivate an American audience already saturated with housewives. With a second season in the bag, the network is clearly betting on these women making their mark.
