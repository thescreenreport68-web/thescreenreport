Chris Rock's latest feature film as a writer-director, *Misty Green*, has released its first trailer and is set for a world premiere at the 2026 [Toronto International Film Festival](/movies/tiff-unveils-special-presentations-lineup-for-2026-festival/), according to The Hollywood Reporter.

Rosalind Eleazar stars in the title role of Misty Green.

## Who stars in 'Misty Green'?

The film features a notable ensemble cast. Eleazar is joined by Adam Driver, Daniel Kaluuya, Anna Kendrick, Topher Grace, and Chris Rock himself. The trailer introduces their characters through a series of tense and candid interactions.

## What does the trailer reveal?

The preview opens with Misty being evicted from her apartment. “I’m broke,” she confesses early on, noting her brother, played by Kaluuya, depends on her. She meets with an executive, played by Topher Grace, who underscores the industry's harsh reality. “It’s tough out there. I don’t know if I can even get you on *Law & Order* now,” he tells her.

Rock appears in a scene where his character recalls a past incident with Misty. “The last time I cast you, you punched this bitch in the face,” his character says, to which Misty retorts, “She hit me first.” Driver’s character is seen making her a dubious offer, suggesting she could make “a little extra cash.” The trailer also shows Misty auditioning and her brother facing difficult times.

## When and where will it be released?

*Misty Green* is scheduled for its world premiere at the Toronto International Film Festival in 2026. Following the festival run, the film is slated for an October theatrical release.

Rock previously directed features including *Head of State*, *I Think I Love My Wife*, and the well-received *Top Five*.
