Tom Hanks and Tim Allen return as Woody and Buzz Lightyear in *[Toy Story 5](/movies/toy-story-5-box-office-day-31-adds-4-7-million-as-domestic-total-hits-430-9/)*, a vibrant new chapter in Pixar's beloved franchise. The adventure is directed by Andrew Stanton, who helmed *Finding Nemo* and *WALL-E*, and runs a brisk 102 minutes.

The story sees the toys' world upended when their owner, Bonnie, receives a Lilypad tablet as a gift and becomes obsessed. Buzz, Woody, Jessie, and the rest of the gang find their jobs as playtime companions become exponentially harder as they go head-to-head with this all-new, digital threat. The film blends animation, family, comedy, and adventure into a tale of friendship facing modern challenges.

The iconic voice cast is back in full force. Tom Hanks leads as the loyal cowboy Woody, with Tim Allen as the space ranger Buzz Lightyear. Joan Cusack returns as the spirited cowgirl Jessie. They are joined by new faces: Greta Lee voices the tablet itself, Lilypad; Conan O'Brien plays the know-it-all action figure Smarty Pants; Craig Robinson is Atlas; Shelby Rabara is Snappy; and Tony Hale reprises his role as the anxious spork, Forky. The film is a testament to the enduring appeal of these characters.

## The Box Office

Playing in 3,400 theaters, its success reaffirms the power of established franchises for Disney and Pixar.

This performance solidifies the toys' place as cinematic titans, proving that audiences are still eager to join them on new adventures decades after their first outing.

## At the Box Office

Toy Story 5 has grossed $431,592,920 at the domestic box office, 32 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,400 theaters. The film added $1,714,276 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $958 million. It carries a reported production budget of $250 million.
