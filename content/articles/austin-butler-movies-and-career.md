[Austin Butler](/celebrity/austin-butler-movies-and-tv-shows/) transformed from a familiar face on teen television into an Oscar-nominated film star through a series of daring, transformative roles. His journey is a masterclass in career reinvention, anchored by a complete immersion into the characters he plays, most notably the King of Rock and Roll.

His early work on shows like 'Zoey 101' and 'The Carrie Diaries' built a foundation, but his path to stardom was cemented by a pivotal supporting role in a major director's film and a lead performance that demanded everything he had.

## What are Austin Butler's most famous movies?

Butler's filmography showcases a sharp pivot from youthful roles to complex, often intense characters. His first major film credit was the 2009 family adventure **'Aliens in the Attic'**. The real turning point came a decade later with Quentin Tarantino's **'Once Upon a Time in Hollywood'**, where he played the chilling Manson Family member Tex Watson.

His star-making role arrived in 2022 with Baz Luhrmann's **'Elvis'**. Butler's embodiment of the music icon was total, earning him critical acclaim and major award recognition. He followed this with a starkly different performance as the ruthless, bald villain Feyd-Rautha in Denis Villeneuve's **'Dune: Part Two'** in 2024, proving his versatility. He also starred in the 2024 motorcycle club drama **'The Bikeriders'**.

## How did Austin Butler get his start?

Butler's career began in the mid-2000s on the very channels that launch many young actors. After working as a background actor, he landed his first regular role on Nickelodeon's 'Ned's Declassified School Survival Guide'. Guest spots on **'Hannah Montana'** and **'iCarly'** quickly followed.

He became more widely recognized for his recurring role as James Garrett on the fourth season of 'Zoey 101'. This led to a lead part in the ABC Family series 'Ruby & the Rockits' and later, his starring turn as Sebastian Kydd on The CW's **'The Carrie Diaries'**, a prequel to 'Sex and the City'. He further built his genre credentials by playing Wil Ohmsford in the fantasy series **'The Shannara Chronicles'** from 2016 to 2017.

## What awards has Austin Butler won?

Butler's performance in 'Elvis' catapulted him into the awards season spotlight. For that role, he won the **Golden Globe Award for Best Actor** and the **BAFTA Award for Best Actor**. He was also nominated for the **Academy Award for Best Actor**, a Critics' Choice Award, and a Screen Actors Guild Award. His work has been recognized by institutions beyond film; Time magazine named him one of the **100 most influential people in the world** in 2023.

## What is Austin Butler's best role?

While his menacing Feyd-Rautha in 'Dune: Part Two' demonstrated incredible range, his role as **Elvis Presley** remains his defining performance to date. The physical and vocal transformation was immense, requiring two years of deep immersion. Critics and audiences alike were captivated by his portrayal, which balanced the singer's explosive stage presence with his personal vulnerabilities. The role didn't just earn awards; it announced Butler as a serious, committed actor capable of carrying a major biographical epic. It redefined his career entirely.

His stage work also deserves note. He made his Broadway debut in 2018 in 'The Iceman Cometh' alongside Denzel Washington, earning praise for his performance as the troubled Don Parritt.
