The best movies on Netflix right now are a powerful mix of timeless classics, modern masterpieces, and acclaimed crowd-pleasers. We’ve ranked the top 12 highest-rated films currently streaming, based on audience and critical scores, to cut through the endless scroll.

## What is the #1 best movie on Netflix?

**Schindler's List** sits at the top of the heap. Steven Spielberg’s 1993 Holocaust drama remains a monumental achievement in cinema, a film that balances profound horror with a story of individual courage. Its stark black-and-white cinematography and Liam Neeson’s career-defining performance create an experience that is as essential as it is difficult to watch. It’s the kind of film that defines what a platform’s library can be.

## Which Netflix movies have won Best Picture?

While not all Netflix Originals, several Best Picture winners are currently available. **Green Book**, the 2019 Oscar winner for Best Picture, offers a feel-good, if simplified, take on a real-life friendship across racial lines during the 1960s Deep South. The epic **Lawrence of Arabia**, the 1963 winner, is also streaming in all its sweeping desert glory. These films showcase the range of acclaimed storytelling you can find, from intimate character studies to grand historical spectacles.

## What are the best animated movies on Netflix?

Netflix hosts some of the finest animated films of the last decade. **Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse** revolutionized the visual language of superhero movies with its comic-book-come-to-life style and heartfelt story. **Klaus**, a Netflix Original, reinvented the Santa Claus origin story with stunning hand-drawn texture and genuine warmth. Then there’s **Guillermo del Toro's Pinocchio**, a stop-motion marvel that reimagines the classic tale with dark, beautiful, and emotionally resonant depth. Each one proves animation isn't just for kids.

## Is there a good war movie on Netflix?

For powerful wartime narratives, **Hacksaw Ridge** delivers. Directed by Mel Gibson, it tells the incredible true story of Desmond Doss, a pacifist medic who saved dozens of soldiers during the Battle of Okinawa without firing a shot. Its harrowing battle sequences are contrasted with a message of unwavering conviction. For a different, devastating perspective on war, **Grave of the Fireflies** is a landmark Japanese anime. It follows two children struggling to survive in the final days of World War II, and its emotional impact is unforgettable.

## What other highly-rated gems are available?

The list includes global cinema and recent hits. The beloved Bollywood romance **Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge** is a cultural touchstone that has charmed audiences for decades. The Oscar-nominated **Lion**, based on a true story, is a moving search for home across continents. For a more recent original, the musical romance **Purple Hearts** struck a major chord with audiences. The platform also offers the new anime feature **KPop Demon Hunters**, blending music and fantasy action.
