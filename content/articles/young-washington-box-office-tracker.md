Three weeks after its release, the historical war drama is holding strong in the top ten.

Directed by Jon Erwin, '[Young Washington](/movies/young-washington-box-office-day-18-adds-433-123-as-domestic-total-hits-41-4/)' is a 125-minute historical war drama that explores the formative years of America's first president. The premise follows George Washington before he led a nation, as a young soldier thrown into a global conflict. With alliances crumbling and war closing in, he must choose who to trust and confront the leader he's becoming.

The film features a robust ensemble cast. William Franklyn-Miller stars as the titular George Washington. He is supported by Ben Kingsley as Robert Dinwiddie, Mary-Louise Parker as Mary Washington, and Leo Hanna as Christopher Gist. Mia Rodgers plays Sally Cary, while Joel Smallbone appears as William Fairfax. Rounding out the key cast are Kelsey Grammer as Lord Fairfax and Andy Serkis as General Edward Braddock.

## At the Box Office

Young Washington has grossed $41,977,728 at the domestic box office, 19 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 2,275 theaters. The film added $627,285 in its most recent day of release. It carries a reported production budget of $20 million.
