What’s the perfect accessory for a Taylor Swift wedding? For Glennon Doyle, it was a clutch with a message straight from the bride’s songbook.

The couple’s stylish arrival sparked immediate buzz, but it was Doyle’s beaded clutch that set tongues wagging.

Doyle and Wambach later shared a joint Instagram post with a sweet message for the newlyweds. In the caption, they wrote about their hope that the couple feels surrounded by love, adding, “You’re not on your own, kids. You never will be.”

They signed off as “your retired English teacher and PE teacher,” a playful callback that fans noted seemed to reference Swift and Kelce’s own engagement announcement caption from last year.

The couple’s wedding looks were a study in chic contrast. Doyle, 50, wore a powder blue Jenny Packham gown embellished with silver sequins and crystals. Wambach, 46, opted for a sharp three-piece tuxedo with tails, a white waistcoat, and a pocket chain.

Their presence at the wedding underscores a longer connection to Swift’s world. As reported by the Democrat and Chronicle, Wambach has been a Swiftie for years. More poignantly, Doyle has previously credited Swift’s music with inspiring her stepdaughter to discover her own musical voice years ago.

Married since 2017, Doyle and Wambach represent a blend of literary and athletic star power that fit right in with the eclectic, A-list guest list. Their thoughtful tribute—both in fashion and words—highlights the personal touch that defined the monumental event.
