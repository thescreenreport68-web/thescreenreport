What's more expensive than a front-row seat? A million-dollar bet on the wrong fighter.

Drake is out a cool million after his very public wager on Conor McGregor backfired spectacularly. The 39-year-old rapper placed a $1 million bet that McGregor would defeat Max Holloway at UFC 329 on Saturday, July 11, according to a report from Just Jared.

Just before the fight, Drake took to social media to hype his pick, captioning a post: "THE MAC IS BACK?!?!?? The proper twelve drinking, tailored suit wearing, Lambo Yacht riding man with the Dubai tan that once had both belts in both hands…Say less."

Say less, indeed. McGregor's first UFC fight in five years ended in disaster. The fighter suffered a leg injury at the beginning of Round 1, forcing the referee to waive the fight after just 69 seconds. The official result was a victory for Max Holloway.

McGregor reacted to the shocking loss on social media. "I was so sharp and so ready for this fight I cannot believe what has happened. The talk of me being off while walking in to the fight is nonsense. I was calm, ready, and confident. I am in shock what has taken place," he wrote. He added, "I will overcome this. I will not be deterred. I will return."

For Drake, this high-profile loss adds to a recent streak of being in the public eye for reasons beyond his music. His penchant for sports betting is well-documented, but this million-dollar misfire is one of his most costly public predictions yet.

The exact terms of the bet and whether the money has changed hands remain unconfirmed. While Drake publicized the wager, the financial logistics behind such a large sum are a private matter. For now, the story is a stark reminder that even for the wealthy, a bad bet can have a very expensive price tag.
