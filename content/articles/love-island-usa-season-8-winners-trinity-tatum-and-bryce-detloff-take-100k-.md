Trinity Tatum and Bryce Detloff have been crowned the winners of [Love Island USA](/tv/love-island-usa-season-8-cast-is-revealed-as-new-episodes-air/) Season 8, taking home the show's $100,000 prize. The finale aired Sunday on Peacock, capping a season that saw the couple's relationship evolve from friendship to romance.

Peacock confirmed the victory and announced additional programming. A reunion special for the Season 8 cast will air on August 31, hosted by Emmy-nominated series host Ariana Madix and Andy Cohen. The platform also renewed the spinoff series **"Love Island: Beyond the Villa" for a third season**, set to premiere in 2027.

## Who were the runners-up?

Aniya Harvey and Carl Schmidt finished as the runners-up. Melanie Moreno and Sincere Rhea placed third, while Kayda Bosse and Zach Georgiou came in fourth. The final four couples were determined after an elimination episode on Friday.

## What happened during the season?

The season was not without controversy. Two contestants, Vasana Montgomery and Alannah Keyser, were removed from the villa after past social media posts containing racist language surfaced. Both have since apologized.

Despite the off-screen issues, the show's popularity continues to grow. According to Nielsen data reported by TheWrap, Season 8 drew over 2 billion viewing minutes in its first two weeks on Peacock, a 50% increase from the previous record-breaking season. The series also topped the Nielsen streaming charts for two consecutive weeks in June.

Host Ariana Madix, who received an Emmy nomination for her work on the series this week, previously highlighted the casting team's skill in creating compelling dynamics. "These Islanders are never together. They don’t meet. There’s no interaction where they can chemistry test the way you would on a scripted show," Madix told The Hollywood Reporter. "And yet year after year, the casting team does such an incredible job."

All episodes of Love Island USA Season 8 are now available to stream on Peacock.
