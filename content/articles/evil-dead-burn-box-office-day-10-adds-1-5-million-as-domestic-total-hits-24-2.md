A New Chapter of Demonic Terror

Souheila Yacoub leads the latest descent into madness with *[Evil Dead Burn](/movies/evil-dead-burn-box-office-day-8-adds-1-5-million-as-domestic-total-hits-20-7/)*, a fresh installment in the enduring horror franchise. The film follows Alice, who, after the sudden death of her husband, seeks refuge with his remaining family. She soon discovers her in-laws are the descendants of a leading researcher on demonic possession. As family members begin to horrifically transform into creatures that feed on fear, Alice must confront a terrifying truth: the vows she took in life may be the only thing that can survive the onslaught of death. Director Sébastien Vaniček steers this 110-minute horror-thriller.

## An Ensemble Haunted by Legacy

The film assembles a formidable cast to face the ancient evil. Souheila Yacoub stars as the grieving yet resilient protagonist, Alice. She is joined by Tandi Wright as Susan, Hunter Doohan as Joseph, and Luciane Buchanan as Thya. Erroll Shand portrays Edgar, with Maude Davey as Polly, George Pullar as Will, and Victory Ndukwe rounding out the key players as Leo. Together, they embody a family lineage cursed by a dark and possessive force.

## A Franchise That Refuses to Die

The *Evil Dead* series has consistently won over critics, and *Evil Dead Burn* continues that tradition. This sustained critical approval underscores the enduring appeal and creative vitality of the property. The story of *Evil Dead Burn* proves that some terrors, and some franchises, are built to last.

## At the Box Office

Evil Dead Burn has grossed $24,151,000 at the domestic box office, 10 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,004 theaters. The film added $1,500,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $35 million. It carries a reported production budget of $20 million.
