The Movie

William Franklyn-Miller stars as the future first president in *[Young Washington](/movies/young-washington-box-office-day-13-domestic-total-climbs-to-36-5-million/)*, a historical war drama that explores the man before the legend. The film, directed by Jon Erwin, follows a young George Washington as he is thrust into a global conflict. With alliances crumbling and war closing in, the 125-minute drama focuses on Washington's pivotal early choices, testing his character and defining the leader he would become.

The ensemble cast brings this formative period to life. Franklyn-Miller leads as George Washington, with Ben Kingsley portraying Virginia's Lieutenant Governor, Robert Dinwiddie. Mary-Louise Parker plays his mother, Mary Washington, while Leo Hanna appears as his guide, Christopher Gist. The supporting roles include Mia Rodgers as Sally Cary, Joel Smallbone as William Fairfax, Kelsey Grammer as Lord Fairfax, and Andy Serkis as General Edward Braddock.

## The Box Office Story

*Young Washington* has delivered a powerful opening performance for Angel Studios. Crucially, this debut represents the best opening ever for a live-action feature from the studio.

This strong launch, particularly for a historical drama targeting adult audiences, demonstrates a clear market for the studio's brand of mid-budget, values-based storytelling.

## At the Box Office

Young Washington has grossed $36,541,620 at the domestic box office, 13 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 2,771 theaters. The film added $885,005 in its most recent day of release. It carries a reported production budget of $20 million.
