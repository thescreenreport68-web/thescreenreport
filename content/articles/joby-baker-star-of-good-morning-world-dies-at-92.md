Joby Baker, the actor best known for starring in the CBS sitcom *Good Morning World* and playing drums alongside Elvis Presley in *Girl Happy*, died on June 22 at the age of 92, his granddaughter Sofia Silverman confirmed to The Hollywood Reporter.

Baker died at a hospital in Yonkers, New York, according to the report. His career began in the 1950s, and he was managed early on by famed comedian Lou Costello.

## What was Joby Baker known for?
Baker is most recognized for his role as radio D.J. Dave Lewis on *Good Morning World*, which premiered in September 1967. The show was created to replace *The Dick Van Dyke Show* after its finale. "They were going to call it *The Joby Baker Show*," he said in a 2020 interview. "I went to these guys and I begged them please, please, don't call it [that]. And I convinced them not to. It's too much responsibility for a painter who might be in a terrible TV show." The series was canceled after one season of 26 episodes.

His film work included a memorable turn as drummer Wilbur in the 1965 musical *Girl Happy*, where his character performed in a band with Elvis Presley's Rusty Wells. Baker also appeared in three *Gidget* films—*Gidget* (1959), *Gidget Goes Hawaiian* (1961), and *Gidget Goes to Rome* (1963).

## What was his early life like?
Born Joseph Baker in Montreal on March 26, 1934, he moved to Oahu, Hawaii, as a toddler after his mother's death. He was living there during the attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941. Baker recalled that the house he was in was hit by friendly fire in the immediate aftermath, but he escaped injury. He and his stepmother were evacuated to San Francisco aboard the RMS *Aquitania*.

After serving in the U.S. Army, where he entertained troops, Baker launched a nightclub act in Los Angeles as a Jerry Lewis impersonator. His television debut came on *The Red Skelton Hour* in 1952, and he made his film debut in *Target Zero* (1955).

Baker was married to actress Joan Blackman from 1959 to 1961 and to lyricist Dory Previn from 1984 until her death in 2012. He illustrated *The Dory Previn Songbook* and other of her works. Survivors include his wife, Megan; his children Ricka, Michelle, and Scott; stepdaughters Emma and Eliza; and grandchildren including Sofia.

Reflecting on his career, Baker told The Hollywood Reporter, **"I was not interested in stardom, in fact I was embarrassed by it."** He considered himself foremost a painter.
