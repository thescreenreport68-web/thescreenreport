Larry David takes a comedic shot at the anti-vaccine activism of Robert F. Kennedy Jr. in a new episode of his HBO Max series, according to a report from Deadline. The scene arrives in the mock-history comedy *Life, Larry and the Pursuit of Unhappiness*, which premiered in June 2026.

In the episode, David plays Dora Salk, mother of polio vaccine pioneer Jonas Salk. While hanging laundry, her character is confronted by a skeptical, overly-tanned neighbor who declares vaccines a dangerous plot. "That vaccine’s gonna kill people," the neighbor says. "It’s gonna give them heart attacks!"

David's character delivers a sharp retort. "Drop dead, Bobby," Dora says. "You should die a dog’s death! You don’t know anything about science, you’re not a doctor. God help us all if some idiot, some moron, ever put you in charge."

The scene is widely interpreted as a direct reference to Kennedy, the high-profile anti-vaccine activist and husband of Cheryl Hines. Hines played David's wife on *Curb Your Enthusiasm* for years, connecting the two men. The report notes that David's friendship with Hines appears to have cooled recently, and she has not appeared on his new HBO Max show.

*Life, Larry and the Pursuit of Unhappiness* is an improvised comedic look at American history, created by Larry David and Jeff Schaffer. The series, rated TV-MA, streams exclusively on HBO Max as of July 2026.
