Ryan Coogler's pilot for a reboot of *The X-Files* has wrapped filming, but a new report suggests the project could pivot from a series to a movie.

The technical choice, reported via World of Reel, is part of Coogler's effort to give the pilot a cinematic look. The report indicates that Disney and Hulu, which are producing the reboot, have not yet decided whether to greenlight a full series or potentially release the pilot in theaters. A hybrid approach, releasing the project theatrically before its streaming debut, is also reportedly being considered.

## Who is starring in the reboot?

Himesh Patel and Danielle Deadwyler are confirmed to star as the new lead FBI agents, playing completely new characters not based on the original series' Fox Mulder and Dana Scully. Needless to say, myself and Danielle Deadwyler are playing completely new characters, and we just wrapped on the pilot."

The pilot also features guest stars including Amy Madigan, Steve Buscemi, Ben Foster, Devery Jacobs, Lochlyn Munro, Tantoo Cardinal, and Joel Montgrand. Hulu initially ordered the pilot in February 2026, with filming beginning in May and concluding in July. An official release date for the project has not been announced.
