### The Defenders: A Nostalgic, Dumb Throwback

For a significant portion of the audience, the new film delivered exactly what they wanted: a relentless, no-pretense comedy that felt like a direct callback to the early 2000s. These viewers went in with their expectations firmly in check, ready to embrace the intentional stupidity.

One user wrote, "I feel like I’m in the minority of people who actually liked this movie. I grew up on the [Scary Movie](/movies/scary-movie-2026-the-internet-is-split-between-nostalgic-cheers-and/) franchise so I knew what I was getting."

The sentiment is that if you understood the assignment, you got your money's worth. Another viewer posted, "It really wasn't THAT bad, if you haven't seen it. It was pretty funny in my opinion; different and definitely trying to update the humor a bit, but it was still funny."

For these fans, the return of the original energy and the Wayans' signature style was the main event. One positive review captured the spirit: "Wayans getting their franchise back had us hyped with high expectations. This is a fun movie full of jokes and many references from many movies. However I was hopeful for a smoother plot and more screen time for certain actors, but I didn’t let that stop me from getting a good laugh and thrill from it."

The ultimate endorsement came from viewers who experienced it as a communal, laugh-out-loud event. "Being in college and watching this with a friend, laughing like hell, was exactly how I wanted to experience this movie!" one fan said. "The Wayans brothers are truly the only ones who can make a Scary Movie!"

### The Critics: A Missed Opportunity and Cluttered Jokes

On the other side of the aisle, viewers found the revival to be a letdown after a long wait, criticizing its structure and joke delivery. The overwhelming feeling was one of missed potential.

"I wish it was more along the lines of the previous Scary Movies with some kind of a base plot," one disappointed viewer wrote. "This was just randomly thrown together. For waiting so many years for a follow up, this was a let down."

The most pointed criticism focused on how the film handled its modern references. A fan online said, "Oof… Miss after miss after miss. The references weren’t meshed into the story at all. They became Family Guy cutaways. It was dreadful. I laughed at some points but the cringe of the rest just overwhelmed the film."

This sense of disjointed, shallow parody left some cold. "Is it me or is this just not funny?" another asked. "loved the original movies, but this aint it.."

### The Nuanced Middle Ground

Between the love and hate, a sizable group of viewers landed in a reflective, mixed space. They acknowledged the film's glaring flaws but couldn't entirely dismiss its nostalgic charm or scattered laughs.

But 2026’s revival of the franchise serves up something that’s harder to pin down than a straight laugh or a straight groan: its a time capsule dressed up as a comedy. The film is fine. Occasionally funny, occasionally inspired, and occasionally so aggressively stupid that youll catch yourself rolling your eyes mid-chew on your popcorn."

The post concludes with the prevailing advice for would-be viewers: "Bottom line: Go in with low expectations and a forgiving mood. You’ll laugh. You’ll groan. You’ll shrug. And somewhere in there, you might feel a little nostalgic, too."

### The Mystery of the Missing Scenes

Adding another layer to the audience discourse is widespread confusion and curiosity about scenes shown in trailers that weren't in the theatrical release. This wasn't a minor gripe; it became a focal point for discussion.

"i wish they showed this scene in the cinema i was really looking forward for this scene," one user posted, a sentiment repeated by others. Another asked plainly, "Is there's a reason why they deleted this scene?"

This led to speculation about extended cuts. "The extended cut is WAAAAYYYY better than the original cut," one claimed, while another wondered, "Is the extended cut, or the deleted scenes going to be on the physical release?"

Even some who enjoyed the film noted the omission. "Just watched it last night, really liked it, but this scene wasn't in the movie. Guess I'll have to watch it again," a fan wrote with a laugh.

### Why the Reaction Is So Divided

For defenders, the film's value is inextricably linked to the nostalgic, lowbrow fun of the originals. They see the messy structure and joke-a-minute pace as features, not bugs—the chaotic signature of the franchise.

For critics, that same chaos reads as a lack of craft. In an era of more polished parodies and satires, the film's reliance on disconnected pop-culture drops feels dated and lazy, not charmingly retro. The long gap between films also raised hopes for a more evolved spoof, making the throwback approach feel like a step backward.
