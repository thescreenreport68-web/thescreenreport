Aziz Ansari's upcoming comedy fantasy film *Good Fortune* will feature two classic Australian songs on its soundtrack, according to a report from Noise11.com. The film will use The Go-Betweens’ “Streets of Your Town” and Real Life’s “Send Me An Angel” to help shape its nostalgic atmosphere.

These Australian tracks join a wider soundtrack that includes music from artists like Beck, Salt-N-Pepa, Buzzcocks, Deee-Lite, and Frankie Knuckles. While neither Australian recording will be on the official soundtrack album, both songs will be heard within the film itself.

## What songs are featured?

The Go-Betweens’ “Streets of Your Town,” released in 1988, became one of the band's defining recordings. It was written by Grant McLennan for the album *16 Lovers Lane* and later appeared in the Australian film *The Black Balloon*.

Real Life’s “Send Me An Angel,” originally released in 1983, has a longer history with Hollywood soundtracks. The synth-pop track previously appeared in films like *Rad*, *Teen Wolf Too*, and *The Wizard*.

## What is Good Fortune about?

Ansari wrote, directed, and stars in the film, which also features Keanu Reeves, Seth Rogen, Keke Palmer, and Sandra Oh. Reeves plays Gabriel, a low-ranking guardian angel whose plan to prove money can't buy happiness goes wrong after he swaps the lives of a struggling gig worker and a wealthy tech investor. The film uses music from multiple decades to reinforce its changing moods, with the Australian songs selected to complement the nostalgic tone.

*Good Fortune* marks Ansari's feature directorial debut after his earlier project *Being Mortal* was suspended in 2022. The film premiered at the Toronto International Film Festival before its theatrical release.
