FX has set Monday, August 17 for the Season 18 premiere of *It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia*, according to Deadline. The new season will be an expanded 10-episode run, up from the eight-episode orders of the previous three seasons.

It will premiere at 9 p.m. ET/6 p.m. PT on FXX, FX, and Hulu, and will stream on Disney+ internationally. The network also released the key art for the eighteenth season.

## What will Season 18 be about?

The new season finds Charlie, Mac, Dennis, Dee, and Frank wrapped up in salvaging inheritances, surviving tent encampments, and withstanding job loss from workplace automation. Their chaotic misadventures will also see them embracing neurodivergence, questioning conspiracy theories, and battling the McPoyle clan at a Renaissance Faire.

All the while, the gang tries to maintain order in their increasingly lawless personal lives and at their failing dive bar, Paddy's Pub.

## Who is behind the new season?

Season 18 is executive produced by series creators and stars Rob Mac, Charlie Day, and Glenn Howerton, alongside Kaitlin Olson and Danny DeVito. The producing team also includes Michael Rotenberg, Nicholas Frenkel, David Hornsby, Rob Rosell, Scott Marder, Dave Chernin, John Chernin, and Vanessa McGee for FX Productions.

The series, which first aired in 2005, holds the record as the longest-running live-action sitcom in American television history.
