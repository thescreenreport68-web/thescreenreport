Corey Kent has a second song gaining momentum on country radio. According to Billboard, his track "Empty Words" is now his second concurrent release.

## How Did This Happen?

The song wasn't being formally worked to radio by his label, RCA. Its journey began when a radio programmer challenged stations to take a risk and play it. Kent remembers, "We had Denver, Nashville, Dallas — like, big, big markets — adding it before we asked them to add it. We didn't anticipate it being a radio single." Concert-goers also responded with intensity. "It just became an accidental sing-along," Kent says. "I mean, people are screaming this song."

## Who Wrote 'Empty Words'?

The song was born from a four-day writing retreat at a family property in January 2025. Kent co-wrote it with Joybeth Taylor, Matt Roy, Lydia Vaughan, and writer-producer Austin Goodloe. Goodloe had created an instrumental track before the retreat. "I basically had a session by myself for a song that didn't exist," he says, "but I knew that the vibe felt great." The group built the song around a hook pairing "empty bed" with "empty words." Roy highlighted a favorite lyric from the session: "It took us a second to get that 'howl' line... 'Been crying wolf so long/ She don’t want to hear me howl.'"

This organic success gives Kent a notable presence on the charts. His collaboration with Koe Wetzel, "Rocky Mountain Low," is currently No. 4 on Billboard's Country Airplay chart.
