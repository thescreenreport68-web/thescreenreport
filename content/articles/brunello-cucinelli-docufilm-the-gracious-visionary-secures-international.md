The docufilm *Brunello: The Gracious Visionary* has secured international sales deals across three continents ahead of its North American debut, according to Variety. The film from Oscar-winning director Giuseppe Tornatore profiles Italian fashion designer Brunello Cucinelli.

Sales company Twenty-Nine Palms Entertainment has closed deals for the film’s release across Europe, Asia, and the Middle East.

## What is the film about?

The documentary-drama chronicles Cucinelli's journey from a farmer's son to the creative director and executive chairman of a global luxury lifestyle brand with a market capitalization of more than $7.8 billion. It interweaves documentary and reenacted components to depict how his philosophy of "humanistic capitalism" was shaped. The film stars Saul Nanni as a younger version of Cucinelli and features a score by Oscar-winning composer Nicola Piovani.

## Where has it been sold?

Twenty-Nine Palms has sold the title to distributors in over a dozen territories. Key sales include Autumn Sun Films for Hong Kong, Destiny Films for France, Long Ride for Japan, MindJazz Pictures for Germany and Austria, Palace Entertainment for Australia and New Zealand, and PVR Inox Pictures for India. The film also secured global airline distribution via Terry Steiner International.

The film is produced by Brunello Cucinelli S.p.A. and MasiFilm in collaboration with RAI Cinema.
