Another country music couple is calling it quits. Jon Pardi and his wife, Summer Pardi, have announced they are ending their marriage.

The news came via a joint statement the pair posted to Instagram on Friday, July 3, as reported by Just Jared. In the statement, the couple said, "After much thought, we have made the difficult decision to end our marriage. Our daughters will always remain our highest priority and we are committed to moving forward with love and respect as co-parents."

They concluded, "Thank you so much for your understanding, support, and respect as we navigate these changes as a family."

The 41-year-old "Head Over Boots" singer also shared a separate note on his Instagram Story, announcing a social media hiatus. "While I navigate this next chapter, I’m going to take some time away from social media to focus on myself and my family. Thanks to you all for the love and support. My team will handle posting for the time being. I’ll see you at the shows," Jon wrote.

Jon and Summer married in November 2020. They share two young daughters: 3-year-old Presley Fawn and 2-year-old Sienna Grace.

The announcement comes as the Nashville-born songwriter is in the midst of a major tour promoting his fifth studio album. He has shows scheduled through December 2026, with stops planned in Ohio, Canada, North Dakota, Minnesota, North Carolina, Virginia, and more.

This split follows a recent trend in the country music world, with another high-profile singer announcing a divorce just last month. While the Pardis have confirmed their separation, the specific reasons behind their decision remain private. The couple's statement focuses solely on their commitment to co-parenting and their request for privacy as they move forward.
