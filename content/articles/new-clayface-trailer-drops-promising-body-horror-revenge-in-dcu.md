DC Studios has released a new trailer for its upcoming horror-thriller, *Clayface*, providing a deeper look at the monstrous origin story set to hit theaters in October 2026. The preview, reported by outlets including Cinelinx and IGN, leans heavily into body-horror as it charts the tragic fall of its protagonist.

James Watkins directs from a screenplay by Mike Flanagan and Hossein Amini, based on a story by Flanagan and characters from DC. The film is produced by Matt Reeves, Lynn Harris, James Gunn, and Peter Safran.

## What is Clayface about?

The official synopsis details a classic tragedy turned monstrous. Matt Hagen’s rise from Gotham street kid to Hollywood leading man is cut short when a crime boss leaves him horribly disfigured. Abandoned and desperate, he seeks a cutting-edge treatment that restores his appearance but shatters his grip on reality, sending him on a path of all-consuming revenge.

## Who stars in the film?

Tom Rhys Harries leads the cast as Matt Hagen, the actor who becomes the monstrous Clayface. He is joined by Naomi Ackie as Caitlyn Corr, with a supporting cast that includes Eddie Marsan, Max Minghella as John Borrelli, and David Dencik.

## How does it fit into the DC Universe?

*Clayface* is deliberately positioned as a dark counterpoint to brighter entries like *Superman* and *Supergirl*. It presents a standalone, nightmarish story within James Gunn's DC Universe. As reported by IGN, the film comes out before the recently delayed *The Batman 2*, which is not part of the DCU, and well before Gunn's own *The Brave and the Bold*. Don't expect Batman to appear.
