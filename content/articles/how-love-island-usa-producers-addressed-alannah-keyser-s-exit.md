What happens in the villa stays in the villa—but what about when a castmate is suddenly removed? After Alannah Keyser was booted from *Love Island USA*, her fellow islanders were given a reason, but not the full story.

Castmate Parmida Keshani revealed to *People* in an interview published July 11 that producers told the group Keyser left for personal reasons. She added, "I just was praying that she was okay. We were friends, so I was just like, well, ‘I hope it’s nothing family-related. I hope she’s okay.’"

Her exit was announced on-air just days later. During the June 25 episode, narrator Iain Stirling noted after a commercial break, "Alannah has left Casa Amor."

Her departure, however, was prompted by controversy outside the villa. Keyser was removed after videos and screenshots of her using a racial slur circulated online.

Following her exit, Keyser addressed the controversy in a June 27 TikTok video. "When I first found out that these things were going around online, it really broke my heart, and I couldn’t do anything about it," she said. "But this has definitely been a learning lesson for me, and it sucks that I didn’t get a chance to really show my personality and who I am." She added, "I do want to say directly that I do not support racism or discrimination of any kind, and I never have."

Back in the villa, Keshani said the cast had to carry on. "We kind of just had to move on," she noted. The 27-year-old, who was eliminated with partner Corbin Mims on the July 5 episode, also shared she thought it was "pretty crazy" that islander Kayda Bosse didn't discover her partner Zach Georgiou's brief connection with Keyser until the show's Movie Night event.

The incident highlights the tightrope reality shows walk when off-screen behavior impacts the on-screen narrative. While producers controlled the message inside the villa, the real story played out on social media, leaving fans to piece together the truth behind the 'personal reasons.'
