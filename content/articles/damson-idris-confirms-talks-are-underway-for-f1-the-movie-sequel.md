[Damson Idris](/movies/damson-idris-teases-exciting-conversations-for-f1-the-movie-sequel/) has confirmed that discussions are underway for a sequel to the 2025 film “F1 The Movie.” According to FormulaRapida.net, the actor revealed the news in a recent interview on the podcast F1 Beyond The Grid.

Idris, who played rookie driver Joshua Pearce in the original film, was cautious with details but acknowledged the talks. **“There are conversations,”** he admitted. “I can’t say too much, but we are talking about exciting things. The main goal is to make the sport and fans feel proud. I believe the first movie achieved this, with people watching it up to 19 times.”

## What did Damson Idris say about the first film?

In the interview, Idris highlighted the family-friendly nature of the movie as a key to its success. He noted its appeal across generations, which he said is uncommon in modern films. The actor also reflected on how the role transformed his career, propelling him to new heights after his work on the series *Snowfall*.

## How successful was the first movie?

The original “F1 The Movie” was a major commercial hit. According to verified data, the film grossed $634 million worldwide. It earned an 82% critics score on Rotten Tomatoes and won one Oscar among numerous awards. The film has been streaming on Apple TV in the U.S. since its digital release in August 2025.

While Idris confirmed the sequel discussions are active, no official greenlight, script details, or release window has been announced. The status of other key cast members, including Brad Pitt and Javier Bardem, remains unconfirmed.
