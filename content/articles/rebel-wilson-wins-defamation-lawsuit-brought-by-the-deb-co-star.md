Rebel Wilson has won a defamation lawsuit in Sydney, Australia, brought against her by actress Charlotte MacInnes, according to Deadline. The court ruled that Wilson's social media posts about her co-star were not defamatory.

Justice Elizabeth Raper delivered the verdict, stating, "MacInnes has failed to establish that the publications have caused or were likely to cause serious harm." The judge also noted that MacInnes had changed her story about feeling uncomfortable regarding the incident at the heart of the case.

## What was the lawsuit about?

The legal battle stemmed from four Instagram posts in which Wilson, star of *Pitch Perfect* and *Bridesmaids*, discussed an incident involving MacInnes and *The Deb* producer Amanda Ghost. Wilson alleged that Ghost shared a bath with MacInnes in a Sydney apartment in September 2023, an encounter that MacInnes claimed made her uncomfortable before later withdrawing her complaint. Both MacInnes and Ghost stated the bath was a non-sexual encounter where they wore swimsuits, following a medical incident experienced by Ghost.

Wilson claimed MacInnes later withdrew her complaint after being offered career opportunities by Ghost, allegations MacInnes denied and led to the defamation suit.

## What did Rebel Wilson say?

In a statement following the ruling, Wilson expressed relief. "I'm grateful the court has taken the time to carefully consider the evidence and reach its decision," she said. "This has been a long process and throughout I have tried to stay grounded in my values and what I believed was right. I'm thankful that process has now reached its conclusion."

She added that her focus is now on her family and her work, specifically her passion project *The Deb*, which marks her directorial debut.

## What happens next?

While this specific case is concluded, the ruling represents only one front in a wider legal conflict. Producers of *The Deb*, including Amanda Ghost, are reportedly suing Wilson in the United States, accusing her of pursuing an unjustified vendetta after being denied a writing credit on the film. MacInnes has been approached for comment on the Australian court's decision.
