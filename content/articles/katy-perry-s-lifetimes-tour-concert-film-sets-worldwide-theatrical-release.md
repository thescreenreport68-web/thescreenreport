The film, titled *The Lifetimes Tour – Live From Paris*, is getting a limited global cinema run following its festival premiere.

Trafalgar Releasing is handling the rollout, bringing the film to cinemas and IMAX screens across 70 countries for a limited time. The release includes the United States, United Kingdom, Ireland, Italy, Brazil, Mexico, Germany, France and Australia.

## What does the concert film feature?

The documentary was shot across two sold-out nights in Paris in November 2025. Director Paul Dugdale managed a 60-camera setup to capture the show. According to the film's official description, it aims to move beyond the traditional concert film format. “The film moves far beyond the traditional concert film, soaring above, around and through the show to place audiences at the heart of the performance,” the description reads. “Every breathtaking aerial moment, explosive visual and roar of the crowd is experienced from perspectives impossible to witness from the arena alone.”

## Who produced the film?

The movie was produced by Daniel E. Catullo III of 10 Lives Studios, along with Simon Fisher, Steve Moss and Rob Lane. A long list of executive and co-executive producers is also credited. In a statement, producer Catullo framed the release as an expansion of the live experience. “More than a million fans experienced this tour live, and now millions more will have the opportunity to see it like never before on the big screen,” he said. “The response to the film has been extraordinary, and we’re excited to share this unforgettable concert experience with audiences everywhere. It’s Katy’s love letter to her fans.”

## When did the film first premiere?

The concert film first premiered at the Tribeca Festival in June 2026, where Perry was present for a Q&A session. The theatrical release marks the first wide availability of the project since that festival debut. The Lifetimes Tour itself supported Perry's *143* album and concluded in December 2025.

Trafalgar Releasing executive Kymberli Frueh said the cinema release is designed to recreate the communal fan experience. “This cinema release gives new and returning KatyCats a chance to experience the moment, the way it was meant to be seen — with other fans, on the biggest screen possible,” Frueh said in a statement. More information on specific theater dates is available on the film’s official website.
