Director James Watkins explained why Tom Rhys Harries was cast as the lead in the upcoming DC film '[Clayface](/movies/new-clayface-trailer-drops-promising-body-horror-revenge-in-dcu/)' at Comic-Con. According to IMDb, the director revealed his reasoning for selecting Harries to play the tragic Hollywood actor Matt Hagen, who transforms into the monstrous Clayface.

Watkins also stated the film will feature "ick and a lot of gore," highlighting its R-rated horror approach. A new trailer for the project was released during the event.

## Who else is in the cast?
Alongside Tom Rhys Harries as Matt Hagen, the film stars Naomi Ackie as Caitlyn Corr and Eddie Marsan. The ensemble also includes Max Minghella as John Borrelli, David Dencik, Francesca Corney, Ruby Sear as Theresa, Wil Coban as Bones, Joshua James as Corey Bowery, and Jake Curran as Patches.

## When does 'Clayface' release?
The film, with a reported budget of $40 million, explores themes of identity, revenge, and the dark side of scientific ambition through its monster-centric story.
