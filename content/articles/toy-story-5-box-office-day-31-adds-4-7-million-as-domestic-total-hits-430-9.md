Woody, Buzz Lightyear, and the beloved Toy Story gang are back for their fifth big-screen adventure. Directed by Andrew Stanton, the 102-minute animated family comedy finds the toys facing their most modern challenge yet. The premise centers on Bonnie receiving a Lilypad tablet as a gift and becoming obsessed. This forces Buzz, Woody, Jessie, and the rest of the gang's jobs to become exponentially harder as they go head-to-head with the all-new digital threat to playtime.

The voice cast is a blend of familiar favorites and exciting new additions. Tom Hanks and Tim Allen return as the iconic duo Woody and Buzz Lightyear, with Joan Cusack back as the spirited cowgirl Jessie. They are joined by newcomers Greta Lee as the voice of the Lilypad tablet, Conan O'Brien as the know-it-all Smarty Pants, Craig Robinson as Atlas, Shelby Rabara as Snappy, and Tony Hale reprising his role as the anxious spork, Forky.

[Toy Story 5](/movies/toy-story-5-box-office-day-29-adds-4-2-million-as-domestic-total-hits-420-3/) is not just a hit; it's a phenomenon.

That powerful launch has translated into remarkable staying power. With momentum this strong, the path to joining the billion-dollar club is clearly in sight.

## At the Box Office

Toy Story 5 has grossed $430,918,566 at the domestic box office, 31 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,400 theaters. The film added $4,700,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $894 million. It carries a reported production budget of $250 million.
