Peacock's new limited series *The Five-Star Weekend* has become the streaming service's biggest launch for an original scripted show, according to an announcement reported by Collider.

The eight-episode drama, starring [Jennifer Garner](/tv/jennifer-garner-shares-how-13-going-on-30-reboot-honors-2004-film/), surpassed all previous Peacock original scripted series in first-week view count.

## What is 'The Five-Star Weekend' about?

Based on Elin Hilderbrand's 2023 bestselling novel, the series follows Garner's character Hollis Shaw, a successful food influencer whose life is upended after her husband's death. Struggling with grief, she hosts a "five-star weekend" at her Nantucket home, inviting one close friend from each major chapter of her life. The ensemble cast includes Chloë Sevigny, Regina Hall, D'Arcy Carden, and Gemma Chan.

The show focuses on themes of friendship, grief, and personal identity. It avoids the murder-mystery tropes common to other dramas set in wealthy coastal communities. Its tension comes from dialogue and the emotional secrets each character carries.

## How has the show been received?

The adaptation has earned a 90% score on Rotten Tomatoes from critics. They have praised the performances and the series' emotional storytelling. Peacock's new viewership numbers show the character-driven approach has resonated with audiences. It proves there is an appetite for drama built on compelling performances and honest conversation.

The complete eight-episode first season of *The Five-Star Weekend* is now streaming on Peacock.
