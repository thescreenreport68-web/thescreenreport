What happens when an author distances herself from the movie version of her own book? Tomi Adeyemi just showed us.

In a series of TikTok posts reported by Just Jared, the author of the best-selling novel "Children of Blood and Bone" made it clear she will not be supporting the upcoming film adaptation. Adeyemi, who co-wrote the screenplay with director Gina Prince-Bythewood, shared a direct message she sent, stating, "There is a reason I will not post anything about the adaptation of my work. That’s all."

She reportedly followed up with another message, writing, "And lastly since someone asked – I have not seen the film, and I will not watch it. It’s been painful holding this back from you all. … And I’m sorry if any of you thought I didn’t care about US. More than any glitter."

The posts, which included a screenshot of a message seemingly sent to the film's star, Amandla Stenberg, set tongues wagging. The screenshot reportedly reads, "Do not ever use my name in an interview or video again. Do not text me. Do not call me." The message appears to be a private communication, and its exact context or what prompted it remains unconfirmed.

This public rift is particularly notable because Adeyemi was directly involved in the project. The film, slated for release in 2027, adapts her wildly popular fantasy novel. Stenberg is set to portray Princess Amari. The author's decision to publicly state she won't watch the film and to share a private message suggests a significant breakdown behind the scenes, though the specific reasons are not yet known.

Fans of the book series are now left wondering about the state of the adaptation and the nature of the apparent conflict between the creator and one of its stars. For now, Adeyemi's stance is clear, and the promotional path for the 2027 movie just got a lot more complicated.
