Jon Bon Jovi abruptly ended his Thursday night performance at New York City’s Madison Square Garden due to a health issue. The Bon Jovi frontman, whose band is in the middle of a residency at the famed arena, performed for about 90 minutes before bringing the show to a premature close.

He addressed the crowd directly, confirming the reason for the early stop. His representative later stated that Jon Bon Jovi spoke from stage and told fans he has been battling a sinus infection which led to the show’s early ending.

## What did Jon Bon Jovi say to the crowd?

The singer was apologetic and direct with the audience. "I’m sorry, I’m hurt and you’re not getting the best of me," he said in videos posted online from the concert. He then made a promise to those holding tickets.

"Don’t throw away your ticket stubs, I’m gonna figure something out, OK? Just hold onto it, we’ll figure out how to reschedule," Bon Jovi told the crowd. He added that he was going to have to "cool it for the night" but assured fans he was feeling great and would see them again soon.

## Why is this concert residency significant?

The sold-out Madison Square Garden residency marks a major comeback for the singer. It is his return to full-scale performing after a four-year break following major vocal cord surgery in the summer of 2022. The residency features Bon Jovi performing alongside original bandmates and other longtime collaborators.

The Thursday concert was the eighth show of this residency. During the performance, Train frontman Pat Monahan joined Bon Jovi on stage to help with higher vocal parts. According to Deadline, the band is set to continue on Sunday at MSG before heading to the UK.

## What happens next for ticketholders?

Fans have been instructed to keep their ticket stubs. The singer's spokesperson said updated information regarding the rescheduled show would be available shortly. The band is next set to perform at Madison Square Garden on Sunday, as originally scheduled.
