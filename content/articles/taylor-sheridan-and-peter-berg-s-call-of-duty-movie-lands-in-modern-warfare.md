Paramount and Activision’s upcoming *Call of Duty* movie will be set in the *Modern Warfare* universe, according to The Hollywood Reporter.

Director and co-writer Peter Berg announced the news during Fanatics Fest in New York City, confirming the film will adapt the franchise’s most popular subseries. Taylor Sheridan, creator of *Landman* and the *[Yellowstone](/tv/dutton-ranch-finale-has-fans-in-a-chokehold-but-a-pre-awards-snub-stings-just-a/)* universe, is co-writing the screenplay with Berg.

## When does the Call of Duty movie release?

The film is slated to hit theaters on June 30, 2028.

## Who is making the film?

Berg and Sheridan are producing the project alongside David Glasser of 101 Studios. The project was first discussed publicly at CinemaCon in April, where Activision head Rob Kostich emphasized the importance of a faithful adaptation. "I told everyone we were only going to make a movie if it’s right," Kostich said. "In David Ellison, we found that partnership." He added that the goal is to capture the game's authenticity on a human level while infusing it with epic scope.

The *Call of Duty* franchise is one of the best-selling video game series of all time, with more than 500 million copies sold worldwide. The movie adaptation represents a major theatrical push for the property.
