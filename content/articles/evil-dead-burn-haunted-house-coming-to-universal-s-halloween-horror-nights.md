A haunted house based on the film **[Evil Dead Burn](/movies/evil-dead-burn-box-office-day-10-adds-1-5-million-as-domestic-total-hits-24-2/)** is headed to Universal's Halloween Horror Nights this year, according to Variety. The attraction will open at both Universal Orlando Resort and Universal Studios Hollywood, starting in late August and early September, respectively.

This marks the latest film from New Line Cinema to be transformed into a live horror experience for the annual event. The haunted house will immerse guests in the film's most frightening moments, beginning at the secluded vacation home of the Price family.

## What will the haunted house be like?

The experience is designed to replicate the escalating terror of the movie. Guests will venture through the attraction, encountering ferocious Deadites around every turn before ultimately facing the evil forces themselves. The film, which was released in July 2026, serves as the sixth installment in the long-running **Evil Dead** franchise and a standalone sequel following the 2013 and 2023 films.

## Who is involved with the film?

**Evil Dead Burn** is directed and co-written by [Sébastien Vaniček](/movies/evil-dead-burn-director-breaks-down-the-film-s-brutal-controversial-style/). The cast includes Souheila Yacoub, Hunter Doohan, Luciane Buchanan, Tandi Wright, and George Pullar. Original franchise creator Sam Raimi produces the film alongside Rob Tapert under their Ghost House Pictures banner, with Bruce Campbell and **Evil Dead Rise** director Lee Cronin serving as executive producers.

## When does Halloween Horror Nights start?

The event runs on select nights. Tickets for Halloween Horror Nights are now on sale through the official event website.
