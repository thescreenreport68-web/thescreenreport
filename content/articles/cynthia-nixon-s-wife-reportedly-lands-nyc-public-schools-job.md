Christine Marinoni, the wife of actress Cynthia Nixon, has reportedly been hired for a senior position within New York City's public school system. According to a report by The Hollywood Reporter, Marinoni will serve as the chief of mass engagement at the Family and Community Empowerment office of the Department of Education.

A Department of Education spokesperson confirmed the appointment to The New York Post, which first reported the news. In a statement to the Post, the spokesperson said, “Christine is a longtime public servant, community organizer and education advocate—precisely the experience and commitment our public schools need in leadership.”

## What is the reported salary for the role?

She previously worked in former Mayor Bill de Blasio's administration and helped found the Alliance for Quality Education in New York, where she first met Nixon.

## Is this a political hire?

The Post's report framed the hiring as a potential reward for Nixon's political support. The actress endorsed Mayor Zohran Mamdani during last year's Democratic primary and celebrated his 2025 election victory on Instagram. Nixon denied to the Post that her wife's appointment was a political hire. However, a City Hall source complained to the paper, calling it an example of handing “taxpayer-funded jobs to the well-connected spouses of multimillionaire celebrities.”

Nixon and Marinoni married in 2012 after meeting through education advocacy work. The actress marked their 14th wedding anniversary earlier this year, writing on Instagram, “Falling in love with Christine was the best thing I ever did (next to having our children).” The Department of Education had not responded to THR’s request for further comment at the time of its report.
