Guillermo del Toro, Kim Novak, and Ari Aster are among the notable figures set to appear in a new two-part documentary feature about Alfred Hitchcock's *Vertigo*, according to a report from Variety.

The film, titled **"The Fall,"** will be unveiled at the Fantasia International Film Festival's Frontières International Co-Production Market on July 22, 2026. It is currently in production and seeking an international sales agent and financing partners.

## What is the documentary about?

"The Fall" investigates the iconic and ambiguous final scene of Hitchcock's masterpiece, where the character Judy plummets from a belltower. The working title refers directly to this moment, which the documentary's director, Alexandre O. Philippe, has called Hitchcock's "biggest mystery." The film is structured as a film-noir investigation, with each interview subject offering their own perspective. Philippe said the project aims to "inhabit" the riddle of the fall rather than solve it.

## When will it be released?

The producers are planning a major rollout for the 70th anniversary of *Vertigo* in 2028. The film has a pre-sale to France's Cine+ and is gathering early interest from festivals.
