A small white speck tumbled from Robbie Williams' mouth and landed squarely on his microphone during a live interview. The moment, captured on camera and shared across social media, immediately set tongues wagging with a single, salacious theory: cocaine.

It happened during an interview with German broadcaster MagentaTV, shortly after Williams performed at the World Cup final's closing ceremony alongside Nicole Scherzinger and Laura Pausini. As the singer spoke, the object fell; he picked it off the mic, then touched his hair, leaving the particle stuck to his forehead. Online, fans were quick to insist the speck had come from his nose, not his mouth.

Williams didn't let the rumors simmer for long. The very next day, he took to Instagram with a video to address the speculation directly. Adopting a mock-serious tone, he said, "Just like to apologize for the object that fell onto the microphone yesterday. I was deeply unprofessional, and people worried about me, but it's okay because I had several more.". He then stuck out his tongue to reveal several mints, laughing. He captioned the post with a pun: "Just woke up and feeling…. Mint.".

A source close to the singer confirmed to OK! Magazine that the object was, in fact, a piece of a mint. With his characteristically dry humor, Williams turned a viral drug rumor into a minty-fresh joke, effectively shutting down the frenzy while acknowledging the absurdity of it all.
