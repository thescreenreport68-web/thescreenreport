Sophie Nélisse is heading to Netflix for *This Summer Will Be Different*, but fans wasted no time making it about something else: the news that *Yellowjackets* will wrap after season four. The reaction? Warm, grateful, and surprisingly chill — no meltdowns, just a collective nod that this is the right call.

## The Endgame They Actually Wanted

Co-creator Ashley Lyle broke the news on Instagram, framing it as a deliberate, thoughtful choice. She wrote, "After three incredible seasons, and great consideration, we’re excited to announce that we will be bringing the story of Yellowjackets to its twisted conclusion in this fourth and final season." The message wasn't about cancellation — it was about a story hitting its intended finish line. Lyle got personal, reflecting on the experience: "Telling this emotional, wild, and deeply human story has been a profoundly meaningful experience and a true honor for us." And she closed with a tease that felt perfectly *Yellowjackets*: "We can’t wait to share the final chapter with you and hope you find it…delicious." The post drew a simple, direct salute from author Jeff VanderMeer, who chimed in: "i like yellowjackets."

## The Reactions Were All Heart

Instead of outrage, there was a quiet hum of appreciation. Fans respected the vision — and one thing is certain: nobody is done talking about this. The community immediately lit up, reaching out to each other.

One user wrote a post seeking fellow enthusiasts, saying, "#promosky any yellowjackets melanie lynskey fans wanna oomf up <3 i also love ariana grande, marina, dan and phil and hate ai slop and capitalism."

Another fan posted a similar call into the digital void, writing, "calling out to any melanie lynskey / yellowjackets fans …… can u hear me." These posts highlight how the show has fostered a specific, shared identity among its viewers, and major announcements become moments to strengthen those bonds.

Other reactions were more tangential but painted a picture of how the show has woven itself into the fabric of fans' lives. One person shared a personal anecdote, posting, "amazing that Wes, T's oldest/autistic brother, wants to go to Las Vegas to see the band that does the Halloween club song in Yellowjackets play at Encore Beach Club for his birthday."

## A Gift of a Final Season

The overwhelmingly positive tone speaks to the show's secure place in the culture. *Yellowjackets* isn't getting the ax — it's getting a runway. In an era where shows either vanish without resolution or get milked dry, a planned finale is a genuine gift. It lets fans prepare and trust the destination. And Sophie Nélisse's new Netflix role? That just sweetens the deal — proof the cast will keep thriving beyond the wilderness. So the conversation now points forward: savoring the final season and looking ahead to her romantic comedy. It's a rare fandom farewell — not mourning, but grateful anticipation for one last, delicious chapter.
