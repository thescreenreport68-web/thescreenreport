Beyoncé just gave her BeyHive a reason to buzz. On July 4, the Grammy winner released a surprise single titled "Morning Dew (Donk)", marking her first new music drop in two years, according to a statement from her company, Parkwood Entertainment.

The soulful track kicks off a 60-day countdown. It leads to two milestones: Beyoncé's 45th birthday on September 4, and the reissue of her 2006 album *B'DAY*. The single is "a direct nod to her loyal BeyHive to commemorate the upcoming epic celebration of B'DAY," the statement said.

*B'DAY* debuted worldwide on September —4, 2006, and hit U.S. stores a day later. Beyoncé co-wrote "Morning Dew (Donk)" with Pharrell Williams, The-Dream, and Darius Dixon.

While this release is confirmed as a birthday and album anniversary celebration, fans have been laser-focused on the bigger mystery: the third and final act of her musical trilogy, following *Renaissance* and *Cowboy Carter*. Rumors have swirled for months.

A fan account on X claimed on April 27 that "The album is 100% complete, only vinyl pressings remain, expected to be finished before June 2026, showing a summer release," adding it would have "two big features." The post suggested the album would be influenced by old-school rock 'n' roll.

Beyoncé's publicist, Yvette Noel-Schure, swiftly shut that down. Commenting directly on the post, she wrote, "This is unequivocally false!!"

Still, the BeyHive remains hopeful. According to a message shared on her website in 2022, Beyoncé provided some context for the trilogy. She reportedly wrote, "This three-act project was recorded over three years during the pandemic," and added, "A time to be still, but also a time I found to be the most creative."

The singer has not revealed any official details about Act III. For now, the countdown is for *B'DAY* — but fans are watching for any sign the final act is coming.
