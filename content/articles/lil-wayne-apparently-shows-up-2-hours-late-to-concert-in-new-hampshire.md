When does a concert start? For Lil Wayne's fans in New Hampshire, the answer was much later than advertised. The rapper arrived two hours late to his scheduled performance at the BankNH Pavilion in Gilford, New Hampshire, on Friday night, according to fan reports. The show, which was supposed to begin around 9 PM ET, didn't see Wayne take the stage until 11 PM. He then played for only one hour before leaving.

Fan frustration was palpable, as reported by TMZ. The late arrival and abbreviated set came just days after Wayne failed to show up at all for a concert in Maine earlier in the week. He later apologized for that no-show but did not offer an explanation.

The New Hampshire incident appears to follow a similar pattern of unpredictability. On his social media, Wayne posted about the Gilford concert, seemingly indicating it went off without a hitch and thanking his New Hampshire supporters. He ended the post by stating he was on his way to Iowa, though TMZ noted there was no guarantee he would actually get there.

This series of last-minute changes and cancellations is starting to test the patience of his loyal fanbase. While artists occasionally face unforeseen delays, two significant disruptions in one week—one a complete cancellation, the other a severely delayed and shortened performance—point to a concerning trend. The lack of a clear explanation for either incident leaves fans to speculate about the reasons behind the schedule chaos.

For now, the facts are clear: Lil Wayne was late, played a short set in New Hampshire, and had recently missed a show in Maine. Any reasons behind these actions remain unconfirmed, as neither the rapper nor his representatives have commented on the specifics of the New Hampshire delay.
