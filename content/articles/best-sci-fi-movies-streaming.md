The single best sci-fi movie streaming right now is **Bong Joon-ho's Mickey 17**, and it just landed on **HBO Max**. Robert Pattinson plays an "expendable" worker who keeps dying and getting reprinted on a frozen colony world — it's the most ambitious big-studio sci-fi in years, and it's finally a click away.

But "best" depends on which app you already pay for. So below is a platform-by-platform ranked list of the **best sci-fi movies streaming right now** in June 2026 — new heavyweight arrivals up top, certified all-timers right behind them, each tagged with the exact service and a one-line reason it earns the spot. No teasing, no "you won't believe number 7." Pick a movie, open the app, press play.

A quick note on freshness, because it's the whole game with a "right now" list: streaming homes move constantly, and most rival guides are months stale. Everything here was re-verified this week. Where a title is a premium rental rather than included-with-subscription, I say so.

## The best new sci-fi movies streaming right now

These are the brand-new arrivals worth opening an app for tonight.

- **Mickey 17 (2025) — HBO Max.** Bong Joon-ho (*Parasite*) directs Robert Pattinson as a disposable colonist in a darkly funny, deeply strange survival story. The best new sci-fi movie streaming right now, full stop.
- **Frankenstein (2025) — Netflix.** Guillermo del Toro's gorgeous, grief-soaked take on Mary Shelley, with Oscar Isaac as Victor and Jacob Elordi as the Creature. More gothic horror than hard sci-fi, but it's the original "mad science" myth done beautifully.
- **Godzilla Minus One (2023) — Netflix.** The Oscar-winning Japanese epic only just hit Netflix, and it's the rare monster movie that's also a shattering postwar human drama. Don't skip it because it has subtitles.
- **The Running Man (2025) — Paramount+.** Edgar Wright remakes the Stephen King dystopia with Glen Powell as a desperate father hunted on live TV. Note: despite what some lists claim, it's on **Paramount+**, not Prime Video.
- **Project Hail Mary (2026) — premium VOD / MGM+.** Ryan Gosling carries Andy Weir's one-man space-rescue story. Right now it's a premium rental on Apple TV and Prime Video, or included with the MGM+ add-on — not standard Prime.

## What are the best sci-fi movies on Netflix right now?

Netflix has quietly become a sci-fi powerhouse in 2026. Beyond *Frankenstein* and *Godzilla Minus One* above, these are the **best sci-fi movies on Netflix right now**:

- **Ex Machina (2015) — Netflix (also on Max).** Alex Garland's chamber-piece about an AI named Ava remains the smartest, most unsettling android movie of the century. If you only watch one cerebral pick, make it this.
- **District 9 (2009) — Netflix (also on Max).** Neill Blomkamp's grimy alien-apartheid thriller still hits like a freight train — gruesome, political and thrilling at once.
- **Nope (2022) — Netflix.** Jordan Peele's widescreen UFO spectacle, newly added, plays even bigger at home than you remember.
- **The Adam Project (2022) — Netflix.** Ryan Reynolds time-travels to team up with his 12-year-old self. The crowd-pleaser of the bunch — perfect for a family movie night.

That mix of brand-new prestige and modern classics is why Netflix and Prime Video together cover most of what you'd want; if you split a subscription with someone, this is the half to keep.

## What are the best sci-fi movies on Max right now?

Max (HBO Max) is where the heavyweight spectacle lives. The **best sci-fi movies on Max right now**:

- **Dune: Part Two (2024) — Max.** Denis Villeneuve's desert war epic is the most visually overwhelming blockbuster on any service. For the full saga, see our [Denis Villeneuve movies ranked](/movies/denis-villeneuve-movies-ranked) and our [Dune: Part Two review](/reviews/dune-part-two-review).
- **The Matrix (1999) — Max.** The film that rewired action cinema. Still essential, still cool.
- **Mickey 17 (2025) — Max.** Yes, again. It's the best thing on the platform right now.
- **Ex Machina & District 9 — Max.** Both live here too, if Max is the only app you keep.

## Best sci-fi movies on Paramount+ and the all-timers

Paramount+ is the sleeper service for five-star sci-fi:

- **Interstellar (2014) — Paramount+ (free on Pluto TV).** Christopher Nolan's black-hole odyssey is the closest thing to a sure-thing on this list. It also streams free with ads on Pluto TV. Curious how Nolan stacks up? See our [Christopher Nolan movies ranked](/movies/christopher-nolan-movies-ranked).
- **Arrival (2016) — Paramount+.** Villeneuve and Amy Adams turn first contact into a quiet meditation on language, time and loss. The thinking person's alien movie.

## What sci-fi movies are streaming free right now?

You don't need another subscription. **Streaming free right now**: *Interstellar* on Pluto TV, plus *Looper* and *Pacific Rim*, which rotate through Pluto TV and Tubi's free, ad-supported tiers. Library-card holders can also stream *Arrival* free on Kanopy and Hoopla. The catalogs shift weekly, so check the app — but on any given night there's a genuinely great free sci-fi pick waiting.
