The creators of HBO Max's *[Stuart Fails To Save the Universe](/tv/stuart-fails-to-save-the-universe-premiere-kills-off-a-big-bang-theory/)* designed its opening credits to be unskippable, packing each sequence with spoilers and a unique musical arrangement. According to Deadline, Chuck Lorre, Bill Prady, and Zak Penn crafted the animated title sequences for their *The Big Bang Theory* spinoff as a direct response to streaming habits.

Lorre told Deadline he was unhappy with the streaming culture of people skipping opening credits. "What if we made a title sequence that you could skip but you shouldn't?" he said. The solution was to make every title sequence different and worth watching.

## What do the title sequences reveal?

Each episode's opening credits feature a series of fourth wall-breaking messages, capped by a spoiler specific to that installment. In the premiere episode, the spoiler reads "Barry Dies." Barry is Denise's boyfriend in a post-apocalyptic Pasadena, and his death is portrayed as simple and pointless after he says "Follow me!" and charges into a heavily guarded CalTech.

The creators embraced the spoiler concept for its comedic value. "We were just trying to keep ourselves entertained and do things that were different," Penn said. Prady added that the idea played on online culture's obsession with spoilers, making the audience laugh with a spoiler that is "just sort of kind of meaningless."

## Who composed the theme music?

Danny Elfman, composer of *The Simpsons* theme, wrote the main title music. After Lorre loved the initial theme, he asked Elfman for a unique challenge. "I wrote it, Chuck loved it, and he said, ‘So here’s the thing. I need you to do 10 variations on it,'" Elfman recalled. He agreed, drawing inspiration from each episode to create a distinct version of the theme for all ten installments.

The series, which premiered in July 2026, follows comic book store owner Stuart Bloom and his friends as they travel through alternate realities to fix a multiverse-threatening device.
