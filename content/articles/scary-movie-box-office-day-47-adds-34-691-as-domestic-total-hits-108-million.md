The Franchise Returns With a Vengeance

Twenty-six years after their first brush with a masked killer, the beloved 'Core Four' are back in the crosshairs in *[Scary Movie](/movies/scary-movie-box-office-day-46-adds-32-282-as-domestic-total-hits-108-million/) (2026)*. Directed by Michael Tiddes, this 96-minute comedy-horror sequel promises that no modern horror movie IP is safe from its signature brand of parody, bringing the iconic franchise roaring back to life.

The original stars have fully returned to their legendary roles. Marlon Wayans reprises his multi-faceted performance as Shorty, 'Joe', 'Tiffany', and Count Brolock, while Shawn Wayans is back as Ray. Anna Faris returns as the perpetually screaming Cindy, and Regina Hall is once again the sharp-tongued Brenda. They're joined by a new generation of potential victims played by Olivia Rose Keegan as Sara, Savannah Lee Nassif as Tuesday, Cameron Scott Roberts as Jack, and Sydney Park as DEI.

## A Box Office Haul That's No Joke

The **Scary Movie 2026 box office** performance is a clear testament to the franchise's lasting power.

The film's availability has expanded to digital rental and purchase platforms including JustWatch TV, Amazon Video, Apple TV Store, and Google Play Movies, ensuring the laughs—and scares—continue long after its theatrical run.

## At the Box Office

Scary Movie has grossed $108,044,772 at the domestic box office, 47 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 225 theaters. The film added $34,691 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $230 million. It carries a reported production budget of $30 million.
