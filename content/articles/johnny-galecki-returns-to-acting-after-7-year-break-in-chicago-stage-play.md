Johnny Galecki is returning to acting after a seven-year break, according to a report by TheWrap. The actor announced on Instagram that he will portray playwright Tennessee Williams in the Chicago stage production "Kowalski" this Fall.

## What role is Johnny Galecki playing?

Galecki revealed he will play the iconic American playwright Tennessee Williams. The production, titled "Kowalski," is set to run in Chicago, the city where the actor first began his career. He expressed gratitude to the production team at Lookingglass Theatre Company for the invitation.

## What did he say about his break?

In his Instagram announcement, Galecki reflected on his time away from performing. "For the last seven years, I’ve been fortunate enough to spend more time living than performing," he wrote. He added, "It turns out those aren’t separate pursuits for me."

His former "Big Bang Theory" co-star Kaley Cuoco celebrated the news in the comments, writing "Yay!!" alongside three fire emojis. Recurring "TBBT" star Wil Wheaton exclaimed, "Oh wow! Break a leg!"

## What was his previous major role?

Galecki is best known for playing Dr. Leonard Hofstadter on the CBS sitcom "The Big Bang Theory." The series aired for 12 seasons from 2007 to 2019.
