Nell Tiger Free, Gemma Arterton, and Bill Nighy have joined a new television adaptation of Henry James’s *The Portrait of a Lady*, according to a report from Deadline. The high-end series is a co-production for PBS Masterpiece, the BBC, and Fox TV.

## Who is playing Isabel Archer?

Nell Tiger Free will play the central role of Isabel Archer, the American ingénue who travels to Europe seeking experience. Gemma Arterton is cast as Madame Merle, one of the expatriate schemers Isabel encounters. Jim Sturgess will play the charming but calculating Gilbert Osmond.

Bill Nighy takes the role of Mr. Touchett, who is enamored with Isabel’s spirit, while Miranda Richardson plays her eccentric aunt, Mrs. Touchett.

## Where is the series filming?

Production has kicked off in the United Kingdom, Hungary, and Croatia. The series is written by Rebecca Lenkiewicz and directed by Aisling Walsh.

## What is the source material?

The project adapts Henry James’s 1881 novel, which follows Isabel Archer as she inherits a fortune and navigates the manipulations of European society. This marks the first significant adaptation since Jane Campion’s 1996 film starring Nicole Kidman.

Suzanne Simpson, Masterpiece's scripted content chief, said of the project, **“The caliber of talent behind this series is exceptional.”**

The series is produced by Number 9 Films in association with Beta Film and High Road Entertainment, with Fox Entertainment Global handling worldwide sales.
