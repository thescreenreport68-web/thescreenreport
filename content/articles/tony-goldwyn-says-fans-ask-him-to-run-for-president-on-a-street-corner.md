Tony Goldwyn confirmed that fans regularly ask him to run for president, a fact he revealed during a playful conversation with Kerry Washington. The exchange happened during an episode of Variety's 'Actors on Actors' series, as reported by OK! Magazine on July 19, 2026.

Goldwyn, 66, confirmed he gets the question often, particularly when he stands "on a street corner.". He humorously replied, "I used to say thank you so much, but that’s a really bad idea." Washington disagreed, telling him, "I don’t think you would be a terrible president, to be honest," She added that he would assemble "a really wonderful Cabinet and team around you."

Goldwyn then playfully offered Washington a cabinet position. She jokingly shut it down, saying, "You would not bring me. I would not be available." Their banter highlighted the easy chemistry that powered their on-screen dynamic for seven seasons.

## Reflecting on Fitz and Olivia

The pair also reflected on their 'Scandal' characters, President Fitzgerald 'Fitz' Grant III and Olivia Pope. Washington acknowledged the complexity, stating "They weren’t the healthiest couple," Goldwyn predicted the fictional couple's enduring connection, saying "I feel that Fitz and Olivia are together," Washington concurred, suggesting the pair would benefit from couple's therapy—a notion Goldwyn humorously endorsed.

The playful 'Actors on Actors' episode showcased the enduring fascination with Goldwyn's fictional presidency and the off-screen rapport that made 'Scandal' a hit.
