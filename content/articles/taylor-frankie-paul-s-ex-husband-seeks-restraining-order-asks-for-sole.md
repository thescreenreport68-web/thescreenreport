What’s happening in Utah? Taylor Frankie Paul’s ex-husband, Tate Paul, has petitioned a court for a temporary restraining order against the reality star and is seeking sole custody of their two children, according to documents obtained by TMZ.

The filings were reportedly submitted on Tuesday within their 2022 divorce case. Tate, who shares kids Indy, 8, and Ocean, 6, with Taylor, submitted a declaration and several exhibits. But the details are sealed, so the specific reasons behind the request are unavailable.

This legal move comes amid a separate, ongoing conflict with Taylor’s ex-boyfriend, Dakota Mortensen. An insider close to Taylor alleged to TMZ that the restraining order filing is “a direct result” of Mortensen “telling Tate lies about her.” The source claimed, “She feels Dakota will do anything to hurt her … including using the children, but she is a survivor.”

Meanwhile, a source told People that Taylor has voluntarily “checked into a facility upon the recommendation of her doctor” and is “committed to getting better.” Another source added, “The people around Taylor want her to get help,” and that “Everyone is just trying to protect the kids.”

The custody and legal drama has been playing out publicly. Just days before Tate’s filing, Mortensen called police in Bluffdale, Utah, with questions about the protective order governing visitation of his and Taylor’s 2-year-old son, Ever. According to the police department, they spoke to both parties and forwarded the case to the District Attorney to determine if a violation occurred. Taylor responded on social media, writing in a since-deleted Instagram Story, “Cops called on me again THIS WEEK … What are the odds?”

This latest chapter follows a series of domestic violence allegations and custody battles between Taylor and Mortensen earlier this year, which had already paused filming of "Secret Lives of Mormon Wives." Reps for Taylor, Tate, and Mortensen did not immediately respond to requests for comment from Page Six.

A rep for Taylor did not immediately respond to Page Six’s request for comment on Tuesday evening.
