Emily Blunt headlines Steven Spielberg's ambitious sci-fi thriller '[Disclosure Day](/movies/disclosure-day-box-office-day-34-adds-452-270-as-domestic-total-hits-112-9/)'. The film follows Daniel Kellner, a cybersecurity expert who becomes a whistleblower after uncovering corporate secrets about alien life, forcing him on the run. His path collides with that of meteorologist Margaret Fairchild, played by Blunt, who is experiencing strange phenomena and joins him in a desperate attempt to prove extraterrestrial existence. This 146-minute genre piece blends science fiction with high-stakes thriller elements under Spielberg's direction.

The cast is a formidable ensemble. Emily Blunt stars as the determined meteorologist Margaret Fairchild, with Josh O'Connor as the whistleblowing expert Daniel Kellner. Colin Firth plays corporate figure Noah Scanlon, while Colman Domingo appears as Hugo Wakefield. The supporting lineup includes Eve Hewson as Jane Blakenship, Wyatt Russell as Jackson, Elizabeth Marvel as Sister Maura, and Henry Lloyd-Hughes as Casper Boyd. It's a story of corporate conspiracy and cosmic mystery, brought to life by a top-tier cast.

## The Box Office Story

The financial narrative for 'Disclosure Day' is one of clear underperformance. It is now playing in just 1,299 theaters, a sharp decline indicating its rapid exit from the theatrical marketplace.

Globally, the picture is similarly challenging.

## At the Box Office

Disclosure Day has grossed $113,653,560 at the domestic box office, 36 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 1,299 theaters. The film added $400,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $233 million. It carries a reported production budget of $115 million.
