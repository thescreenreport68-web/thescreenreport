After years of fan skepticism, the Rick and Morty team is back with a defiantly confident Reddit AMA, promising Season 9 will prove they still have a full tank — but the internet did what it does best, and the response is anything but unanimous.

Is the well running dry? Their answer was a firm, almost defiant no.

## The Creators Came to Play

Scott Marder set the tone immediately, dropping the claim that has become a rallying cry for the hopeful fans. "I would say Season 9 will convince everyone we've got a full tank of gas left in us," he wrote, directly pushing back on the idea that the show is running on fumes. He doubled down on the show's edge: "The episode ideas are still REALLY flowing, and Rick still has plenty of mean left in him."

Steve Levy focused on character growth, teasing a Rick who is still evolving. "I'm excited for where we take Rick over the course of the next few seasons," Levy said. "He has come a very long way from who he was in the Pilot." The hints got more specific — Marder promised an episode centered on "false reality in a totally different cool way" and revealed that "Jerry has some really memorable ones coming in 9!"

The biggest tease was saved for Evil Morty.

But not everyone is buying it.

## The Fandom's Two Camps

The reaction was far from unanimous. Some fans matched the creators' energy with their own hype, but a loud chunk of the conversation was colored by frustration — over release delays, tonal shifts, and a feeling that the show's best days are behind it.

For every post raving about recent episodes — "Last two Rick and Morty eps have been pretty amazing," one fan wrote — there was another pointing out logistical headaches. Finished Episode 903 'Rick Fu Hustle' and was a bit disappointed it stopped. Inconvenient because Adult Swim (linear) has ads." That's not just nitpicking; it shows how release strategies can kill the communal buzz.

But even the skeptics couldn't deny the show's cultural impact. The conversation wasn't just about plot — it was about what Rick and Morty means. One standout post came from a viewer having a belated epiphany. "i had never seen a whole episode of rick and morty until now, and now i get it," they wrote, landing on a perfect, succinct definition: "rick and morty is doctor who for cynical assholes." That comparison nails the show's mix of infinite sci-fi possibilities and nihilistic self-awareness — the very thing that hooked its core audience.

## Why the Discourse Isn't Cooling Off Any Time Soon

This split isn't just about quality. It's what happens when a show enters its second decade. Early seasons thrived on shock, innovation, and transgressive discovery. Now it's an institution. The creators are promising refinement — a deeper Rick, a bigger Evil Morty — while some fans crave the raw chaos of the beginning. Throw in streaming delays and platform exclusivity, and you get a fandom that can't agree.

The conversation also showed how deep the show's cultural reach goes, often in absurd ways. One user posted, "Just saw Mitch McConnell walk out of the smoke shop with a Rick and Morty bong and $40 in singles in his hand." It's a meme-ready, surreal image that proves Rick and Morty's iconography has leaked into the wider world in ways the creators never imagined.

So where does this leave us? Right at the doorstep of Season 9's premiere. The creators have laid their cards on the table. The optimistic fans are ready to be proven right; the skeptics are waiting to be won over — or have their doubts confirmed. The AMA was a tense, energetic preview of the debate to come. The only thing that will settle it is the show itself.
