Jessica Dimmock’s documentary 'Maternal Instinct' is a gripping 97-minute true-crime film that unravels the horrific 2020 murder of Reagan Simmons-Hancock and the kidnapping of her unborn baby. The story begins with police pulling over a woman who claims she just gave birth — but the baby and the blood aren’t hers. This crime documentary delves into the twisted lies and brutal reality behind the case, focusing on Taylor Parker, who faked a pregnancy to deceive those around her before committing a shocking murder. Directed by Dimmock, the film combines interviews, police bodycam footage, and archival photos to paint a chilling portrait of deception and cruelty.

Audiences have responded strongly to 'Maternal Instinct,' captivated by its raw and unsettling portrayal of a rare fetal abduction case. The film’s unique angle lies in its exploration of Taylor Parker’s prolonged selfishness and cruelty, which director Jessica Dimmock highlights as particularly disturbing. While the documentary leaves out some details, its focus on the human cost and the shocking brutality of the crime has made it a standout in the true-crime genre.

## Streaming Metrics and What They Mean

In its third week on Netflix, 'Maternal Instinct' streamed an impressive 14 million hours, securing the #3 spot on Netflix’s Top 10 list. This sustained viewership reflects strong word-of-mouth and ongoing interest in the film’s dark subject matter. The film’s ability to hold steady in the competitive streaming landscape underscores its resonance with true-crime fans and documentary viewers alike.

## What 14 Million Hours Means for 'Maternal Instinct'

Streaming 14 million hours in a single week signals that 'Maternal Instinct' is more than a fleeting curiosity; it’s a compelling narrative that continues to engage a broad audience. For Jessica Dimmock and the production team, these numbers validate the film’s impact and the public’s appetite for deeply unsettling, real-life crime stories.
