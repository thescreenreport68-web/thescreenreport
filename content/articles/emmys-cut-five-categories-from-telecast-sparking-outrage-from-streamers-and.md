The Television Academy plans to move five Emmy categories from its primetime telecast to the Creative Arts Emmys weekend, according to a report from Variety. The decision was made after a vote by the Board of Governors.

This move has reportedly sparked outrage among major streamers, networks, and industry professionals. An awards strategist told the outlet, "I don’t know how the Emmys fucked this up so bad."

## Which categories were cut from the telecast?

The five categories being shifted are writing for a variety series; supporting actor and supporting actress in a limited or anthology series or movie; directing for a limited or anthology series or movie; and writing for a limited or anthology series or movie.

## Why is this decision so controversial?

The change creates major logistical headaches. It forces numerous high-profile nominees to attend the first weekend of ceremonies over Labor Day weekend. This conflicts with major film festivals and requires studios to plan for two consecutive weekends of events.

More critically, it risks historic wins happening off-air. The Creative Arts ceremonies currently have no red carpet and are typically edited into a tape-delayed special.

Academy chair Cris Abrego defended the move, stating the goal was to create a better viewing experience for the main telecast.

Talks are reportedly ongoing to produce a tape-delayed special for the newly expanded Creative Arts weekend, but nothing has been confirmed.
