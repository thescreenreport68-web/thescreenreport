Former James Bond casting director Debbie McWilliams believes the next actor to play 007 should be an unknown, according to a report by IndieWire. McWilliams, who has worked on 13 Bond films and cast three actors in the role, stated in an interview with The Independent that it is "absolutely essential" for Bond to remain "a total enigma."

## What did the former casting director say?

Her comments advocate for a clean slate as the franchise searches for Daniel Craig's successor. McWilliams' perspective suggests that casting an actor without prior major roles or a public persona would allow audiences to fully immerse themselves in the character without preconceived expectations. This approach, she argues, aligns with the spy's core nature as a detached and shape-shifting figure.

## Who else has been rumored for the role?

While McWilliams champions an unknown, the casting rumor mill has swirled with more familiar names. IndieWire's report mentions actors such as Callum Turner, Harris Dickinson, and Jacob Elordi as potential contenders. The search coincides with a major transition for the franchise, as producers Barbara Broccoli and Michael G. Wilson have transferred creative control to Amazon MGM Studios.

## What's next for the Bond franchise?

The next chapter of the series is set to be directed by Denis Villeneuve and written by [Steven Knight](/movies/oasis-releases-first-teaser-for-don-t-look-back-in-anger-documentary/). As of July 2026, no official casting announcement has been made. The debate between selecting a known star or a fresh face continues, with McWilliams' insider view adding significant weight to the argument for an unknown actor to lead the iconic spy into a new era.
