The official trailer for *Spider-Man: Brand New Day* confirms Tom Holland’s return to a radically different status quo, launching a new, more mature chapter for the web-slinger.

Fighting crime full-time as Spider-Man in a world that doesn't remember him—and the pressure of seeing his old friends move on without him—sparks a change in Peter Parker he may not have the power to control. But that transformation might also be the only thing that can stop a shocking new threat to the city and those he loves - a powerful villain no one can even see.

## What is Spider-Man: Brand New Day about?

The story picks up four years after the spell cast by Doctor Strange in *Spider-Man: No Way Home*. Everyone has forgotten Peter Parker exists. He now lives in a small apartment, anonymously protecting New York City from street-level crime. Director Destin Daniel Cretton says Parker is "dedicating his entire existence" to being Spider-Man. This intense pressure, combined with seeing his former friends MJ and Ned move on at MIT, triggers a dangerous evolution in his powers. He gains the ability to create organic spider webs, similar to Tobey Maguire's Spider-Man.

## Who is in the cast?

Tom Holland leads as Peter Parker, exploring a more profound and isolated version of the hero. Zendaya returns as MJ, who is attending MIT and has a new love interest, creating conflict for Peter. Jacob Batalon is back as Ned Leeds, who has created a "Spidey Tracker" app to find and thank Spider-Man. The new additions are significant. Jon Bernthal reprises his role as Frank Castle, the Punisher, bringing his violent brand of justice into Spider-Man's world. Mark Ruffalo returns as Bruce Banner, now a professor at Empire State University, whom Peter turns to for help with his changing abilities.

The supporting cast includes Sadie Sink in a significant role, Michael Mando returning as Mac Gargan/Scorpion, and Tramell Tillman as the head of the Department of Damage Control. Villains like Tombstone, Tarantula, and the organization The Hand will also appear.

## How does this fit into the MCU and Spider-Man's story?

This film is intended as the first in a new trilogy starring Tom Holland. Producer Kevin Feige has stated the goal was to finally show Holland as a "proper Spider-Man," on his own and dealing with local threats instead of world-ending events. The inclusion of street-level characters like the Punisher and connective tissue to the *Daredevil: Born Again* series reinforces this grounded direction. It represents a tonal shift from the high-school-centric previous trilogy toward a story about adulthood, identity, and consequence.

Director Destin Daniel Cretton, who previously directed *Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings*, brings a fresh perspective. The title is derived from the 2008 comic book storyline that reset Spider-Man's status quo, signaling a true fresh start for the cinematic version.

## When does it come out?

*Spider-Man: Brand New Day* is scheduled to be released in the United States on **July 31, 2026**. It will be part of Phase Six of the [Marvel](/movies/mcu-movies-in-order/) Cinematic Universe. Filming took place from August to December 2025 in locations including Glasgow, Scotland, and Pinewood Studios in England.
