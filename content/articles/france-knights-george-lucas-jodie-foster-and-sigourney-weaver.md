France has knighted Hollywood legends [George Lucas](/celebrity/george-lucas-created-star-wars-after-hollywood-rejected-his-apocalypse-now/), Jodie Foster, and Sigourney Weaver, according to a report from IMDb. The honor recognizes their significant contributions to film and culture.

## What is the French Knighthood?
The French knighthood is a high honor awarded for cultural achievements. Awarding it to foreign nationals in the arts is a significant gesture of cultural appreciation. The honor for these three American filmmakers and actors underscores France's long-standing admiration for Hollywood's storytelling.

## Who Received the Honor?
The report names three specific recipients. George Lucas is the creator of the Star Wars franchise. Sigourney Weaver is a renowned actor. The French government is claiming these myth-makers as part of its own cultural legacy through this honor.

## What Does This Mean?
This knighthood is a formal recognition of the global impact these artists have had. It highlights the cross-cultural influence of American cinema. The move is seen as France celebrating and incorporating Hollywood's most influential creators into its pantheon of honored figures.
