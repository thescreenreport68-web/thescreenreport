Marvel Studios President [Kevin Feige](/movies/kevin-feige-calls-failed-blade-reboot-a-loser-moment-for-marvel/) has confirmed plans to introduce a live-action version of Miles Morales into the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

According to Deadline, Feige stated the popular Spider-Man character will appear in a future movie after [Sony Pictures](/movies/hugh-jackman-ridley-scott-s-treasure-island-package-in-talks-at-sony/)' animated 'Spider-Verse' film series concludes. The final chapter, *Spider-Man: Beyond the Spider-Verse*, is scheduled for release on June 18, 2027.

## What did Kevin Feige say?

Feige made the comments in an interview with China’s Watching Hollywood. “The good news is, Sony has the Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse series, which are very popular and excellent, and *Beyond the Spider-Verse* is coming out next year,” he said. “But yes, we definitely have plans [for a live-action Miles], and I think a live-action Spider-Man movie has this destiny: to add Miles sometime after the Spider-Verse series ends.”

He also noted that audiences saw a hint toward this future in *Spider-Man: No Way Home*, where Electro says, “Somewhere there’s bound to be a Black Spider-Man.”

## What's the status of future Spider-Man movies?

Feige indicated that discussions about the web-slinger's cinematic future are already underway with his production partner, Amy Pascal. “We’ve been thinking and discussing more Spider-Man adventures, regardless of agreements or contracts,” Feige said. “My production partner, Amy Pascal, is already working on it, and we’ve had in-depth discussions about what the next two, three, four, and five Spider-Man movies might be about.”

This follows Pascal's own 2023 confirmation to Variety that a live-action Miles Morales movie was in development, where she said, “You’ll see all of it. It’s all happening.” Meanwhile, [Tom Holland](/movies/tom-holland-praises-robert-pattinson-s-casting-in-the-odyssey-you-re-so/) is set to return as Peter Parker in *Spider-Man: Brand New Day*, premiering in theaters on July 30.
