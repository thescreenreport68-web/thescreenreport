**The official trailer for James Gunn’s *Superman* reveals the first chapter of the new DC Universe, introducing David Corenswet as a younger, more hopeful Man of Steel.** This isn't a sequel to Henry Cavill's DCEU tenure but a complete reboot, with a story that draws direct inspiration from the beloved comic book *All-Star Superman*. The film sees Superman navigating his dual heritage while facing unintended global consequences, setting the tone for a connected universe of heroes and villains.

## What Is the Story of James Gunn's Superman?

The film follows Kal-El as he tries to reconcile his Kryptonian heritage with his human upbringing as Clark Kent, a journalist at the **Daily Planet** in Metropolis. His life becomes unbalanced when he falls for colleague Lois Lane and makes friends with other superheroes who find him naive. The central conflict begins when Superman intervenes to stop the nation of Boravia from invading Jarhanpur, an international crisis secretly orchestrated by tech billionaire **Lex Luthor**. This act leads to massive public backlash, forcing Superman to win back trust with the help of his reporter and superhero colleagues, including members of the **Justice Gang**.

## Who Stars in the New Superman Movie?

**David Corenswet** leads the cast as a 25-year-old Clark Kent / Superman, a portrayal described as the "embodiment of truth, justice and the American way." Corenswet also plays **Ultraman**, a Kryptonian clone created by Luthor. **Rachel Brosnahan** is Lois Lane, a fiercely intelligent reporter who is initially unsure about Clark. **Nicholas Hoult** portrays Lex Luthor as an obsessive genius driven by envy, with a look inspired by *All-Star Superman*.

The supporting cast is vast, establishing the wider DCU. It includes **Edi Gathegi** as Mister Terrific, **Nathan Fillion** as Guy Gardner / Green Lantern, and **Isabela Merced** as Hawkgirl—together they form the Justice Gang. **Anthony Carrigan** plays Metamorpho, **Skyler Gisondo** is Jimmy Olsen, and **Wendell Pierce** is Daily Planet editor Perry White. The cast also features the superpowered dog **Krypto** and cameos from future DCU stars like Milly Alcock as Supergirl.

## Why Is This Superman Movie So Important for DC?

This film is the foundational entry for the **DC Universe (DCU)**, specifically **Chapter One: Gods and Monsters**. After years of stalled sequels and a disconnected DC Extended Universe, co-CEOs James Gunn and Peter Safran are building a cohesive, long-term plan starting here. The movie moves past the previous era, which saw a Henry Cavill-led *Man of Steel* sequel stuck in development for nearly a decade. Gunn's vision is deliberately more colorful and earnest, aiming to restore Superman's iconic status as a symbol of hope.

## What Are the Major Influences on This Superman Reboot?

James Gunn has cited **Grant Morrison and Frank Quitely's *All-Star Superman*** as a primary influence, a comic renowned for its optimistic and mythic take on the character. The film also draws inspiration from the classic **Superman animated series** and the Silver Age comics, where Luthor was a "reckless scientific genius." Gunn even incorporates lesser-known canon elements, like the hypnotic glasses Clark uses to protect his secret identity. This approach suggests a deep reverence for Superman's history while forging a new path.

## When Does the New Superman Movie Come Out?

*Superman* is scheduled for release in theaters on **July 11, 2025**. It will premiere a few days earlier at the TCL Chinese Theater on July 7. The film kicks off a new slate for DC Studios, with a sequel, *Man of Tomorrow*, already scheduled for 2027 and a spin-off HBO Max series featuring Jimmy Olsen in development. Its success is critical for launching the shared universe Gunn and Safran have planned.
