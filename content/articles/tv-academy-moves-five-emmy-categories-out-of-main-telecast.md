## Which categories are being moved?
The categories affected are writing for a variety series, supporting actor and actress in a limited series or movie, directing for a limited series or movie, and writing for a limited series or movie. This means nominees like Richard Gadd, star of HBO Max’s “Half Man,” who received a supporting actor nomination, will now have their awards presented earlier in the week.

## Why is this controversial?
The move has been swiftly condemned by the Directors Guild of America, the Writers Guild of America, and SAG-AFTRA. The guilds issued a joint statement arguing, “The decision to eliminate these categories from the primetime broadcast devalues the contributions of the talented people the Academy is meant to honor.” This marks a repeat of a similar attempt to move categories in 2005, which was reversed after industry backlash.

The [TV Academy](/celebrity/tv-academy-defends-beast-in-me-emmy-nomination-amid-eligibility-questions/), NBC, and producers Jesse Collins Entertainment are reportedly making the change as a gamble to improve ratings and reviews for the telecast.
