Darren Aronofsky’s AI-focused production company, Primordial Soup, is looking to raise $15 million in equity, according to a recent Securities and Exchange Commission filing. The director and his producing partner, Dylan Golden, are listed as executive officers of the venture. The filing indicates the company has already sold a little over $11 million, with just under $4 million left to raise.

The news was first reported by The Hollywood Reporter. The company launched last year and quickly announced a partnership with Google DeepMind to produce short films, signaling Aronofsky’s commitment to exploring artificial intelligence in storytelling.

## What is Primordial Soup?

Primordial Soup is a studio venture founded by Aronofsky and Golden specifically to develop and produce content using artificial intelligence tools. In a statement at its launch, Aronofsky framed the effort as a natural progression in filmmaking technology. "Filmmaking has always been driven by technology," he said. "After the Lumiere Brothers and Edison’s ground-breaking invention, filmmakers unleashed the hidden storytelling power of cameras. Today is no different. Now is the moment to explore these new tools and shape them for the future of storytelling."

## What is the company's status?

The SEC filing from last week provides a concrete snapshot of the company's current fundraising efforts. It confirms the $15 million target and the amount already sold, moving the venture beyond its initial launch phase into active capital raising. Aronofsky’s most recent directorial work, the crime thriller "Caught Stealing," was released in 2025.

## What happens next?

The filing marks a formal step in securing the capital needed to advance Primordial Soup's projects. With the majority of its funding target already met, the company appears to be on solid footing to continue its work at the intersection of AI and cinematic narrative. The focus now shifts to how the studio will deploy these funds to develop its slate of AI-driven productions.
