A new trailer for the upcoming Resident Evil movie has arrived, signaling a fresh and fan-focused take on the iconic survival horror franchise. According to reports from Den of Geek and Siliconera, the footage for director [Zach Cregger](/movies/zach-cregger-teases-weapons-prequel-gladys-says-script-is-in-progress/)’s 2026 reboot aims to balance standalone horror with deep reverence for the source material.

Cregger’s film stars Austin Abrams as Bryan, a medical courier who accepts a dangerous, last-minute delivery to Raccoon City General Hospital. The official synopsis confirms his simple job turns into a fight for survival when a sudden, catastrophic outbreak occurs. The story unfolds on the same night as the events of the classic video game *Resident Evil 2*, but from a new perspective on the city's periphery.

## What Does the New Resident Evil Movie Trailer Show?

While the trailer itself hasn't been viewed for this preview, multiple outlets have detailed the specific fan service elements it contains. The footage reportedly establishes the terrifying speed of the zombie outbreak, with one character noting, "Everything that has happened in this town transpired in a matter of hours." For gamers, the trailer is said to be packed with authentic references to the series' survival horror roots.

Key details include the appearance of a classic typewriter—a series staple representing safe rooms and save points—and a tense sequence where Bryan must frantically search for shotgun shells before he can load his weapon. This emphasis on scarce resources and vulnerability directly mirrors the gameplay that defined the original *Resident Evil* titles.

## Who is Starring in the New Resident Evil Movie?

Austin Abrams leads the cast as the everyman protagonist, Bryan. He is joined by Zach Cherry, Kali Reis, and Paul Walter Hauser, with Johnno Wilson also appearing. The film marks a reunion for Abrams and director Zach Cregger, who previously collaborated on the horror film *Weapons*.

## How Does This Reboot Compare to Past Resident Evil Movies?

This project represents the second reboot of the film series in five years, following 2021's *Resident Evil: Welcome to Raccoon City*. Early reporting suggests Cregger is aiming for a middle ground between the original, more loosely adapted film series and the recent reboot that heavily leaned on fan service. The goal appears to be crafting a tense, standalone horror story that also rewards longtime fans with thoughtful easter eggs and faithful mechanics.

As a horror, science fiction, and thriller film with a reported budget of $80 million, this new entry seeks to reclaim the series' frightening atmosphere. *Resident Evil* is scheduled to arrive exclusively in theaters on September 18, 2026.
