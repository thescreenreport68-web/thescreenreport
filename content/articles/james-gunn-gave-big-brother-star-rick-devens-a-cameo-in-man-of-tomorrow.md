James Gunn confirmed that reality star Rick Devens was slated for a cameo in his upcoming DC film, "Man of Tomorrow," but had to cancel. The director revealed the news on social media, according to a report by Variety.

Gunn shared a clip from the live broadcast of "Big Brother" Season 28's Thursday eviction episode, where Devens gave the DC Studios co-head a shoutout. When a follower asked if Devens would appear in the Superman sequel, Gunn replied on Threads.

## What did James Gunn say about the cameo?

Gunn provided a direct answer about the canceled appearance. "No because he’s on ‘Big Brother’! Otherwise he would have been," he wrote. In a follow-up post, Gunn elaborated: "We do. He was gonna appear in the background of 'MoT' but he had to cancel (his awesome wife and kids still came). He couldn’t tell me why but told me I’d be excited. Obviously I was!"

## Who is Rick Devens?

Rick Devens is a former contestant on "Survivor" who was revealed as one of three surprise houseguests during the "Big Brother" Season 28 premiere.

## When does 'Man of Tomorrow' release?

"Man of Tomorrow" is the sequel to Gunn's "Superman," which was released in 2025. The film, starring David Corenswet as Superman and Nicholas Hoult as Lex Luthor, is slated for a July 9, 2027, theatrical release.
