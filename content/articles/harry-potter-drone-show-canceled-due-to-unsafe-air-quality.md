A Harry Potter-themed drone show at Subaru Park was canceled Thursday night due to unsafe air quality from nearby wildfires, according to 6abc Philadelphia.

The event, organized by Fever and Nova Sky Stories, was set to feature drones recreating characters and scenes from the popular series. Doors opened at 7:15 p.m., but attendees received an email around 8 p.m. announcing the 9:15 p.m. show would not go on.

## What Happened?

Jeremy Sutherland, a senior project manager with Fever, addressed the disappointed crowd. "We're so, so sorry for guests who came out," Sutherland said. "We know it's a commitment of time and effort to come to the show. We hope they will join us again this weekend."

Many attendees had traveled significant distances. Lance Wood of Enola said he drove over two hours with his family. "I'm just frustrated because my wife wanted to go so bad," he told the outlet. "Turnpike fees, gas fees - all that stuff in addition to spending $410 to be here."

## What Are the Options for Attendees?

Sutherland said guests will receive a voucher to attend Friday or Sunday's show. Saturday's show has been canceled due to expected rain. Those unable to attend the rescheduled dates can request refunds for Thursday's show and parking by contacting hello@feverup.com.

Organizers acknowledged communication problems. "We apologize for the sequence of how they were notified today and there was miscommunication along the way," Sutherland said. "We are reviewing what happened to make sure in the future guests are getting those notifications far in advance."

Some attendees expressed frustration with the late notice. Jaylyn Cortes of Vineland said, "You should have handled it better." Montanna Gallardo of Atco added, "We're all Harry Potter fans. We love Harry Potter, so for this to happen, it's very disappointing."
