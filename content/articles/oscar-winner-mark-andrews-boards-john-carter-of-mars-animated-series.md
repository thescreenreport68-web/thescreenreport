Oscar winner Mark Andrews has been announced as supervising director and co-showrunner for a new animated series titled **"John Carter, Warlord of Mars,"** according to TheWrap. The [Pixar](/movies/toy-story-5-fans-are-crying-and-cringing-at-the-same-time/) veteran, who directed the Academy Award-winning film "Brave," will co-run the project with head writer Michael Kogge.

The series is based on Edgar Rice Burroughs' classic science fiction novels, which follow a former Confederate soldier transported to a war-torn Mars.

## What is Mark Andrews' connection to John Carter?

Andrews co-wrote the screenplay for Disney's live-action "John Carter" film. "Animation is the best way to achieve the visual intensity and scope the story demands," Andrews stated.

## Who else is involved in the project?

Michael Kogge, who wrote and directed a previous "John Carter of Mars" audio series, initially developed the animated show and will serve as co-showrunner. Jim Sullos and Wolf Larson of Edgar Rice Burroughs, Inc. are overseeing the project for the author's estate.

Larson called Andrews and Kogge a "dream team" in a statement. Sullos added that Andrews brings "unparalleled creative vision and experience" to the Martian world.

## When will we learn more?

The first official details will be revealed during the pair's Comic-Con panel this week. The series represents the first animated adaptation of the Burroughs novels.
