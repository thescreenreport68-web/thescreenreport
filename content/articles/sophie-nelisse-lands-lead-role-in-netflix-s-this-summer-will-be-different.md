Sophie Nélisse has been cast in the lead role of Lucy for Netflix’s upcoming TV adaptation of Carley Fortune’s novel, *This Summer Will Be Different*, according to Deadline.

The French-Canadian actress, known for *Yellowjackets* and *Heated Rivalry*, will portray Lucy, a young woman navigating her twenties and her first real love with her best friend’s brother—the one person she was never supposed to fall for. The series is set across multiple summers on Prince Edward Island.

## Who is Sophie Nélisse playing?

Nélisse will play Lucy, one of three main characters in the story alongside her best friend Bridget and Bridget’s brother Felix. While Netflix did not provide a character description, in Fortune’s novel Lucy occupies a complicated space between her friend and Felix. The book opens with Lucy recalling her first meeting with Felix, who caught her eye while shucking oysters at a restaurant during a trip to PEI.

## When will filming start?

Production for the series is scheduled to take place in Prince Edward Island and Toronto later this summer. The show is executive produced by creators and showrunners Dane Clark and Linsey Stewart, along with author Carley Fortune, Jennifer Kawaja, Elise Cousineau, and Samantha Levine for Sphere Media.

*This Summer Will Be Different* joins Netflix’s growing slate of Canadian productions. Fortune’s novel became a New York Times bestseller after its 2024 release and has sold millions of copies worldwide.

Nélisse will soon return for the fourth and final season of Showtime’s *Yellowjackets*, where she plays Young Shauna.
