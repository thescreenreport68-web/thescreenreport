Peacock has reported its first-ever profitable quarter, according to Comcast's second-quarter earnings report.

The NBCUniversal streaming service posted an EBITDA of $189 million, with subscribers growing to 48 million. The growth was driven by coverage of the NBA playoffs, FIFA World Cup, and the reality series *Love Island*.

## What drove the profit?

The subscriber growth and profitability mark a significant milestone for the service, which launched almost six years ago.

A major driver was the company's studios division, where revenue soared 25 percent to $3 billion. This surge was powered by the performance of *The Super Mario Galaxy Movie* and the film *Obsession*.

## What is Comcast's next move?

Comcast is currently executing a plan to split the company in two. The separation will create a distinct Comcast cable and connectivity business, led by CEO Michael Angelakis, and a separate entertainment and content business, led by Mike Cavanagh.

While the streaming and studios businesses showed strength, Comcast warned of "near-term softness" in its theme parks division.
