The full setlist for Usher and Chris Brown's joint 2026 tour has been revealed, featuring 58 songs performed at the opening night in Denver. According to Billboard, the extensive list covers hits from both artists' long-running careers.

The news outlet provided a detailed breakdown of every song played in sequence during the first show of the highly anticipated tour. The collaboration, unofficially referred to by fans as the 'Raymond Brown' tour, marks a major event in contemporary R&B, bringing together two of the genre's most successful performers.

## What Songs Are on the Setlist?

The reported 58-song setlist suggests a marathon concert experience. While the exact order and specific song titles were detailed by Billboard, the length alone indicates the show will include a deep dive into both artists' catalogs. Fans can expect a mix of Usher's classic hits from albums like *Confessions* and *8701*, alongside Chris Brown's chart-toppers from his self-titled debut through recent projects.

The structure likely allows for solo sets from each artist, potential duets, and high-energy choreography that both performers are known for. An opening night setlist of this scale sets a high bar for production value and fan service for the remainder of the tour's scheduled dates.

## Where Did the Tour Start?

The tour launched in Denver in June 2026. The choice of city for the kickoff follows typical industry patterns for major arena tours. The detailed reporting from the first show gives fans attending later stops a clear preview of what to expect, though setlists can evolve throughout a tour's run.

This joint venture follows years of speculation and fan demand for a collaboration of this magnitude. Both Usher and Chris Brown have headlined their own successful global tours, but this marks their first major co-headlining series. The tour announcement and subsequent ticket sales were among the most talked-about events in music this year.
