Emily Blunt leads the cast of Steven Spielberg's latest cinematic venture, '[Disclosure Day](/movies/disclosure-day-box-office-day-38-adds-440-000-as-domestic-total-hits-114-7/),' a high-concept science fiction thriller that blends corporate intrigue with cosmic mystery. The film, clocking in at 146 minutes, follows cybersecurity expert Daniel Kellner, who becomes a whistleblower after discovering secrets about extraterrestrial life, forcing him to flee a powerful corporation. His path converges with meteorologist Margaret Fairchild, who is investigating bizarre atmospheric phenomena, and together they race to prove the existence of life beyond Earth before they are silenced.

## A Stellar Ensemble Cast

The film boasts a formidable ensemble. Emily Blunt stars as the determined meteorologist, Margaret Fairchild, while Josh O'Connor plays the fugitive tech expert, Daniel Kellner. They are joined by Colin Firth as the enigmatic corporate figure Noah Scanlon and Colman Domingo as Hugo Wakefield. The supporting cast includes Eve Hewson as Jane Blakenship, Wyatt Russell as Jackson, Elizabeth Marvel as Sister Maura, and Henry Lloyd-Hughes as Casper Boyd, creating a rich tapestry of characters caught in the conspiracy.

## Critical Reception

Critics have responded positively to the film, with review aggregator Rotten Tomatoes awarding it a solid score. The reception highlights Spielberg's enduring skill in crafting suspenseful, large-scale narratives, even as the film's financial outcome tells a more complex story of a modern blockbuster's margins.

## The Box Office Story: A Narrow Path to Profit

The final domestic numbers are in for 'Disclosure Day,' which is now playing in just 1,299 theaters as it winds down its run.

This narrow gap underscores the film's status as a borderline performer, a testament to the challenging economics of big-budget filmmaking today. The story of the *Disclosure Day* box office underperformance is one of a film that connected with critics but struggled to achieve the commercial altitude needed for a clear victory.

## At the Box Office

Disclosure Day has grossed $114,785,765 at the domestic box office, 39 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 1,299 theaters. The film added $185,595 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $233 million. It carries a reported production budget of $115 million.
