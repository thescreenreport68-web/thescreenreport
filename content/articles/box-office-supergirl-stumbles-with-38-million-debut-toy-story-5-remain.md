Warner Bros.' latest DC Universe entry, 'Supergirl,' has crashed to Earth at the box office, opening to a weak $38 million domestically and struggling to justify its $175 million price tag. The film's debut represents a significant stumble for the studio's rebooted superhero slate, landing far behind the massive opening of '[Superman](/movies/superman-2025-reactions-what-fans-are-saying/)' just months prior and failing to dethrone the family-friendly juggernaut 'Toy Story 5.' For a franchise hoping to build interconnected momentum, this is a concerning start that puts immediate pressure on its future chapters.

According to Variety, the Milly Alcock-led space adventure debuted in 3,600 North American theaters but fell short of its projected $50 million target. The global opening stands at $68 million, bringing its current worldwide total to $29 million according to verified data from TMDB. The film's premise, which sees Kara Zor-El reluctantly teaming with an unlikely companion on an interstellar journey, failed to connect with a broad audience in its crucial first frame. This soft launch places immense pressure on its international performance and subsequent weekend holds to avoid becoming a major financial loss for the studio.

## How does Supergirl's debut compare to Superman's?

The contrast is stark and damaging for DC's shared universe ambitions. As reported by Collider, 'Supergirl' earned only about a third of the box office debut posted by James Gunn's 'Superman.' That film, which launched the new DCU earlier in 2026, opened to massive numbers that set a high bar. For a franchise attempting to build a connected narrative across multiple films, a second chapter that opens this far behind the first creates a perception problem. It suggests the audience's excitement for the new universe may not be transferable between heroes, a critical issue for long-term planning.

The underperformance is particularly acute given the film's reported $175 million production budget, a figure that does not include global marketing costs. To reach a conventional break-even point, a film of this scale typically needs to earn 2.5 times its budget worldwide. That means 'Supergirl' needs to clear approximately $437.5 million globally. A $68 million global start makes that path extraordinarily difficult. The film must demonstrate exceptional staying power in the coming weeks, a tall order against summer competition.

## What won the weekend box office?

Pixar's '[Toy Story](/movies/best-movie-trilogies/) 5' easily retained the crown, adding an estimated $70 million in its second weekend. Its strong hold highlights the clear audience preference for a trusted, family-friendly brand over a risky new superhero venture. The animated sequel's continued dominance sucked oxygen out of the marketplace, leaving 'Supergirl' to fight for secondary screens and audience attention. The weekend result reinforces a recurring theme in modern Hollywood: established IP with multigenerational appeal often outperforms attempts to launch new franchise pillars, especially when those attempts receive mixed reception.

The result for 'Supergirl' is a clear setback. Directed by Craig Gillespie and featuring a cast that includes David Corenswet's Superman in a supporting role, the film was positioned as a key expansion of the DC Universe. Its struggle suggests the reboot still faces significant hurdles in winning over audiences burned by previous DC film cycles. Warner Bros. will now need to scrutinize its rollout strategy for upcoming films and potentially recalibrate budgets. For now, the Girl of Steel is grounded, while Woody and Buzz soar.
