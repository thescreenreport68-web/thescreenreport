Eddie Peng, Daniel Ings, and Artie Wilkinson-Hunt have joined the cast of the upcoming action-comedy “Jason Statham Stole My Bike,” according to a report from Variety. The film stars Jason Statham in a fictionalized, meta version of himself, a concept the outlet compares to Nicolas Cage's role in “The Unbearable Weight of Massive Talent.”

## Who is directing the film?
David Leitch is directing the project. Leitch’s production company, 87North, is producing alongside Black Bear, a frequent collaborator of Statham’s. The film also stars Hannah Waddingham, whose casting was recently announced.

## When is the release date?
The screenplay was written by Alison Flierl, who created the story with Scott Chernoff. Kelly McCormick, Leitch, and Statham are among the film’s producers.

## What else is known about the new cast?
The report highlighted the new actors' recent credits. Eddie Peng, a major star in Chinese-language cinema, recently starred in the Cannes award-winning film “Black Dog.” Daniel Ings has extensive television work, including roles in HBO's “A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms” and Netflix's “Sex Education.” Young actor Artie Wilkinson-Hunt's credits include the HBO series “C.B. Strike” and a role in the upcoming “Masters of the Universe” film.
