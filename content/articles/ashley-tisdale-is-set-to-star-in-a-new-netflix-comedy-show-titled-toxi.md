Ashley Tisdale is taking her parenting gripes from the page to the screen.

According to Deadline and The Hollywood Reporter, Tisdale is set to star in a new Netflix comedy series titled 'Toxic Moms.' The project is in development and follows her viral essay, "Toxic Mom Group."

She will team up with Ali Wong and Sabrina Jalees on the series, as reported by Pop Crave.

The show is confirmed to be in the works at Netflix, as announced by the trade publications.

The concept stems directly from Tisdale's own experiences, which she detailed in her personal essay. This move from a viral personal story to a scripted series is a classic Hollywood pivot, leveraging relatable, modern parenting anxieties for comedy.

Tisdale, best known for her role as Sharpay Evans in the 'High School Musical' franchise, has more recently been open about her life as a mother. Partnering with Wong—a stand-up and actress celebrated for her raw, hilarious takes on motherhood and marriage—and Jalees, a writer and comedian, suggests the series will have a sharp, authentic comedic voice.

No plot details, release date, or additional casting have been confirmed at this time.

The development signals Netflix's continued investment in comedy driven by specific, often female-centric perspectives. It also marks a new chapter for Tisdale, moving from teen idol to a producer and star of content drawn from her adult life.

All details are sourced from the confirmed trade reports and the original social media announcement.

According to The Hollywood Reporter, the series is currently in the development stage at Netflix. This means the core creative team, including Tisdale, Wong, and Jalees, is actively working on the show's concept, story, and scripts, but full-scale production has not yet begun. The project was reported by trade publications in early July 2026, indicating it is a recent development in the entertainment landscape.

The collaboration brings together distinct comedic talents. Ali Wong is an acclaimed stand-up comedian, actress, and writer known for her Netflix specials and projects that often explore themes of family, career, and cultural identity with unflinching humor. Sabrina Jalees is a comedian and writer with credits on various television shows, known for her sharp, observational comedy. Their involvement, alongside Tisdale's personal source material, points to a series aiming for a specific and authentic tone regarding contemporary motherhood.

For Ashley Tisdale, this project represents a significant evolution in her career. While she remains iconic for her role in the Disney Channel phenomenon 'High School Musical,' she has steadily built a profile as a producer and entrepreneur. Developing 'Toxic Moms' allows her to leverage her personal narrative and experiences as a mother into a mainstream comedy series on a major streaming platform. It follows a trend of actors creating and starring in projects based on their own lives, offering a built-in sense of authenticity and relatability for audiences.

The viral essay that inspired the show, 'Toxic Mom Group,' tapped into a widespread cultural conversation about the pressures and judgments within modern parenting, particularly in digital spaces like social media groups. By adapting this concept, the series has the potential to explore the humor and drama found in the competitive, advice-filled, and often overwhelming world of parenthood today. The involvement of Netflix suggests the platform sees broad appeal in this premise, continuing its strategy of backing creator-driven comedy with strong, specific points of view.
