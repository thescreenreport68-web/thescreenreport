Zoey Deutch’s ‘Voicemails for Isabelle’ Dominates Netflix with 61 Million Hours Viewed

The film’s blend of heartfelt emotion and romantic comedy continues to resonate strongly with viewers, cementing its place as a breakout streaming hit.

Directed by Leah McKendrick, *Voicemails for Isabelle* runs 119 minutes and tells the story of Jill Shaw (Zoey Deutch), a young woman who copes with the loss of her sister by leaving voicemails filled with confessions, humor, and heartbreak. Unbeknownst to her, these voicemails are redirected to Wes (Nick Robinson), a real estate agent who becomes enamored with Jill’s candid messages despite never meeting her. This romantic comedy deftly balances laughter with emotional depth, drawing comparisons to classics like *You’ve Got Mail* but with a modern, poignant twist.

## Critical and Audience Reception: A Tearjerker That Strikes a Chord

Audiences and critics alike have embraced the film’s sincere storytelling and authentic performances. Many viewers report being deeply moved, with the film sparking conversations about love, loss, and connection in the digital age.

## Streaming Success: 61 Million Hours and Counting

In its second week on Netflix, *Voicemails for Isabelle* amassed 61 million hours viewed, maintaining its position as the #1 movie on the platform’s Top 10 list.

## What This Means for Netflix’s Rom-Com Strategy

The continued success of *Voicemails for Isabelle* highlights the enduring appeal of well-crafted romantic comedies on streaming platforms. Its strong viewership and emotional resonance demonstrate that audiences are eager for stories that blend humor with genuine emotional stakes. For Netflix, this film’s performance underscores the value of investing in original, heartfelt content that can capture and hold viewers’ attention beyond the initial release.
