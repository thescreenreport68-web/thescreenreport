## The Vibes Are Impeccable (And So Is the Score)

The fan response is built on a foundation of pure, unguarded joy. For many, the film served as a triumphant return to the core elements they love about [Star Wars](/movies/grogu-comes-home-fans-react-to-the-mandalorian-and-grogu-release-dates-with/). One user wrote, "I absolutely love the way Mando is introduced in the film, lurking in the shadows and from the perspective of a mouse droid. This is the way!" This kind of specific, cinematic praise is everywhere. Another viewer highlighted the tactile feel of the production, posting, "I love how they kept grogu practical you can tell by his ears they move like rubber."

The action and score were also major hits. A fan said, "I like the full action [James Bond](/movies/industry-insider-debbie-mcwilliams-says-the-next-james-bond-actor-should-be/) introduction for this two. Very Badass, and very fun. Ludwig Göransson? GOAT" The communal theater experience left a lasting impression, too. "This was awesome in the movie theater. Hearing the main theme blasting in the cinema was glorious...." one viewer recalled.

## Repeat Viewings and Rekindled Love

Perhaps the strongest indicator of the film's success is the sheer number of repeat viewings fans are reporting. This wasn't a one-and-done experience for the dedicated audience.

Most powerfully, several fans noted that the movie brought them back to Star Wars after a long hiatus. One posted, "This movie made me back as the SW fan since I saw the episode 9... After the theatre, I watched all of the episodes of Mandalorian. This is the way" Another shared an even more dramatic conversion: "I had never watched Star Wars before, but The Mandalorian made me fall in love with Star Wars. The Mandalorian & Grogu is my favorite movie of all time. I’ve already watched it seven times in just four weeks!"

Others spotted creator Dave Filoni among the rescue pilots and noted the use of stop-motion for certain droid sequences.
