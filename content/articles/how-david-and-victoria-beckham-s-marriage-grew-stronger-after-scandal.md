How do you keep a marriage together when the world thinks it's falling apart? David and Victoria Beckham are offering a rare, raw look at the crisis that nearly broke them two decades ago.

In the 2023 Netflix docuseries *Beckham*, the couple revisited the painful period after Rebecca Loos, a former member of David's management team, alleged she had an affair with the athlete. He adamantly denied it, but the scandal rocked their world.

"I don't know how we got through it, in all honesty," David said in the series, per E! News. "Victoria's everything to me. To see her hurt was incredibly difficult. But, we're fighters. And at that time, we needed to fight for each other, we needed to fight for our family. And what we had was worth fighting for."

Victoria agreed, calling it "the hardest period for us. Because it felt like the world was against us. And here's the thing: We were against each other, if I'm being completely honest."

Their marriage, which began with a lavish wedding at a castle in Ireland on July 4, 1999, just four months after Brooklyn was born, emerged stronger.

In a recent interview with ELLE, Victoria reflected on the evolution that likely saved them. "So often in relationships, one will say to the other, 'You’ve changed.' Well, of course you’ve changed!" she said. "You’re a different person when you’re 20 than when you’re 50."

Today, the Beckhams' foundation is described as "rock solid." David received a knighthood in November for his services to sport and charity, and Victoria's fashion brand continues to thrive. While their son Brooklyn has indicated a strained relationship with his parents, he was still included in their latest Father's Day tributes.

The scandal, while intensely private, became a very public test. The couple's candid reflections suggest the ordeal ultimately reinforced their commitment, proving that some bonds are indeed worth the fight.
