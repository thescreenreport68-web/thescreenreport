A new version of the 2008 film *The X-Files: I Want to Believe* is set to debut on Hulu on August 14, according to MovieWeb.

The cut, titled *The X-Files: I Want to Believe Vrach Frankenshteyn*, represents a restored director's version from franchise creator Chris Carter. He confirmed last year that he was working on this edit, which reportedly leans more into the concept's horror elements than the original theatrical release.

## What is this new version of the film?

This is not a sequel but a re-edited cut of the existing 2008 movie. The original *I Want to Believe* was a standalone "Monster-of-the-Week" story featuring FBI agents Fox Mulder and Dana Scully investigating a case involving a former priest with psychic visions. The new *Vrach Frankenshteyn* version is described as Carter's director's cut, restored to present a darker, more horror-focused take on the material.

## Why is this happening now?

The release arrives amid renewed interest in the *X-Files* franchise. A new take on the concept is in development from filmmaker Ryan Coogler, whose pilot episode has wrapped filming, though it has not yet been officially ordered to series. The debut of this lost cut on Hulu could serve to gauge audience enthusiasm for the property as the streamer considers moving forward with Coogler's project.

The original 2008 film was met with mixed reactions from critics and fans, failing to capture the widespread appeal of the 1990s television series or the 1998 film *The X-Files: Fight the Future*. This newly restored version offers a fresh perspective on a divisive chapter in the franchise's history.
