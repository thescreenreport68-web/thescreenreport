James Purefoy, Colin O’Donoghue, and Luke Roberts have joined the cast of the MGM+ series Robin Hood for its second season, according to TV Series Finale.

Purefoy will play King Henry, described as the "formidable monarch whose ruthless vision for England sets the season’s events into motion." O’Donoghue takes on the role of Prince Richard, Henry's eldest son and rightful heir, "whose fate threatens to plunge the kingdom into civil war." Roberts will appear as Amaury D’Montfort, an "ambitious Norman nobleman whose political aspirations place him at the center of Nottingham’s growing power struggle."

## Who else is in the cast?

They join the returning ensemble led by Jack Patten as Robin, alongside Lauren McQueen, Sean Bean, Lydia Peckham, Steven Waddington, and Connie Nielsen. The series follows Robin as he tries to bring peace to the people of Nottingham.

## What will happen in Season Two?

According to the network, the arrival of King Henry, Prince Richard, and Amaury D’Montfort will reshape the political landscape of the show. This forces Robin and his allies to navigate shifting loyalties, royal power struggles, and dangerous new threats. The second season promises to expand the world of the legend with new alliances, formidable enemies, and greater stakes.

The premiere date for the second season of Robin Hood will be announced later.
