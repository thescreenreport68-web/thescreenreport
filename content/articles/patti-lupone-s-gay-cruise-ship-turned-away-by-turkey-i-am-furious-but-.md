Patti LuPone revealed that an LGBTQ+ cruise ship she is performing on has been banned from entering Turkey, according to The Hollywood Reporter. The Tony Award-winning actress shared the news on Instagram.

LuPone is scheduled to perform on Virgin Voyages’ Scarlet Lady, which sets sail on July 5 through July 15. She expressed her shock and anger in a social media post.

“I am shocked. The Atlantis cruise I am performing on next week has been banned from entering Turkey,” she wrote. “A ship — a magnificent ship — full of gay men. And me. Denied entry to Turkey simply because of who is on board.”

## What did Patti LuPone say?

LuPone confirmed she will still sail. “I am furious, but I am sailing, as the ship will make other ports of call,” she wrote. “I am ready to perform for all the wonderful men on this Atlantis cruise, who deserve so much better than this.”

## How did the event organizer react?

Rich Campbell, president and CEO of Atlantis Events, told CNN he was stunned by the decision. “It’s pretty stunning, to be honest. I mean, and the reasoning behind it is that it’s a gay group,” Campbell said.

The cruise, described in a May Instagram post by Atlantis, was promoted as an “epic all-gay voyage” from Athens to Venice with stops in iconic Mediterranean destinations.
