A Final Farewell from the Stunt Crew

Johnny Knoxville and the original 'Jackass' crew suit up one last time for a final, chaotic hurrah. '[Jackass: Best and Last](/movies/jackass-best-and-last-box-office-day-25-adds-33-196-as-domestic-total-hits-18/)' serves as the fifth and concluding chapter of the franchise that redefined stunt comedy. Directed by the series' longtime architect Jeff Tremaine, this 92-minute film blends action, comedy, and documentary elements as the aging team embarks on what is promised to be their last insane crusade together.

## The Full Crew Returns for One Last Ride

The film reunites the full, iconic lineup of daredevils, with each member appearing as themselves. Johnny Knoxville, Steve-O, and Chris Pontius lead the charge, joined by Jason 'Wee Man' Acuña, Dave England, Preston Lacy, Ehren McGhehey, and the often-controversial Bam Margera. Their dynamic—a blend of camaraderie and calculated recklessness—remains the beating heart of the project, as they perform stunts that underscore both their enduring spirit and their advancing years.

A trade report has characterized the film as a 'lazy repurposing of old footage,' suggesting a creative approach that may rely heavily on the franchise's past. This final chapter aims to close the book on two decades of televised and cinematic mischief.
