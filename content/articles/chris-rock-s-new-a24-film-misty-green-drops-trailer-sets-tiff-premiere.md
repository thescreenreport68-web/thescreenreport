Chris Rock’s new film *[Misty Green](/movies/chris-rock-s-new-film-misty-green-drops-trailer-sets-toronto-premiere/)* has released its first trailer and is set for a world premiere at the Toronto International Film Festival, according to The Hollywood Reporter.

Rock serves as writer and director of the A24 feature, which centers on a Hollywood actress whose career has stalled years after her breakthrough.

## Who is in the cast?

The film features a notable ensemble. Rosalind Eleazar stars as Misty Green, with Adam Driver, Daniel Kaluuya, Anna Kendrick, and Topher Grace in supporting roles. Chris Rock and Anthony Anderson also appear.

## What does the trailer reveal?

The preview paints a portrait of a defiant but struggling artist. Misty is shown being evicted from her apartment and facing blunt industry assessments. “It’s tough out there. I don’t know if I can even get you on *Law & Order* now,” an executive played by Topher Grace tells her.

Rock’s character, seemingly a director, references past volatility: “The last time I cast you, you punched this bitch in the face.” Driver’s character appears to make her a questionable offer, while Kaluuya plays her brother, who faces his own hardships.

*Misty Green* is scheduled for its world premiere at the 2026 [Toronto International Film Festival](/movies/tiff-unveils-special-presentations-lineup-for-2026-festival/). A theatrical release is planned for October.
