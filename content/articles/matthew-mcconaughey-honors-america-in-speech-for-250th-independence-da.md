Matthew McConaughey delivered a patriotic speech in a video message posted Friday, according to a report by TheWrap. The actor addressed the nation ahead of America's 250th Independence Day celebration.

McConaughey posted a two-minute clip on social media seeking to rally fellow Americans. In the video, he explained why he believes the country is great and argued against cynicism.

## What did Matthew McConaughey say?

“We didn’t start this country on any kind of proof, we started it on a belief that was worth fighting for,” McConaughey said. “A wager that was worth betting on. An act of faith that a self-governing people could be something worth being and becoming.”

He continued, emphasizing the ongoing nature of the American experiment. “Here we are 250 years later – the bet’s still on the table, folks. We have not cashed in, and lets hope we never cash in. We need skeptics, yes we do. We do not need cynics. One cares enough to question, which we should, and the other ones already quit. We don’t need you.”

## How did he conclude the speech?

The actor finished his message on a celebratory note. “So, here we are today lighting the fire, breaking the bread, raising a glass to our family and friends, and maybe even the neighbor we don’t agree with,” he said. “Today, we’re going to let the kids run barefoot after dark, baby. That’s the celebration. Not that we’ve arrived anywhere, but to the fact we’re still on our way.”

McConaughey signed off with his signature phrase: “Here’s to more dancing in the home of the brave. Happy 250th, America. Just keep living.”

His impassioned speech arrives as larger planned celebrations for the country’s 250th birthday have reportedly encountered difficulties, including extreme heat and logistical issues.
