Angel Studios announced a sequel to 'Young Washington', titled '1776', is in the works according to Deadline. The announcement follows the historical drama's successful opening weekend, where it became the studio's highest-grossing live-action debut.

Director, producer, and co-writer Jon Erwin took to social media to reveal the Revolutionary War follow-up is already in development. This news comes on the heels of the original film's strong performance at the box office.

## How did 'Young Washington' perform?

The film performed strongly in the Mountain, Midwest, Southcentral, and Southern regions, which drove a majority of its weekend revenue.

## What are the reactions to the film?

The film received an overall CinemaScore of 'A' from audiences. Actor Chris Pratt urged his fans on social media to see it, comparing it to films like 'The Revenant' and 'Braveheart'. Brandon Purdie, EVP of Theatrical at Angel Studios, said in a statement, "Seeing audiences across the country embrace 'Young Washington' during our nation’s 250th anniversary celebration has been incredibly meaningful."

Jon Erwin, who is also Chief Creative Officer of the film's production company Wonder Project, expressed his honor in sharing the story. "The American experiment is one of the greatest origin stories in history, and George Washington’s journey through failure, perseverance, and sacrifice forged the leader who would help shape a nation," he said.

With the sequel now officially in development, the studio is moving forward with its plan to tell more American stories on film.
