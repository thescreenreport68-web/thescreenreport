Lucasfilm confirmed at Disney's 2026 Upfronts presentation that Ahsoka Season 2 has been delayed from 2026 to early 2027, according to a report by The Direct. This move leaves the 2026 [Star Wars](/tv/star-wars-kicks-off-new-era-this-august/) release slate without a single new live-action series.

It marks the first time since Disney+ launched that a calendar year will pass without a fresh live-action Star Wars show. The streaming service has been the franchise's primary storytelling home for the past six years.

## What does the 2026 Star Wars slate include?

The year is not devoid of Star Wars content. Animated series are stepping up. *Maul – Shadow Lord* debuted earlier in 2026, and the *Visions* spin-off *The Ninth Jedi* is set to stream this August on Disney+ and Hulu.

Even with these projects, the pivot away from streaming is clear. The franchise is now steering back toward theaters, a strategy made official with the May premiere of *The Mandalorian & Grogu*. Lucasfilm is hoping for a stronger reception with *Star Wars: Starfighter*, a Ryan Gosling-led film scheduled for May 2027.

## What does this mean for future Star Wars shows?

Currently, *Ahsoka* is the only live-action Star Wars series that Lucasfilm has confirmed and put into production. A mess of rumors and reports creates a fuzzy picture for anything else.

Potential projects in various stages of rumor include a live-action series from Carlton and Nick Cuse, a second season of *Obi-Wan Kenobi*, a fourth season of *The Mandalorian*, and a long-rumored Star Wars horror series. None have been officially greenlit or confirmed by the studio.

For fans of the franchise's small-screen adventures, the wait for the next major chapter just got longer.
