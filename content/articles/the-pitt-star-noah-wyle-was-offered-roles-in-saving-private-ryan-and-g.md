Wyle, best known for playing Dr. John Carter on the long-running NBC medical drama, said he was offered a part in George Clooney's film "Good Night, and Good Luck." He was also offered the part of Private Ryan in Steven Spielberg's "Saving Private Ryan." Both opportunities fell through because he could not get out of his "ER" filming schedule.

## What did Noah Wyle say about missing the roles?

On the podcast, Wyle reflected on the missed chances. "I almost am glad that I didn’t do them, because, you know, those guys made those parts," he said. He clarified that the "Saving Private Ryan" role he was offered was ultimately played by Jeremy Davies. "Matt Damon was Private Ryan. There were two. I got offered the part of Upham, which was Jeremy Davies’ part, and he was so phenomenal in that I couldn’t imagine doing anything close to what he did."

## What is Noah Wyle doing now?

Wyle currently stars as Dr. Michael 'Robby' Robinavitch in the HBO Max medical drama "The Pitt." The show's second season wrapped in April, and production on a third season began in June. "The Pitt" Season 1 won five Emmy awards in 2025, including outstanding drama series and lead actor in a drama for Wyle.
