The British Academy of Film and Television Arts has clarified that its awards are for humans only. According to *The Hollywood Reporter*, BAFTA's updated rulebook explicitly states that AI-generated performers, like the controversial Tilly Norwood, will never be eligible for a BAFTA Film Award.

## What Are the New BAFTA Rules on AI?

The academy now reserves the right to ask for more information regarding the use of AI and "the extent of human creative involvement" in submitted work. BAFTA executive director of awards and content Emma Baehr told THR the language allows the film committee to "dig into" specific uses of AI. "We’re never going to allow an AI actor [to win a BAFTA]," Baehr said. "That’s not human."

While there is no specific percentage that would disqualify a film, the focus remains on human creativity. The rules are designed to recognize "outstanding human achievement in originating, shaping and realising the final artistic outcome, regardless of the tools used."

## What Other Changes Did BAFTA Make?

Beyond AI, the academy made several other updates. The Outstanding British Film category will now feature a longlist of 20 films, up from 15, to better showcase the range of British filmmaking. The category will retain 10 nominations.

In a move to bring additional expertise to performance categories, members of the Casting Chapter will now vote alongside the Acting Chapter during the longlisting and nomination rounds. Finally, the children’s & family film category has been renamed simply "family film" to better reflect its focus on cross-generational appeal.

Entries for the 2027 BAFTA Film Awards open on July 22.
