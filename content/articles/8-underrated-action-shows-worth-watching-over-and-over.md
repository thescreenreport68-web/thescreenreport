Some of the best action television never found a massive audience, but its rewatch value is immense. These eight underrated series offer layered stories, unforgettable characters, and kinetic set pieces that only get better on a second or third viewing.

## What Makes an Action Show Rewatchable?
A great action show isn't just about the fights and explosions. It needs characters you care about, a world you want to return to, and a pacing that pulls you through the story again. The best ones hide details in the margins—a line of dialogue, a subtle character beat—that you only catch on a rewatch. The shows here all have that quality in spades.

## The 8 Most Rewatchable Underrated Action Shows

1. **Banshee**
 This Cinemax series is a masterclass in pulp storytelling. An ex-con assumes the identity of a murdered sheriff in a small Pennsylvania town, leading to a chaotic mix of crime, romance, and brutal violence. The fight choreography is raw and punishing, but the show's heart lies in the tangled relationships between its morally grey characters. You rewatch it for Antony Starr's unhinged performance and the sheer audacity of its plot twists.

2. **Alice in Borderland**
 A high-stakes Japanese survival thriller, this Netflix series drops its characters into a deserted Tokyo where they must compete in deadly games to survive. The premise is gripping, but the rewatch value comes from spotting the foreshadowing and unraveling the mysteries of the Borderland's rules. Its visual spectacle and relentless tension make it a binge-worthy experience that holds up.

3. **Strike Back**
 As the other pillar of Cinemax's early original programming alongside Banshee, this globe-trotting military drama is pure, unadulterated action. The camaraderie between its lead operatives drives the show, and the mission-of-the-week structure is perfect for jumping back into favorite episodes. It's a **comfort food of explosions and quippy one-liners**.

4. **Warrior**
 Based on the writings of Bruce Lee, this series set in 1870s San Francisco's Tong Wars blends historical drama with breathtaking martial arts. The political intrigue and complex character alliances reveal new layers upon each viewing, while the fight scenes—choreographed with a balletic ferocity—are worth revisiting for their technical brilliance alone.

5. **Into the Badlands**
 A post-apocalyptic saga where feudal barons rule and warriors called Clippers settle disputes with spectacularly stylized combat. The show's unique blend of wire-fu, swordplay, and a mythic American landscape creates a world that feels wholly original. Its visual poetry and operatic storytelling make it a rewarding rewatch.

6. **Hap and Leonard**
 This SundanceTV series is a sweaty, Southern-fried crime romp starring two best friends who stumble into trouble. The chemistry between the leads is the main attraction, full of witty banter and genuine affection. The slower-burn, character-driven plots are packed with small moments that shine brighter when you know where the story is headed.

7. **Gangs of London**
 A cinematic tour de force of urban warfare, this show depicts the power vacuum after a London crime lord's murder. The action sequences are famously elaborate, often stretching across entire episodes, and directed with a filmic grandeur that demands a second look. The intricate web of alliances and betrayals also benefits from a rewatch.

8. **The Last Kingdom**
 While often categorized as historical drama, this tale of a Saxon-born Viking warrior is propelled by frequent, visceral battlefield combat and personal duels. The sprawling saga of Alfred the Great's England is dense with political maneuvering and character evolution, making subsequent viewings essential to fully appreciate its narrative scope.

## Where Can You Watch These Shows?
Most of these series are readily available on major streaming platforms. Banshee and Strike Back can be found on Max, while Alice in Borderland is a Netflix original. Warrior and Into the Badlands are also on Max. Hap and Leonard streams on AMC+, and Gangs of London is available on AMC+ and Prime Video. The Last Kingdom is a Netflix staple. Their accessibility makes building a rewatch queue easy.
