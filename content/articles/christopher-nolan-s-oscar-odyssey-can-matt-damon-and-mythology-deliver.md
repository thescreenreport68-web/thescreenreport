[Christopher Nolan](/movies/christopher-nolan-addresses-the-odyssey-backlash-over-casting-and-modern-di/)’s new film, *The Odyssey*, has been released to critical acclaim and is being positioned as a strong contender for awards season, according to Variety. The adaptation of Homer's epic arrives as the director’s follow-up to his Oscar-winning film *Oppenheimer*.

The film pairs one of Hollywood’s most ambitious filmmakers with source material that has shaped Western storytelling for nearly three millennia. It features an ensemble stacked with some of the biggest stars on the planet and a technical approach designed to make the theatrical experience part of the conversation.

## What is the film's critical reception?

It represents the rare summer tentpole with awards ambitions built into its foundation.

## Who could be recognized for their performances?

Matt Damon, an Oscar winner for screenwriting but never for his acting, carries the film as Odysseus. Variety notes that aside from *Good Will Hunting* and *The Talented Mr. Ripley*, he has never made a stronger case for his acting to be recognized on the big stage. In the supporting categories, two-time nominee [Samantha Morton](/movies/samantha-morton-discusses-her-intense-the-odyssey-performance-as-circe/) is highlighted as a standout, potentially providing an easy call for voters.

## Why is this the perfect moment for Nolan?

Fresh off *Oppenheimer*, which delivered him his first best director Oscar and a best picture victory, Nolan enters his next campaign with unprecedented Academy goodwill. The narrative for him this season, per Variety, will be simple: rinse and repeat the last one. He takes the oldest adventure story in Western literature and gives it the full prestige treatment.
