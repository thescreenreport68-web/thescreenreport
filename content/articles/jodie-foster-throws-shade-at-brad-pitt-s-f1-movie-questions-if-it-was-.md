Is a blockbuster film the product of artificial intelligence? Jodie Foster seems to think so.

During the "Who Owns the Future of Hollywood" panel at the Aspen Festival of Ideas on June 30, the Oscar-winning actress took a thinly veiled swipe at the Oscar-nominated movie *F1*, starring Brad Pitt. According to reports from *Variety* and *Deadline*, Foster questioned if the film was created by AI.

"I don’t say this disparagingly — how could I? This movie went on to make millions of dollars," she prefaced, according to the reports. "But I look at a movie like *F1* and I’m like, '*F1* was made by AI.' Wasn’t it?"

Foster elaborated on her reasoning, stating, "I mean, the structure was exactly the structure that you would learn in school. The actors say the lines exactly the way it would be written if a computer was writing exactly what would be the right thing for that time." She added that the filmmakers "were able to dominate the technology to make something big and beautiful."

Her comments weren't a blanket condemnation of AI's role in filmmaking. Foster acknowledged the technology can be helpful in the pre-visualization stage, when directors plan scenes ahead of production. Her hope, she said, is that "filmmakers would be able to dominate AI, and never lose sight of that."

The comments stand as one veteran artist's pointed observation on where technology and traditional storytelling might be converging in modern Hollywood.
