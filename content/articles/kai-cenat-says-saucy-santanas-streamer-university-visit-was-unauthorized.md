Videos of rapper Saucy Santana at Streamer University went viral, prompting streamer Kai Cenat to address the situation.

Kai Cenat hopped on a livestream to confirm he had seen Saucy Santana on campus and that the visit was not authorized. He explained to his viewers that he had previously reached out to Lizzo's team to make it clear Streamer University was a private event, asking them to "please respect the event.".

Despite that conversation, a miscommunication led to Santana's appearance. The rapper had been on campus, seemingly filming content for his new song, 'Quiet On The Creek.' On TikTok, Santana claimed Lizzo tried to sneak him onto campus as a surprise. Viral clips later showed Santana and Lizzo chatting with Kai's security.

## Setting the Record Straight

In his livestream, Kai Cenat defended his security team and absolved Lizzo of blame, saying he believes she is innocent in the mix-up. He stood by the rules set for his event. According to The Shade Room, he reportedly said, "Whatever y’all trying to put on the security […] come on bro, it’s just not fair, because we lay good rules down."

Reactions on social media, per The Shade Room, were split. Some commenters supported Kai for setting boundaries, while others joked that Santana's surprise was anything but quiet.

The incident highlights the tension between celebrity access and the exclusivity of private influencer events. For now, Kai Cenat has made his campus rules crystal clear.
