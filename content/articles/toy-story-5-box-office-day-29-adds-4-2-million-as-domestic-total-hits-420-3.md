Woody & Buzz Face Their Toughest Foe Yet: A Tablet

Tom Hanks and Tim Allen return for one more round in the toy box in *[Toy Story 5](/movies/toy-story-5-box-office-day-27-adds-2-7-million-as-domestic-total-hits-413-3/)*, Pixar's latest animated adventure. Directed by franchise veteran Andrew Stanton, the 102-minute film sees the beloved gang facing an all-new, modern threat to playtime. When Bonnie receives a high-tech Lilypad tablet as a gift and becomes obsessed, the jobs of Woody, Buzz Lightyear, Jessie, and the rest of the toys become exponentially harder as they must go head-to-head with digital distraction.

## A Stellar Cast of Voices Old and New

The film brings back iconic voices, with Tom Hanks as the loyal cowboy Woody and Tim Allen as the space ranger Buzz Lightyear. Joan Cusack returns as the spirited cowgirl Jessie. They are joined by a roster of new characters voiced by a talented ensemble: Greta Lee as the tablet itself, Lilypad; Conan O'Brien as the know-it-all Smarty Pants; Craig Robinson as Atlas; Shelby Rabara as Snappy; and Tony Hale reprising his role as the anxious spork, Forky. This blend of classic and fresh talent brings the story to life.

## The Billion-Dollar Box Office Sprint

This performance has it on track to become the biggest film in the 31-year-old franchise's history.

## At the Box Office

Toy Story 5 has grossed $420,318,566 at the domestic box office, 29 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,400 theaters. The film added $4,200,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $894 million. It carries a reported production budget of $250 million.
