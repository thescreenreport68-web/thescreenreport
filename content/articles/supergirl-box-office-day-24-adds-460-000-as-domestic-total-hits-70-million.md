Milly Alcock takes flight as the Girl of Steel in '[Supergirl](/movies/supergirl-box-office-day-22-adds-435-000-as-domestic-total-hits-68-9-million/),' a new chapter for the DC Universe. Directed by Craig Gillespie, this 108-minute action-adventure sci-fi film follows Kara Zor-El on an epic, interstellar journey. When a ruthless adversary strikes close to home, Supergirl is forced to team up with an unlikely companion on a mission fueled by vengeance and justice.

Alcock leads a stellar cast that includes Eve Ridley as Ruthye, Matthias Schoenaerts as Krem, and Jason Momoa as the fan-favorite anti-hero Lobo. David Krumholtz and Emily Beecham play her Kryptonian parents, Zor-El and Alura In-Ze, while David Corenswet appears as Superman and Kadiff Kirwan as Bomar Vran. The film marks a major big-screen introduction for Alcock's Supergirl.

## The Box Office

This dismal debut marks the worst box office performance for any DC movie since 2004's 'Catwoman,' a notorious flop.

## At the Box Office

Supergirl has grossed $69,957,000 at the domestic box office, 24 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 1,572 theaters. The film added $460,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $120 million. It carries a reported production budget of $175 million.
