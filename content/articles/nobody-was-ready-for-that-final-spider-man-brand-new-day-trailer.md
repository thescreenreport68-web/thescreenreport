Viewers are connecting deeply with a world-weary Peter Parker while cheering the long-awaited return of a classic [Marvel](/movies/avengers-doomsday-trailer-sent-fans-into-a-frenzy-and-the-skeptics-came-out/) hero, sparking a wave of positive buzz with only a few minor notes of criticism.

## The One Line That Broke Everyone

Amidst the spectacle, one quiet moment has captured the collective imagination. Peter Parker’s simple admission, “I’m not in high school anymore,” has struck a profound chord. Fans immediately unpacked the heavy emotional baggage behind those words, seeing it as the culmination of everything Tom Holland’s [Spider-Man](/movies/spider-man-brand-new-day-has-fans-split-hulk-fears-downey-s-villain-tease-and/) has lost.

The sentiment was echoed widely, with a fan online posting, “*Okay, the 'not in highschool anymore' part is more relatable than anything else I've seen recently.*” The line serves as a stark marker of time and trauma, transforming the character from the youthful hero introduced in ‘Civil War’ into a battered adult. As one person noted, “You realize just how much he lost and how much he had to grow up.”

## The Hulk We Needed

If Peter’s line provided the heart, the re-introduction of the original, rage-fueled Hulk provided the thunderous applause. The sight of Mark Ruffalo’s green goliath back to his classic, savage self — not the cerebral Professor Hulk — sent fans into a frenzy. The excitement wasn’t just about the visual; it was layered with narrative significance.

A fan online posted, “Seeing Hulk back to his normal self genuinely made me happy 😭”

Another pointed out the fresh dynamic, writing, “Love how Spidey did not know Hulk’s true size. He has only ever encountered Professor Hulk, not OG Savage Hulk. About time we got him back!”

This detail — that Holland’s Spider-Man has never met this version of the character — added a new layer of anticipation for their team-up. The sheer joy was palpable, with one user celebrating, “Ladies and Gentlemen, It has 8 years 1 month and 22 days since we saw Hulk. Now we're back Baby!!” The return was hailed as a major win, with another simply stating, “Hulk being back to his normal self is the greatest thing in my life.”

## Still Got the Quips

Fans are thrilled that even in what appears to be his darkest hour, Peter Parker hasn’t lost his signature wit. The trailer’s ending, which shows him stretching and quipping before a fight, was highlighted as a perfect character beat.

“This is the darkest his life has been and he’s still joking around and shit. THIS is Spider-Man,” one viewer said.

Another elaborated, “I really like the ending where Peter still quips, stretching before he fights. I’m glad that despite this is literally the worst he’s ever been in life he still has the heart to joke around.”

This balance of pain and humor defines the hero for his fans. One post perfectly summarized the appeal: “Peter is making stupid jokes, his life is falling apart and he's making stupid jokes. That’s my Peter Parker.” The pre-fight stretch itself was singled out as a quintessential move, with a fan noting, “Peter Parker stopping to stretch is the most Spider-Man pre fight move he could have pulled.”

## Not Everyone Is Buying the Angst

While the response is overwhelmingly positive, a minor undercurrent of criticism focused on two points: the cyclical nature of Peter’s pain and the film’s theatrical format. A handful of fans expressed weariness with the hero’s perpetual suffering.

One user posted succinctly, “Spider have 3 life circles. 1 Suffering 2 Suffering 3 Suffering.”

Separately, a more specific logistical disappointment emerged regarding the film’s release. Not only that, Spider-Man has been forced to the gimmick format ScreenX which barely exists in UK.”

The trailer’s success lies in its dual appeal: it offers rewarding nostalgia for long-time Marvel fans with Hulk’s return, while simultaneously advancing Peter Parker’s story in a mature, emotionally honest way. It acknowledges the weight of the character’s history — the graves, the losses, the sacrifice — without stripping away the core traits that make him beloved. It promises a hero who is weathered but not broken, facing monumental threats with a quip and a stretch. One thing is certain: nobody is done talking about this.
