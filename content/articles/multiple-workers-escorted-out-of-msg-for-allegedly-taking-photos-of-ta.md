How tight was security for Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce’s wedding at Madison Square Garden? Tight enough that multiple workers were reportedly escorted out for allegedly trying to take pictures.

Page Six reports at least four union workers were escorted out of the arena on Thursday night, the night before the July 3 wedding. One source told the outlet two stagehands "were escorted out Thursday night for taking pictures." Another source said the number was actually four. The workers were not fired, as they are union employees. "Since they work for the union, and not MSG, they would have to go to arbitration before they were fired," a source clarified. Instead, they face sanctions. "They can’t work at the Garden anymore," the source explained, "but they could work at other places [within the Madison Square Garden conglomerate]."

This incident highlights the extreme measures taken to protect the couple's privacy. Security had been extremely tight around the Garden for days leading up to the nuptials. Earlier in the week, Page Six exclusively reported that Madison Square Garden’s corporate employees were banned from the arena. "MSG is off limits to all corporate employees this week," a source said, adding employees "were not given a reason why it’s a no-go zone."

Many of the 1,000 guests for the festivities were also required to sign non-disclosure agreements, prohibiting them from sharing details. However, it seems not all guests signed. According to a Page Six source, "Their closest friends and family haven’t been asked to sign NDAs. Protecting their privacy is just something everyone in their inner circle has always naturally done."

The wedding reportedly took place on July 3 at Madison Square Garden. So far, very few details have trickled out about the starry night. A spokesperson for MSG did not respond to Page Six’s request for comment on the worker incident.
