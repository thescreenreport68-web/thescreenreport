A Killer Return for the Core Four

More than two decades after first dodging a masked maniac, the original gang is back for another round of cinematic carnage in *[Scary Movie](/movies/scary-movie-box-office-day-45-adds-74-000-as-domestic-total-hits-108-million/) (2026)*. Directed by Michael Tiddes, this 96-minute horror-comedy sequel sees the 'Core Four' once again in the crosshairs of a very familiar killer, this time taking aim at the entire landscape of modern horror movie intellectual property. No franchise is safe from its irreverent, slapstick spoofing.

## The Spoof Squad Reassembles

The film's success is powered by the return of its iconic ensemble. Marlon Wayans pulls multiple duties as the scene-stealing Shorty (and several other disguises), while Shawn Wayans returns as the hapless Ray. Anna Faris and Regina Hall reprise their roles as the perpetually screaming Cindy and the sharp-tongued Brenda, respectively. They're joined by Damon Wayans Jr. as Agent Underwood, Gregg Wayans as Brad, Kim Wayans as Nurse Ratchett, and Benny Zielke as Jess, creating a chaotic blend of characters ready for anything.

## A Box Office Horror Story (For the Competition)

The **Scary Movie 2026 box office performance** is the stuff of studio dreams. It continues to play in 225 theaters, demonstrating impressive staying power and audience demand long after its opening.

The combination of a beloved cast, a proven spoof formula, and minuscule financial risk has resulted in a comedy-horror hit with remarkably strong legs.

## At the Box Office

Scary Movie has grossed $108,010,081 at the domestic box office, 46 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 225 theaters. The film added $32,282 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $230 million. It carries a reported production budget of $30 million.
