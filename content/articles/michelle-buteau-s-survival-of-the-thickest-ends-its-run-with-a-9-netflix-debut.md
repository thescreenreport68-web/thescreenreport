Michelle Buteau’s comedy-drama series Survival of the Thickest follows Mavis Beaumont, a Black, plus-size woman navigating life after a major breakup. Determined not just to survive but to thrive, Mavis rebuilds her life with the support of her chosen family, embracing body positivity and self-love. The show blends humor and heartfelt moments across its 27-minute episodes, offering a fresh, inclusive take on love, career, and identity.

## Who’s Behind the Series?  
Michelle Buteau leads the cast as Mavis Beaumont, joined by Danielle Sanchez-Witzel, Marouane Zotti, Wanda Sykes, D.L. Hughley, Ashley Graham, Ronny Chieng, and Ice-T in guest roles. Both Buteau and Sanchez-Witzel make their directorial debuts this season, adding a personal touch to the show’s final chapter. The series is celebrated for its authentic representation of Black, trans, and queer communities, making it a standout in contemporary streaming content.

## Netflix Viewership Numbers and Ranking  
The third and final season of Survival of the Thickest debuted at #9 on Netflix’s Top 10 chart, with 5.6 million hours viewed in its first week. This marks its first week in the Top 10, signaling modest but steady interest. While not a breakout hit, the series’ presence in the lower half of the chart reflects a dedicated audience tuning in for the show’s heartfelt conclusion.

Audiences appreciate the show’s unwavering commitment to body positivity and joy within Black, trans, and queer communities. The final season deepens Mavis’s journey, exploring her relationships, career challenges, and personal growth with honesty and humor. The show’s authentic New York City setting and diverse cast contribute to its cultural relevance and emotional impact.

With its third season now streaming, Survival of the Thickest closes out with a meaningful, if modest, Netflix presence, leaving a lasting impression on viewers who value representation and heart in their comedy-drama.
