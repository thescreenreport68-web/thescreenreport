Chloe Fineman has been cast as a series regular in the upcoming Netflix show *[Myron Bolitar](/tv/colin-woodell-kj-apa-diane-guerrero-to-star-in-netflix-s-myron-bolitar/)*, according to *The Hollywood Reporter*. This marks her first major television role since announcing her departure from *Saturday Night Live*.

Fineman will play Parker Quinn, a sports reporter for a top publication described as a dogged reporter with a passion for her beat. The show is an adaptation of Harlan Coben’s thriller series, which follows an NBA star-turned-sports agent.

## Who else is in the cast?

Netflix announced Fineman's casting alongside fellow series regulars Jabari Banks and Jamie McShane. Ben McKenzie will appear as a recurring guest star. They join a cast led by Colin Woodell as Myron Bolitar, with KJ Apa and Diane Guerrero also starring.

## Who is making the show?

The series will be co-showrun, written, and executive produced by TV veteran David E. Kelley and Kyle Long. Harlan Coben also serves as an executive producer. Veteran director Greg Yaitanes will executive produce and direct several episodes.

Fineman announced her exit from *Saturday Night Live* after seven seasons. In an Instagram post, she said, "It’s really hard to leave SNL but it does feel like the right time. I’m going to miss it a lot. But the people who work there are my family and that place is my home, and I know I’ll never be too far away."
