William Franklyn-Miller leads the cast of '[Young Washington](/movies/young-washington-box-office-day-17-adds-1-million-as-domestic-total-hits-41/),' portraying the formative years of America's first president in a historical epic from Angel Studios. The film explores the crucible of George Washington's youth, before he led a nation, when he was a young soldier thrown into a global conflict. Directed by Jon Erwin, this 125-minute war drama follows Washington as alliances crumble and war closes in, forcing him to choose who to trust and confront the leader he is destined to become.

The ensemble cast brings this pivotal era to life. William Franklyn-Miller stars as George Washington, with Ben Kingsley playing Virginia's Lieutenant Governor, Robert Dinwiddie, and Mary-Louise Parker as Washington's mother, Mary. Leo Hanna appears as frontiersman Christopher Gist, while Mia Rodgers plays Sally Cary. Joel Smallbone portrays colonial advisor William Fairfax, Kelsey Grammer is Lord Fairfax, and Andy Serkis takes on the role of British General Edward Braddock. It is a sweeping origin story of a legendary figure.

## The Box Office Performance

Its opening was roughly double the genre average, signaling strong audience interest.

The **Young Washington box office performance** has firmly established it as a major win for the studio, demonstrating the potent draw of a well-crafted historical drama with a fresh-faced star at its center.

## At the Box Office

Young Washington has grossed $41,350,443 at the domestic box office, 18 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 2,275 theaters. The film added $433,123 in its most recent day of release. It carries a reported production budget of $20 million.
