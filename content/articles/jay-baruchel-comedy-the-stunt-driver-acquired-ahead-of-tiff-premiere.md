The comedy film *The Stunt Driver* has been acquired by Independent Film Company ahead of its world premiere at the Toronto Film Festival, according to Deadline. The company will release the film in U.S. theaters on September 25.

Directed by Michael Dowse, the film tells the true story of Canadian daredevil Ken Carter, played by Jay Baruchel. In the mid-1970s, Carter, a world champion stunt driver, attempts the impossible: a mile-long jump across the St. Lawrence River in a rocket car.

## What is the cast of The Stunt Driver?

The film features a notable ensemble. Ben Foster plays the iconic Evel Knievel. The cast also includes Ed Helms, Laurence Leboeuf, and Dan Bakkedahl.

## What is the film's creative vision?

Director Michael Dowse, who previously worked with Baruchel on *Goon*, was drawn to the human story behind the spectacle. "What captivated me wasn’t simply the spectacle of Ken Carter’s legendary jump, but the humanity behind it," Dowse told Deadline. "He was a dreamer who refused to let the world define what was possible."

Ayo Kepher Maat of Independent Film Company expressed enthusiasm for the project. "We’re thrilled to collaborate with Michael Dowse to bring the incredible true story of stunt driver Ken Carter to the big screen," Maat said. "Jay Baruchel delivers an unforgettable performance, joined by a remarkable cast."

*The Stunt Driver* is produced by Jennifer Wilson and Dowse for Film Cardinal, with executive producers including Baruchel. The deal was negotiated by Maat on behalf of Independent Film Company with UTA representing the filmmakers.
