The conversation online quickly became a celebration of the song's 90s-inspired music video, which viewers are calling a masterclass in style, performance, and nostalgic homage.

The near-universal praise zeroes in on the video's artistic direction. Fans aren't just liking it — they're dissecting every frame and calling it a career milestone. There's a strong sense that Monét has fully stepped into a lane that's all her own. The sentiment is best captured as "Victoria.. I need you to understand that this lane is YOURS. we need you. hits, looks, dance numbers, out of this world choreography serving it like you. STEP OUT. you're really it!!!". That feeling of arrival and mastery pops up everywhere, with another reaction echoing "THIS IS WHAT WE BEEN WAITING FOR. Whenever she gets in her uptempo, dance bag, its a WRAP. A MASTERFUL song. Production and visually, 10s across the board. Lets gooo Victoria. SO PROUD!".

## The 90s Aesthetic That's Stealing the Show

That 90s flashback is the star of the show. Fans aren't just saying it's nostalgic — they're calling out specific references and appreciating the refined throwback. The sepia tones, styling, and that demure yet powerful energy are hitting exactly the right notes. One fan summed it up as "The coloring of the visual also reminds me of MJ’s In the Closet and Janet’s Love Will Never Do Without You…. 😍😍😍😍". The comparisons to Michael and Janet Jackson are no casual thing — it's fans recognizing Monét is operating at that level of performance and storytelling. Others simply soaked in the vibe, with reactions like "Very 90s,very demure. Even the sepia tones! Love it!" and "The video, the choreography, and the vibes are giving a 90s flair to the fullest, and I'm loving every bit of it.".

## Dance as the Beating Heart of the Video

If the 90s look sets the scene, the choreography is the pulse of the praise. Fans are applauding not just the moves, but Monét's dedication to spotlighting top-tier talent and how smoothly the dance fits into the video. The excitement even went as far as shouting out specific contributors, showing the community spirit — one fan recognized "Omg Danielle Polanco was one of the voguers. She's such a master dancer and choreographer! 😢 🎉❤💐💐💐💐". That attention to detail shows just how much fans appreciate the craft. The choreography is viewed as key to the video's impact, pushing it past a simple performance clip into serious artistry. One post nailed it with "This is what serious artistry looks and sounds like.", and another added "This is how you make a sexy music video without abandoning sophistication. This deserves a Grammy.". The takeaway is clear: the blend of visual style and strong dance makes for a full, elevated package.

## The One Dissenting Voice (That's Still a Compliment)

Amid all the praise, there's one more measured take that adds some depth to the reaction. It's not a critique of quality, but of category — suggesting the video's artistry might overshadow the song as a standalone radio hit. One viewer offered "The artistry trumps the sound. I doubt I’ll blast the song if it plays on the radio, but wouldn’t mind rewatching the video.". That take is interesting because it still praises the video's artistry while questioning the song's hookiness. It highlights Monét's strength as a visual album artist, where the full experience is what's on offer. Most fans, though, aren't bothered by that. One summed it up as "She doesn't miss. Everything is always added to the Playlist immediately.".

Now all eyes are on the full 'Frequency of Love' album. If "Reach Out" is the opening statement, fans are convinced they're watching Monét in her defining era — one built on confident throwback elegance, stunning performance, and serious, Grammy-worthy artistry. And the discourse isn't cooling off any time soon.
