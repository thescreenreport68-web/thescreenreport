Rory Scovel will premiere a new stand-up special, *Show Must Go On*, on Netflix this August, according to Deadline. The global release is set for Tuesday, August 11.

The comedian returns to the streaming giant for his first new hour on the platform since 2017's *Rory Scovel Tries Stand-Up for the First Time*. The new special was filmed at The Neptune theater in Seattle, Washington.

## What is the new special about?

Details on the material Scovel covers in *Show Must Go On* have not been revealed. The special was directed by Scott Moran. Producers include Scovel, Alex Bach and Daniel Powell of Irony Point, Molly Mandel, and Matt Schuler.

## What else has Rory Scovel done?

Scovel's past comedy specials include *The Charleston Special* (2015), *Live Without Fear* (2021), and *Religion, Sex, and a Few Things In Between* (2024) for Max. He has built a parallel career as an actor, with credits on HBO's *Rooster*, Apple TV's *Physical*, and in Damien Chazelle's *Babylon*. He recently appeared in an episode of the HBO Max series *Life, Larry and the Pursuit of Unhappiness: An Almost History of America* and will be seen in the upcoming Amazon MGM comedy *Judgment Day* starring Will Ferrell and Zac Efron.

Previously, Scovel co-created and starred in the Comedy Central series *Robbie* and wrote for multiple seasons of *The Eric Andre Show*.
