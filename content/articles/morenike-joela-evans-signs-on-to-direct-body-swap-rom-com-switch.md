Veteran TV director Morenike Joela Evans has signed on to direct the feature romantic comedy "Switch," according to Deadline. Alexis Louder is attached to star in and produce the film.

Evans brings a wealth of television experience to the project, with directing credits that include episodes of "Grey’s Anatomy," "iCarly," and "How I Met Your Father."

## What is the plot of 'Switch'?

The film is described as a body-swap rom-com centered on a Black woman and a white man who have to walk a week in each other’s lives. Louder will play Imani, a Black elementary school teacher who butts heads with Ben, a white executive. The two wake up in each other's bodies after a mysterious escape room adventure.

According to a synopsis, the pair are forced to work together to find a way to switch back, overcoming assumptions about each other and learning from each other's perspective. The story suggests they may fall in love in the process.

## Who is behind the film?

Bryan McClure, known for his work on "The Walking Dead" and "The Summer I Turned Pretty," originated the premise and brought the concept to Louder and scriptwriter Alix Reeves for development.

In a statement, McClure expressed his enthusiasm for Evans joining the project. "I couldn’t have imagined a better fit than Morenike Joela Evans—she was a true answer to prayer," he said. "We’re thrilled to have her on our team, bringing her vast experience and impressive skill set to help bring our story to life."

Louder also shared her excitement about the reunion with Evans, stating, "It’s an honor to reunite with Morenike after all these years! She is an excellent and well-respected director across mediums, and we’re lucky to have her." She added, "I’m excited for what she will bring to SWITCH. It is a needed, heartfelt romcom. Heavy on the ‘com’ and sincere with the ‘rom’."

The two previously worked together on the 2017 TV One film "Deadly Dispatch."
