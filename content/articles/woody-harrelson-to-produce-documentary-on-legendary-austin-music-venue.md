Woody Harrelson is attached as a producer on a new documentary about the legendary Austin music venue Armadillo World Headquarters, according to Deadline. The Texas native and Academy Award nominee is expanding his connection to the city's creative community with the project.

The feature-length documentary is currently in development. It is set to chronicle the venue's rise, cultural impact, and enduring legacy.

## What will the documentary feature?

The film is expected to feature archival footage from a restored archive of more than 100 audio and video recordings captured inside Armadillo World Headquarters. This could include snippets of performances from iconic artists like Willie Nelson, Bruce Springsteen, and Talking Heads. Audiences may get a peek at Springsteen’s show on his first Texas tour, as well as sets by Fats Domino and Waylon Jennings.

Harrelson explained his personal connection to the project in a statement. “The Armadillo wasn’t just a music venue,” he said. “It was where all kinds of worlds came together: artists, outlaws, hippies, students, dreamers, everyone under one roof. Careers got started there, genres mixed, and what really sticks with me is how it made people feel like they belonged.”

## Who else is involved?

The documentary is directed and edited by David Brodie. It is produced by Angus Wall, Eve Kornblum, Kent Kubena, and Terry Leonard of MakeMake, along with Marcus Peterzell and Amy Slotnick of Passion Point Collective. Harrelson’s producing partners Jeremy Plager and Ilya Bronchtein will executive produce.

Through recordings and firsthand accounts, the film will trace how the venue grounded the “cosmic cowboy” movement. It served as a cultural crossroads where outlaw country, rock, blues, and counterculture converged, helping cement Austin’s identity as the Live Music Capital of the World. Additional production partners are expected to be announced.
