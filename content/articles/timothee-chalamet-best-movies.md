Here is the verdict up front, no teasing: the best **Timothée Chalamet movie** is still *Call Me by Your Name*. Eight years and two more Oscar nominations later, his 2017 breakout remains his finest, most critically acclaimed performance — the role that made him, at 22, the third-youngest Best Actor nominee in Academy history. That is the headline of this ranking of the best **Timothée Chalamet movies ranked** from his essential work down: the quiet indie romance still beats the blockbusters. Not *Dune: Part Two*, his biggest hit. Not his full-body Bob Dylan transformation. The breakthrough wins.

The real tension is what sits behind it. Chalamet is a three-time Best Actor nominee who has never won, and his recent run — the Dylan biopic *A Complete Unknown* and the buzzy A24 sports drama *Marty Supreme* — has narrowed the gap to a sliver. Below, we rank every major film, weighing his performance, the movie's quality, and its place in a fast-rising career.

## Timothée Chalamet's best movies, ranked

1. **Call Me by Your Name (2017)** — 95% on Rotten Tomatoes. His breakout and still his most devastating work.
2. **Dune: Part Two (2024)** — 92%. The blockbuster that proved he could carry an epic.
3. **A Complete Unknown (2024)** — 82%. A full-body transformation into Bob Dylan.
4. **Marty Supreme (2025)** — 93%. A go-for-broke A24 sports drama and his third Oscar nod.
5. **Lady Bird (2017)** — 99%. A sharp, funny supporting turn in a modern classic.
6. **Little Women (2019)** — 95%. A warm, romantic Laurie opposite a stacked cast.
7. **Dune (2021)** — 83%. His entry into Denis Villeneuve's sci-fi saga.
8. **Beautiful Boy (2018)** — 67%. A wrenching addiction drama.
9. **Bones and All (2022)** — 82%. A tender, gruesome road-trip horror romance.
10. **Wonka (2023)** — 82%. Proof he can carry a family-friendly musical.
11. **The King (2019)** — 71%. A brooding, underrated medieval lead.

That is the short version for anyone scanning **Timothée Chalamet movies ranked worst to best** — flip the list and *The King* anchors the bottom of the films worth your time. Now the why.

## 1. Call Me by Your Name — the best Timothée Chalamet performance

Luca Guadagnino's sun-drenched romance is where the legend begins. As Elio, a teenager falling for an older summer guest (Armie Hammer) in 1980s Italy, Chalamet delivers a performance so raw and unguarded that the film's final, lingering close-up became instantly iconic. The role earned him his first Academy Award nomination for Best Actor — making him, at 22, the third-youngest nominee the category has ever seen.

This is the **best Timothée Chalamet performance**, full stop, and the consensus pick across critics and most **Reddit** threads. It is the purest distillation of what makes him special: a willingness to let the camera sit on real, unflattering emotion without flinching. If you watch only one of his films, make it this one.

## 2. Dune: Part Two — the blockbuster era

If *Call Me by Your Name* made him a critics' darling, *Dune: Part Two* made him a movie star. Reprising Paul Atreides, Chalamet shoulders one of the decade's most ambitious sci-fi epics and turns Paul's slide toward dangerous, messianic power into something genuinely chilling. It is a different muscle entirely — commanding, physical, operatic.

It is also his biggest blockbuster and a high point of modern science fiction; for more in that register, see our [best sci-fi movies streaming](/streaming/best-sci-fi-movies-streaming) guide and our [Dune: Part Two review](/reviews/dune-part-two-review). To trace how this one fits its director's run, our [Denis Villeneuve movies ranked](/movies/denis-villeneuve-movies-ranked) breakdown puts it in context. Essential big-screen filmmaking — and Chalamet is its beating heart.

## 3. A Complete Unknown — becoming Bob Dylan

James Mangold's biopic could have been a karaoke act. Instead, Chalamet disappears into a young Bob Dylan, performing the songs himself — guitar, harmonica, that unmistakable nasal drawl — across the singer's mid-'60s electric pivot. The commitment is staggering, and it earned him his second Best Actor Oscar nomination, making him the youngest two-time Best Actor nominee since James Dean. He won the SAG Award for it but lost the 2025 Oscar to Adrien Brody.

The 82% Tomatometer undersells one of his **best acting** showcases: he never feels like he is imitating Dylan; he is inhabiting him.

## 4. Marty Supreme — the A24 swing that nearly tops Dylan

Here is where the ranking gets contentious. Chalamet leads Josh Safdie's frenetic period drama as Marty, a motormouthed table-tennis hustler chasing greatness in 1950s New York — loosely inspired by real champion Marty Reisman. It is a maximalist, sweaty performance that earned him a **third** Best Actor nomination at the 2026 Oscars, making him the youngest male performer ever to reach that milestone.

At 93% on Rotten Tomatoes, it is the **best-rated** of his lead vehicles — higher than both Dune films and the Dylan biopic. For more in the studio's sensibility, browse our [best A24 movies ranked](/movies/best-a24-movies-ranked) and our [best heist movies](/movies/best-heist-movies) for kindred high-stakes hustlers. It does not quite unseat *Call Me by Your Name*, but it is the closest a non-romance has come.

## 5–7. Lady Bird, Little Women, and Dune

Before he carried epics, Chalamet stole scenes. In Greta Gerwig's **Lady Bird** (99% on Rotten Tomatoes — his highest-scoring film overall), he plays Kyle, the too-cool, vaguely insufferable teen crush, in a masterclass of comic specificity. It kicked off a fruitful run with Gerwig that continued in **Little Women** (95%), where his Laurie is all easy warmth and heartbreak, surrounded by the new Hollywood class — much like [the company Zendaya keeps on screen](/celebrity/zendaya-movies-and-tv-shows).

The first **Dune** (83%) is more setup than payoff, but Chalamet grounds its world-building with a watchful, internal turn. Watch it first — *Part Two* hits twice as hard once you have sat with Paul's beginnings.

## 8–11. The deeper cuts worth watching

Two dramas show his range at full stretch. In **Beautiful Boy**, he plays a young man wrestling with addiction opposite Steve Carell, in a performance of unbearable tenderness. In Guadagnino's **Bones and All**, he and Taylor Russell turn a cannibal road-trip horror into an aching romance — a true cult entry among his **best roles**.

Then the lighter swings: **Wonka** is a sweet, song-filled origin story that proved he could anchor a family hit, and **The King** is an underrated Netflix medieval drama in which he plays a brooding young Henry V. Together they prove the breadth of his appeal — whimsy, warmth, and grim historical weight, often within the same calendar year.

## The bottom line

The best **Timothée Chalamet movies ranked** map a remarkable trajectory: from the aching intimacy of *Call Me by Your Name* to the planetary scale of *Dune: Part Two*, with a Bob Dylan transformation and a *Marty Supreme* swing closing fast behind. He is a three-time Oscar nominee who still has not won — a streak that only sharpens the anticipation. The blockbusters got bigger and the scores climbed higher, but the breakthrough still beats them all. For now, *Call Me by Your Name* holds the top spot — and the ranking is far from finished.
