Few actors have climbed as fast, or as deliberately, as Timothée Chalamet. In under a decade he went from a scene-stealing teenager to a three-time Best Actor Oscar nominee and a bankable franchise lead — the rare star who can open a blockbuster and anchor a whisper-quiet indie in the same year. If you are looking for the best **Timothée Chalamet movies** to watch, the short answer is this: start with *Call Me by Your Name*, *Dune: Part Two*, and *A Complete Unknown*, then work outward. Below, we rank his essential films and tell you exactly where each one sits.

## The best Timothée Chalamet movies, ranked

Here is our ranking, weighing his performance, each film's quality, and its place in his career:

1. **Call Me by Your Name (2017)** — His breakout and still his most quietly devastating work.
2. **Dune: Part Two (2024)** — The blockbuster that proved he could carry an epic.
3. **A Complete Unknown (2024)** — A full-body transformation into Bob Dylan.
4. **Lady Bird (2017)** — A sharp, funny supporting turn in a modern classic.
5. **Dune (2021)** — His entry into Denis Villeneuve's sci-fi saga.
6. **Marty Supreme (2025)** — A go-for-broke A24 sports drama.
7. **Beautiful Boy (2018)** — A wrenching, deeply felt addiction drama.
8. **Little Women (2019)** — A warm, romantic Laurie opposite a stacked cast.
9. **Wonka (2023)** — Proof he can carry a family-friendly musical.
10. **The King (2019)** — A brooding, underrated medieval lead.

## 1. Call Me by Your Name — the breakout that started it all

Luca Guadagnino's sun-drenched romance is where the legend begins. As Elio, a teenager falling for an older summer guest in 1980s Italy, Chalamet delivers a performance so raw and unguarded that the film's final, lingering shot became instantly iconic. The role earned him his first Academy Award nomination for Best Actor at just 22 — one of the youngest nominees the category has ever seen. It remains the purest distillation of what makes him special: a willingness to let the camera sit on real, unflattering emotion. If you watch only one Chalamet film, make it this one.

## 2. Dune: Part Two — the blockbuster era

If *Call Me by Your Name* made him a critics' darling, *Dune: Part Two* made him a movie star. Reprising Paul Atreides, Chalamet shoulders one of the most ambitious sci-fi epics of the decade and turns Paul's slide toward dangerous, messianic power into something genuinely chilling. It is a different muscle entirely — commanding, physical, operatic — and he flexes it beautifully. The film is also a high point of modern science fiction; for more of that, see our roundup of the [best sci-fi movies streaming](/streaming/best-sci-fi-movies-streaming). Denis Villeneuve's two-part adaptation is essential big-screen filmmaking, and Chalamet is its beating heart.

## 3. A Complete Unknown — becoming Bob Dylan

James Mangold's biopic could have been a karaoke act. Instead, Chalamet disappears into a young Bob Dylan, performing the songs himself — guitar, harmonica, that unmistakable nasal drawl — across the singer's restless rise and his mid-'60s electric pivot. The commitment is staggering, and it earned him his second Best Actor Oscar nomination. It is the kind of immersive transformation that signals an actor moving from "promising" to "great." The film leans on his voice and stage presence, and he never once feels like he is imitating; he is inhabiting.

## 4. Lady Bird — the perfect supporting turn

Before he was carrying epics, Chalamet was stealing scenes. In Greta Gerwig's beloved coming-of-age film, he plays Kyle, the too-cool, vaguely insufferable teen crush — and he is very funny. It is a small role, but it is a masterclass in comic specificity, and it kicked off a fruitful run of work with Gerwig. *Lady Bird* is also a cornerstone of A24's catalog; if you love that sensibility, browse our list of the [best A24 movies ranked](/movies/best-a24-movies-ranked).

## 5. Dune — entering the saga

The first *Dune* is necessarily more setup than payoff, but Chalamet grounds its world-building with a watchful, internal performance as a reluctant heir. It is the film that introduced mainstream audiences to his range beyond indie drama, and it set the table for the sequel's fireworks. Watch it first — *Part Two* hits twice as hard once you have sat with Paul's beginnings.

## 6. Marty Supreme — the A24 swing

Reuniting with the indie world, Chalamet leads Josh Safdie's frenetic period drama as Marty, a hungry, motormouthed table-tennis hustler chasing greatness in 1950s New York — a character loosely inspired by real-life champion Marty Reisman. It is a maximalist, sweaty, go-for-broke performance that earned him a third Best Actor nomination at the 2026 Oscars, making him the youngest male performer to reach that milestone. Safdie's first solo feature runs on the same anxious energy and forward momentum as his earlier collaborations.

### The indie dramas worth your time

Two earlier films show his dramatic range. In **Beautiful Boy**, he plays a young man wrestling with addiction opposite Steve Carell, in a performance of unbearable tenderness that announced he could shatter you, not just charm you. And in Greta Gerwig's **Little Women**, his Laurie is all easy warmth and heartbreak — a reminder that he is one of the most natural romantic leads of his generation, here surrounded by a deep ensemble of the new Hollywood class, like [the company Zendaya keeps on screen](/celebrity/zendaya-movies-and-tv-shows).

## The lighter side: Wonka and The King

Not every great Chalamet vehicle is awards bait. **Wonka** (2023) is a sweet, song-filled origin story that proved he could anchor a family hit and still bring genuine sincerity to a role that could have been pure confection. It was a sizable commercial success and showed off a softer, more playful register. **The King** (2019), meanwhile, is an underrated Netflix medieval drama in which he plays a young Henry V with brooding restraint — a quieter, moodier showcase that rewards a second look. Together they prove the breadth of his appeal: he can sell whimsy, warmth, and grim historical weight, often within the same calendar year.

## Where to watch Timothée Chalamet's movies

His catalog is scattered across platforms, so a quick guide helps:

- **Dune** and **Dune: Part Two** — have streamed on HBO Max and are available for digital rental.
- **A Complete Unknown** — has streamed on Hulu and Disney+ following its theatrical run.
- **Wonka** — available across major streamers and rental services.
- **The King** — on Netflix.
- **Call Me by Your Name**, **Lady Bird**, **Little Women**, and **Beautiful Boy** — rotate between subscription services and digital rental, so check your platforms.

Availability shifts by region and over time, so confirm before you settle in. For tracking down other big titles the same way, our guide to [where to watch the Harry Potter movies](/streaming/where-to-watch-harry-potter-movies) breaks down the same rent-versus-stream logic.

## The bottom line

The best **Timothée Chalamet movies** map a remarkable trajectory: from the aching intimacy of *Call Me by Your Name* to the planetary scale of *Dune: Part Two*, with a Bob Dylan transformation and a string of indie swings in between. He is now a three-time Oscar nominee who still has not won — a streak that only sharpens the anticipation around whatever he does next. With major directors lining up and his appetite for risk showing no signs of slowing, the most exciting entry in his filmography may not have arrived yet. Keep an eye on the release calendar; the Chalamet ranking is far from finished.
