According to Kotaku, the film is a test for a potential full-length feature using the same technology.

## What is the film about?

The story, allegedly inspired by the book *Echopraxia* by Peter Watts, follows a woman on a U.S. military operation in Syria whose helicopter crashes. She is then implanted with a device that turns her into a "killer zombie" forced to fight others. The plot is framed in a rough documentary style, but the reviewer found it derivative and lacking in interesting ideas.

## Why is the film being criticized?

"It is a waste of your time," the critic wrote.

The short film "Nightborne" serves as his first full test.

## What is Blomkamp known for?

Neill Blomkamp is best known for directing the 2009 sci-fi hit *District 9*, which was nominated for four Academy Awards. The film, starring Sharlto Copley, is a critically praised allegory about xenophobia and has a 90% critics score on Rotten Tomatoes. His subsequent work includes *Elysium* and *Gran Turismo*.
