Elon Musk has vowed to create an AI-generated film adaptation of Homer's epic poem "[The Odyssey](/movies/christopher-nolan-s-oscar-odyssey-can-matt-damon-and-mythology-deliver/)" by the end of 2026, according to a report from TheWrap. The tech mogul made the announcement on his social media platform X.

## What did Elon Musk announce?

In a post on Tuesday evening, Musk declared his intention for his AI tool, Grok Imagine, to produce a full-length movie. "Before this year ends, Grok Imagine will make a full-length movie of 'The Odyssey' that is historically accurate and true to the art of Homer," Musk wrote. He did not provide further details on the venture.

## Why is Musk making his own 'Odyssey'?

Musk's announcement follows his repeated public criticism of [Christopher Nolan](/movies/christopher-nolan-addresses-the-odyssey-backlash-over-casting-and-modern-di/)'s live-action adaptation of "The Odyssey." The film, which stars Matt Damon, Tom Holland, and Anne Hathaway, was released in July 2026. Musk has previously taken issue with the casting choices in Nolan's version, specifically criticizing the casting of Lupita Nyong'o as Helen of Troy.

## Is a live-action version also planned?

In a separate post, Musk responded to a fan's suggestion about a live-action project. He stated he was "down" with the idea of giving director [Mel Gibson](/movies/passion-of-the-christ-set-for-theatrical-return/) a significant budget to create a painstakingly historically accurate adaptation, with dialogue taken directly from the original poem in Homeric Greek.

## How has Nolan's 'Odyssey' performed?

It has also received critical acclaim, with TheWrap's critic calling it Nolan's "best" film.
