A New Chapter for Woody and Buzz

In "[Toy Story 5](/movies/toy-story-5-box-office-day-32-adds-1-7-million-as-domestic-total-hits-431-6/)," the gang faces their most modern challenge yet. When Bonnie receives a Lilypad tablet as a gift and becomes obsessed with the digital world, the classic toys find their jobs threatened. Woody, Buzz Lightyear, Jessie, and the rest must go head-to-head with this all-new threat to playtime in a 102-minute animated adventure that blends comedy, family, and adventure. The film is directed by Pixar veteran Andrew Stanton, returning to the franchise he helped pioneer.

## Returning Favorites and Fresh Faces

The voice cast is a mix of beloved icons and exciting new talent. Tom Hanks and Tim Allen reprise their iconic roles as the loyal cowboy Woody and the space ranger Buzz Lightyear. Joan Cusack returns as the energetic cowgirl Jessie. They are joined by newcomers: Greta Lee voices the digital entity Lilypad, while Conan O'Brien, Craig Robinson, Shelby Rabara, and Tony Hale lend their voices to new toy characters Smarty Pants, Atlas, Snappy, and Forky, respectively.

**Toy Story 5** continues to be a powerhouse at the box office.

## At the Box Office

Toy Story 5 has grossed $434,642,978 at the domestic box office, 33 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,400 theaters. The film added $3,050,058 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $958 million. It carries a reported production budget of $250 million.
