Steven Spielberg's *[Disclosure Day](/movies/disclosure-day-box-office-day-36-adds-400-000-as-domestic-total-hits-113-7/)* is a high-stakes sci-fi thriller starring Emily Blunt and Josh O'Connor. The film, which runs for 146 minutes, follows cybersecurity expert Daniel Kellner (Josh O'Connor) who becomes a whistleblower after uncovering corporate secrets about extraterrestrial life. On the run, he joins forces with meteorologist Margaret Fairchild (Emily Blunt), who is experiencing unexplained phenomena, as they race to prove the existence of life beyond Earth.

The ensemble cast is a powerhouse. Colin Firth portrays the corporate figure Noah Scanlon, while Colman Domingo plays government agent Hugo Wakefield. Eve Hewson is investigative journalist Jane Blakenship, and Wyatt Russell appears as a character named Jackson. Rounding out the key players are Elizabeth Marvel as Sister Maura and Henry Lloyd-Hughes as Casper Boyd. The film is a classic Spielbergian blend of intimate human drama against a vast, mysterious backdrop.

## The Box Office

However, the film's financial picture is complicated. The film is currently playing in **1,299 theaters**, a reduced count from its initial wide release. While the domestic cume is a personal best for the director in recent years, the overall *Disclosure Day box office* performance suggests the film may ultimately fall short of turning a profit.

## At the Box Office

Disclosure Day has grossed $114,704,000 at the domestic box office, 38 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 1,299 theaters. The film added $440,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $233 million. It carries a reported production budget of $115 million.
