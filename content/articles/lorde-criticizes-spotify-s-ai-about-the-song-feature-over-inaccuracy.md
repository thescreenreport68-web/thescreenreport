Lorde has publicly criticized Spotify’s new AI-powered “About the Song” feature. The singer called out the streaming service on Instagram after the beta tool shared inaccurate information about the context of one of her tracks.

According to a report from *The Hollywood Reporter*, Lorde posted a screenshot of the AI-generated text on her Instagram Story. The feature, which is currently in limited beta, appeared to have pulled information from a third-party website, incorrectly describing a specific performance from her recent Ultrasound tour. “I’m going to go out on a limb and say we don’t want this,” Lorde wrote.

## What did Lorde say about the AI feature?

In her post, Lorde took issue with both the factual error and the broader implication of the tool. “Not only is this inaccurate (not the song i did that in) but reducing a song to an ai generated meaning right at the source feels like it limits free interpretation imo,” she wrote. She concluded her critique with a direct request to the platform: “At least make it possible for artists to opt out please.”

## How did Spotify respond?

When asked for comment, a Spotify spokesperson confirmed to *The Hollywood Reporter* that “the text has been removed.” The spokesperson described “About the Song” as “a beta feature that presents cited information from third-party sources,” adding, “We’re continuing to improve it based on feedback from artists and fans.” The feature was released to a limited set of users in February.

Lorde’s comment highlights ongoing sensitivity around AI among artists. Other major figures, like SZA, have expressed similar concerns about artificial intelligence in creative spaces earlier this year. The incident also mirrors challenges faced by other tech companies, where AI data-scrapers can pull from a wide range of sources, sometimes leading to errors in the information presented.
