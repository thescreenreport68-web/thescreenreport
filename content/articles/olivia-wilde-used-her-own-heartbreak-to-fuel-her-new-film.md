You might expect a film rehearsal to be about blocking and lines, but for Olivia Wilde’s new project, it became a confessional. According to OK! Magazine, the director and star shared intimate secrets about her rocky romance with ex Jason Sudeikis to help shape her upcoming black comedy.

A source close to the production told the outlet that Wilde, 42, was brutally frank during pre-filming discussions for 'The Invite.' "The rehearsals quickly became less about reading lines and more about exploring what relationships look like behind closed doors. Olivia wasn't afraid to draw on difficult experiences, and everyone responded with the same honesty," the source said.

## The Creative Process

The film stars Wilde and Rogen as a married couple who invite their sexually adventurous neighbors, played by Penélope Cruz and Edward Norton, to dinner. The source claimed Wilde’s openness about her split from Sudeikis created a deep trust, encouraging the rest of the cast to share their own personal experiences to ground the film’s emotional conflicts in reality.

According to the report, Wilde hopes the final product resonates on a personal level.
