A Dinner Party That Spins Out of Control

Olivia Wilde steps behind the camera once more, directing and starring in her latest film, *[The Invite](/movies/the-invite-box-office-day-22-adds-1-1-million-as-domestic-total-hits-12-2/)*. The dark comedy-drama, which runs 107 minutes, centers on a couple whose marriage is on the rocks. Joe and Angela's tense evening takes a sharp turn when they invite their mysterious upstairs neighbors over for dinner, setting the stage for a night that spirals into completely unexpected territory.

## An A-List Ensemble

Wilde stars as Angela, one half of the troubled couple, while Seth Rogen plays her husband, Joe. The enigmatic and glamorous new neighbor, Pina, is portrayed by Penélope Cruz. Edward Norton rounds out the principal cast as Hawk, Pina's partner. The supporting roles feature Skip Howland as a Doorman, Mel Powell as a Bartender, and Rachel Thurow and Mario Valdez as Subway Goers, populating the film's urban landscape.

## The Box Office Trajectory

This early success suggested strong initial intrigue for Wilde's project.

## At the Box Office

The Invite has grossed $15,017,888 at the domestic box office, 24 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 2,110 theaters. The film added $1,221,904 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $16 million.
