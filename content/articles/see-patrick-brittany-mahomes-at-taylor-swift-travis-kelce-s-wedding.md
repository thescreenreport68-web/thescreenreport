Did Patrick and Brittany Mahomes score an invite to the wedding of the year? According to E! News, they did. The outlet reports the Kansas City Chiefs quarterback and his wife were in attendance as teammates Travis Kelce and Taylor Swift tied the knot at Madison Square Garden on July 3.

For the occasion, Brittany Mahomes reportedly wore a baby blue gown with statement diamonds, which Patrick showed off in a July 4 Instagram post. E! News notes she later changed into a chartreuse off-the-shoulder gown. Patrick, meanwhile, was described as looking dapper in a navy and black tuxedo before switching to a high-sheen brown suit.

The Mahomes' presence would be no surprise, given the close bond the two couples have forged since Swift and Kelce began dating in 2023. They've been spotted together at events like the US Open tennis tournament and when Swift attended Brittany's birthday in Nashville last fall.

Brittany, a mom to Sterling, 5, Bronze, 3, and Golden, 17 months, with Patrick, was publicly thrilled for the couple when they announced their engagement in August 2025. She wrote on Instagram at the time, "Two of the most genuine people meet and fall in love," adding, "Just so happy for these two."

The friendship extends to professional support as well. According to E! News, Patrick confirmed he got the inside scoop on Swift's latest album, The Life of a Showgirl. The outlet reports he said at a press conference in August, referencing Travis’ New Heights podcast where Taylor revealed the album, "They called me after the podcast was done," adding, "They didn't call me, they called Brittany, and I was in the back of the FaceTime." He reportedly said he was "super excited," noting, "Just knowing Taylor and seeing the process from just a little bit closer of how she makes the albums, it's going to be amazing because of how much work she puts into it."

While E! News reports the Mahomes were among the wedding guests, the full guest list and specific details of the ceremony have not been officially confirmed by Swift or Kelce's representatives.
