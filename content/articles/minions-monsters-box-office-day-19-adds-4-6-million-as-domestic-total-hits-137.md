The Animated Origin Story of Cinematic Chaos

Pierre Coffin's '[Minions & Monsters](/movies/minions-monsters-box-office-day-17-adds-4-4-million-as-domestic-total-hits-127/)' delivers the rambunctious, ridiculous, and totally true story of the little yellow henchmen's most catastrophic misadventure yet. This 90-minute animated adventure comedy chronicles how the Minions conquered Hollywood, became movie stars, lost everything, and then unleashed a horde of monsters onto the world. Forced to band together, they must now save the planet from the very mayhem they created. Coffin returns to the director's chair to helm this frenzied film-within-a-film.

## A Star-Studded Cast of Voices

The film boasts a deep bench of vocal talent. Pierre Coffin himself voices the core Minion quartet of James, Henry, Ed, and Dick. 'South Park' co-creator Trey Parker lends his voice to the character Goomi, while Academy Award winner Allison Janney plays Olivia. Christoph Waltz voices Max, and Jeff Bridges pulls double duty as Frank and Elwood. The ensemble is rounded out by Jesse Eisenberg as Dort, Zoey Deutch as Debbie, and Bobby Moynihan as Phillips. It's a chaotic tale of fame, failure, and frantic redemption, straight from the source of the mischief itself.

## At the Box Office

Minions & Monsters has grossed $137,584,000 at the domestic box office, 19 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,510 theaters. The film added $4,560,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $358 million. It carries a reported production budget of $85 million.
