**[Star Trek: Strange New Worlds](/tv/sci-fi-tv-premieres-star-trek-strange-new-worlds-avatar-aang-and-more/)** will continue its embrace of outrageous, episodic storytelling in its fourth season, according to a new report from IndieWire. The Paramount+ series, a prequel to *Star Trek: The Original Series*, is leaning into its fun, exploratory roots as it approaches its known narrative conclusion.

## What is the show's approach in Season 4?

Season 4 features a slate of standalone genre experiments, including a drug-trip episode, a black-and-white noir, and a puppet episode. The report describes the show as pushing the boundaries of what audiences expect from *Star Trek* while honoring the franchise's past through its world-of-the-week framework. One character even calls out the inherent absurdity, with a guest stating, "I’m sorry, but I have stood by this lunacy long enough."

This focus on episodic adventures helps distinguish the series within the established universe and serves to distract from persisting serialized challenges. The show is described as "science-fiction that’s pushing the fiction a little harder than the science."

## What are the challenges for the series?

The report notes that the series faces a classic prequel problem as it builds toward the start of *The Original Series*. Character arcs in Season 4 are reportedly revisiting old dilemmas, with crew members like Christine Chapel, La'An Noonien-Singh, and Dr. Joseph M'Benga grappling with issues similar to those from prior seasons. This creates a sense that characters are "running in circles" rather than progressing toward a finish line.

## When does the show end?

According to the IndieWire report, **Season 5 is slated to be the final season** and will consist of only six episodes. There is no word on whether Paramount is interested in "Year One," a reported pitch from the creative team to bridge the gap between *Strange New Worlds* and *The Original Series*. The show's episodic structure is seen as a strength that softens the impact of these narrative constraints, allowing it to remain "strangely fun" and culturally curious.
