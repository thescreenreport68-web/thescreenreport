Pixar [Animation](/movies/avatar-aang-the-last-airbender-fans-are-thrilled-the-movie-is-finally-here/) Studios was significantly impacted by company-wide layoffs at Disney on Tuesday, according to a report from Variety. A Disney spokesperson confirmed the media conglomerate is cutting several hundred jobs across certain corporate functions.

The cuts affect ESPN, many related to the integration of NFL Network, Disney Entertainment Television, and Disney’s studios. The majority of the layoffs on the studios side are within [Pixar](/movies/disney-layoffs-hit-pixar-hard-in-new-round-of-job-cuts/), and the majority of cuts in the TV group are at National Geographic.

## Where were the Pixar layoffs focused?

The job cuts at Pixar are concentrated in production and operations. A source familiar with the layoffs told Variety the changes reflect Pixar’s evolving needs related to production volume and the projects that are in process at the studio. More broadly, Walt Disney Studios has evolved its production strategy over the past three years to reduce overall volume and prioritize quality.

## What is the recent context for these cuts?

At that time, newly appointed Disney CEO Josh D’Amaro said in a memo that the company was looking to streamline operations to foster a more agile and technologically-enabled workforce.

Pixar has released two movies in 2026: the original adventure “Hoppers” and the summer blockbuster “[Toy Story 5](/movies/toy-story-5-box-office-day-26-domestic-total-climbs-to-410-6-million/),” which is imminently crossing the billion-dollar mark. The studio has struggled since the pandemic to launch new properties, a challenge executives have linked to the decision to send several films directly to Disney+ during COVID.
