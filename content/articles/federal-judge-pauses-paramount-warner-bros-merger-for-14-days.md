A federal judge has granted a temporary restraining order pausing the proposed merger between Paramount and Warner Bros. Discovery for 14 days, according to Deadline. The order was issued by U.S. District Judge Araceli Martinez-Olguin in response to a lawsuit filed by California Attorney General [Rob Bonta](/movies/california-ag-rob-bonta-denies-cnn-spinoff-would-end-paramount-merger-lawsu/) and 11 other states.

## What Does the Judge's Order Say?

The judge's order bars the companies "from closing or consummating the Transaction or taking any steps, directly or indirectly, to integrate or consolidate their operations." In her ruling, Martinez-Olguin wrote that the states presented compelling evidence of substantial market share. "On this combined firm market share alone, the Court is persuaded that it can presume the proposed merger is likely to violate antitrust laws," she stated.

She also noted that the "balance of equities, combined with the public’s vital interest in antitrust enforcement, therefore tips sharply in favor of the requested injunctive relief."

## What Are the Arguments Against the Merger?

The coalition of state attorneys general argues the deal creates undue concentration in markets for wide-release films, distribution of top-grossing theatrical films, and licensing of basic cable channels. They contend such consolidation harms workers, artists, and consumers.

New York Attorney General Letitia James said, "This lawsuit is about a simple fact: when one company controls a massive share of our film and television industries, workers, artists, businesses, and consumers suffer."

## How Has Paramount Responded?

Paramount has defended the transaction vigorously. A company spokesperson said the merger is "lawful, pro-competitive, and will benefit consumers, creators, workers, and the entertainment industry." The company argues the states' alleged markets and claims are "without any basis in modern market realities."

Paramount contends the transaction would bolster competition in streaming, as the combination would stand a better chance against stronger rivals.

## What Happens Next?

The judge has set a schedule for the states' motion for a preliminary injunction, which could halt the merger indefinitely. The states must file their motion by Thursday, with Paramount's opposition due by July 27 and a reply from the states due by July 30.

The legal timing carries financial stakes.

California Attorney General Rob Bonta called the TRO a "critical first win." He added, "We have a full tank of gas, the law on our side, and look forward to continuing to make our case."
