Sydney Sweeney is set to star in a new film adaptation of the classic 'Sleepy Hollow' tale for Sony Pictures, according to Deadline. The project, titled *Hollow*, will be directed and adapted by writer-director Lindsey Anderson Beer.

Beer will also produce the film, which is based on her upcoming debut novel of the same name. Sweeney's production banner, Honey Trap, will produce alongside LuckyChap Entertainment.

## What is the story of 'Hollow'?

The film is a reimagining of *The Legend of Sleepy Hollow*, told from the perspective of Katrina Van Tassel. The novel reframes Van Tassel not as a romantic prize but as the central figure in a supernatural mystery and love triangle. The story blends gothic atmosphere with psychological intrigue and erotic thriller elements.

## Who is producing the film?

Production will be handled by Lindsey Anderson Beer, LuckyChap Entertainment, and Sweeney's Honey Trap banner. *Hollow* marks the first film project for the newly launched Honey Trap, which recently entered a first-look deal with Sony. Alexandra Banks and Spencer Walken will executive produce for Beer’s Lab Brew.

## What is the status of the project?

Sony Pictures acquired the film rights in a competitive situation. The studio is reportedly moving quickly and has already begun casting for the iconic roles of Brom Bones and Ichabod Crane. The source novel is scheduled for publication by Putnam in Fall 2027, with translation rights sold globally.
