Is the door to a *Jackass* reunion finally slammed shut? Bam Margera just made his position crystal clear.

"I’ll definitely check out the movie, and I hope it’s good, but as far as a reunion, it’s not going to happen, not in 10 million years," he told the outlet.

Margera appears in the newly released *Jackass: Best and Last*, but only through archival footage. His comments, however, are entirely focused on the present and future. "I don’t have any bad blood with the cast of *Jackass*," he clarified. "It’s just the decisions that Johnny Knoxville and Jeff Tremaine decided to make. I never want to see them ever again in my life. Enough is enough."

This latest statement adds another chapter to years of public tension. Margera was fired from 2022's *Jackass Forever* after an alleged violation of a "wellness agreement," leading to a lawsuit that was later settled. Director Jeff Tremaine was also previously granted a temporary restraining order against Margera following alleged threats.

Knoxville has previously expressed a more conciliatory tone. Speaking to Variety about Margera's absence from *Jackass Forever*, he said, "unfortunately that’s not the way it worked out." He added, "It’s really heartbreaking. I love Bam. We all love Bam. He’s our brother, you know? You just hope that he takes it upon himself to get the help that he needs, because we all care about him a lot."

While Margera's stance seems definitive, the full story behind the "decisions" he references and whether any private reconciliation attempts have occurred remains between those involved. For now, Margera is watching the final *Jackass* movie from the sidelines, with a firm boundary drawn in the sand.
