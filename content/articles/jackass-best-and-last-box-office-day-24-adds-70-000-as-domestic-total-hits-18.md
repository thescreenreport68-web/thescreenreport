Johnny Knoxville and his band of merry masochists are back for one last, painful hurrah. '[Jackass: Best and Last](/movies/jackass-best-and-last-box-office-day-22-adds-73-000-as-domestic-total-hits-17-9/)' is the fifth and final installment in the iconic stunt comedy franchise, a 92-minute action-documentary where the aging crew embarks on one final, insane crusade. Directed by longtime franchise helmer Jeff Tremaine, the film promises to deliver the same chaotic, cringe-inducing spectacle that has defined the series for over two decades.

The film reunites the full, original squad of daredevils, all playing heightened versions of themselves. Johnny Knoxville leads the charge as the fearless ringleader, with Steve-O, Chris Pontius, Jason 'Wee Man' Acuña, Dave England, Preston Lacy, and Ehren McGhehey all returning for more self-inflicted punishment. The cast is rounded out by Bam Margera, marking a full-circle moment for the franchise that built its legacy on friendship and folly.

## The Box Office

The strong **Jackass Best and Last opening weekend box office** performance proves that, even after all these years, audiences are still willing to pay to watch these guys hurt themselves for our amusement.

## At the Box Office

Jackass: Best and Last has grossed $18,020,000 at the domestic box office, 24 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 305 theaters. The film added $70,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $25 million. It carries a reported production budget of $10 million.
