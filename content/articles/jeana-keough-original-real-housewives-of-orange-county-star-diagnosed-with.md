Jeana Keough, an original star of *The Real Housewives of Orange County*, has been diagnosed with tongue cancer at age 70, according to Deadline. The news was confirmed by her daughter, Kara Bosworth, who shared details on social media.

Bosworth explained on a GoFundMe page that her mother first noticed a painful spot on her tongue a few months ago. After a biopsy, doctors confirmed it was cancer. An initial surgery to remove the tumor was followed by a second, where they discovered the cancer had spread.

Keough is now in an intensive treatment regimen. **“What we thought would be a one-time surgery became a full cancer battle,”** Bosworth wrote. “Jeana is now in her fifth week of chemotherapy, radiation, and immunotherapy. The treatments are relentless.”

## What is her current condition?

Bosworth described her mother as exhausted and struggling to stay awake. The pain makes swallowing difficult, leading to concerns about malnutrition. The treatments have also prevented Keough from working, creating a significant financial burden.

## How are fans supporting her?

To help cover medical and living expenses, Bosworth started a crowdfunding campaign. She wrote that all funds raised will go directly toward treatment costs, nutrition, and support.

Keough was a foundational member of the Bravo reality series when it premiered in 2006. She appeared on the show for its first five seasons and has made subsequent guest appearances. She is also featured in the premiere episode of *The Real Housewives Ultimate Girls Trip: Roaring 20th*, celebrating the franchise's anniversary.
