The first wave of fan reactions to **[Spider-Man](/movies/spider-man-brand-new-day-has-fans-split-hulk-fears-downey-s-villain-tease-and/): Brand New Day** is here, and yeah—the internet went into full meltdown. But not everyone is on the same page. The discourse is split between emotional connection and superhero fatigue, and it's not cooling off anytime soon.

## A Spider-Man Who's Been Through It—and Still Jokes

For many fans, this is the most real Spidey yet. Peter Parker's older, scarred, weighed down by loss, but he still can't shut up with the quips. That contrast? It's landing hard.

One viewer nailed the whole vibe, writing, "Peter is making stupid jokes, his life is falling apart and he's making stupid jokes. That’s my Peter Parker." Another added, "This is the darkest his life has been and he’s still joking around and shit. THIS is Spider-Man."

The moment that's really stuck is a single, brutal line that says it all. "I'm not in highschool anymore" —fans wasted no time calling it the most relatable thing Peter's ever said. One user posted, "God I feel that peter in my knees," while another unpacked the deeper weight: "'I'm not in high school anymore' You realize just how much he lost and how much he had to grow up." It's a forced, painful step into adulthood, and audiences are feeling it.

## The Other Side: Tired of the Pain Parade

But not everyone is buying this round of suffering. A smaller, louder group is over it. The critique is blunt: "Spider have 3 life circles. 1 Suffering 2 Suffering 3 Suffering." —a simple mic drop on a narrative loop some say is getting old.

There's also real-world frustration. A disappointed fan shared, "Disappointed to hear '[Spider-Man: Brand New Day](/movies/new-spider-man-brand-new-day-trailer-drops-exclusively-in-theaters/)' won't be on IMAX screens because Nolan has a contract to have 'The Odyssey' in IMAX screens exclusively for a month. Not only that, Spider-Man has been forced to the gimmick format ScreenX which barely exists in UK." —adding a layer of annoyance to the experience for international audiences.

## Why the Split Runs So Deep

For the fans who love it, the darkness isn't gratuitous—it's the price of hope. One fan summed it up: "I really like the ending where Peter still quips, stretching before he fights. I’m glad that despite this is literally the worst he’s ever been in life he still has the heart to joke around." The idea being that Peter keeps choosing the mask despite losing everything, and that's the point.

For the critics, it feels like a dead end—a refusal to let the character actually grow past the trauma. Meanwhile, insiders are stirring the pot. Alex Perez teased, "I’ve heard she is in it. Likely a small cameo, nothing major to add to the story," and clarified about Peter's location: "We don’t know where [Spider-Man] went. It shows he’s in 'space,' but the distance is so massive that he could be in another universe." [Tom Holland](/movies/spider-man-brand-new-day-trailer-drops-teasing-a-world-without-recogni/) himself gave a shoutout to a co-star: "She handled the pressure of the character that she's playing with such grace. And the third act, she steals the movie. It is so good."

One thing is certain: nobody is done talking about this. The audience's own words show they're split—both moved and exhausted by Peter Parker's latest, darkest chapter.
