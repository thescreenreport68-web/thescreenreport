[Tom Holland](/movies/tom-holland-praises-robert-pattinson-s-casting-in-the-odyssey-you-re-so/) has shared a new update on the long-gestating Fred Astaire biopic, revealing his ambitious plans for the film's dance sequences. The actor discussed the project in a recent interview, according to Deadline.

Holland is preparing to dive back into the dance studio after finishing promotional tours for his other films. He explained his approach to the role, stating a desire to honor Astaire's legacy through his performance.

## What does Tom Holland want to do for the dance scenes?

He aims to perform all the dancing himself without using stunt doubles. Holland also wants to shoot those dance sequences in single, continuous takes, mirroring how Fred Astaire himself would have filmed them. This method presents a significant physical challenge.

## How does Holland feel about the role?

The actor described a mix of excitement and apprehension after his first few rehearsals. "I did my first few rehearsals recently, and it both filled me with excitement and absolute dread because I’ve got so much work to do to try and do Fred proud," Holland said on *Good Morning America*.

## What is the status of the Fred Astaire movie?

The film is in development at Sony, with Paul King attached to direct and Steven Levenson writing the script. Holland was first cast in the role in 2021, and he is now actively preparing for the demanding physical work required.
