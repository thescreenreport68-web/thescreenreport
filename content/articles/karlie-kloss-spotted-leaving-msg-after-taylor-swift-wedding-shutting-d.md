Was Karlie Kloss really there? After years of speculation about a rift, fans were quick to dissect every detail of Taylor Swift's reported wedding weekend. A new photo seems to provide a definitive answer.

According to a report from Just Jared, Karlie Kloss was photographed in a car leaving Madison Square Garden in the early morning hours of Saturday, July 4, in New York City. The sighting indicates the 33-year-old model did, in fact, attend the reported ceremony held at the venue, despite online theories to the contrary.

The speculation began earlier that weekend. As guests headed to MSG on Friday, Kloss was seen leaving her New York City home in a gold Tove dress alongside her husband, Joshua Kushner. While most assumed this was her wedding party departure, a subset of fans spun a conspiracy that she had staged the paparazzi photos to create the illusion of attendance without actually going.

The latest image from outside the venue appears to shut that theory down. Kloss's friendship with Swift dates back to 2012, after the singer mentioned wanting to bake cookies with the model in her Vogue cover story. Their bond became a pop culture fixture, leading to joint runway walks at Victoria's Secret Fashion Shows and a shared Vogue cover in 2015.

Rumors of a falling out began circulating in 2018. Kloss addressed the chatter that year in a profile for The New York Times, telling the publication, "Don’t believe everything you read." The speculation intensified in 2019 when Kloss had music executive Scooter Braun—who had just purchased Swift's master recordings—as a guest at her own wedding to Kushner.

In recent years, signs have pointed toward a thaw. Kloss attended Swift's Eras Tour in 2023, though she sat in regular seats, not the VIP tent. Her confirmed presence at the reported wedding is the strongest signal yet that any past tensions have been resolved.
