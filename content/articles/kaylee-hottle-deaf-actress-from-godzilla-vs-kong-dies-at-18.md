Kaylee Hottle, the deaf actress who played Jia in the blockbuster films “Godzilla vs. Kong” and “Godzilla x Kong: The New Empire,” died at age 18 in a car accident in Maryland. The news was confirmed by her father, Joshua Hottle, and reported by multiple outlets including The Hollywood Reporter.

Her father announced the news in a Facebook livestream using American Sign Language. He captioned the video, “I am taking a flight that I never would like to take,” indicating he was flying to Maryland to claim his daughter’s body, according to TheWrap.

## What happened to Kaylee Hottle?

Hottle died in a single-vehicle accident in Frederick, Maryland, as reported by Deadline, citing the Frederick County Sheriff’s Office. The preliminary investigation indicated the crash occurred in the early morning hours. She was a passenger in the vehicle. The driver, a 19-year-old man, sustained non-life-threatening injuries.

## How did the community react?

The Texas School for the Deaf, where Hottle was a senior, issued a statement on social media. “It is with profound sadness that we share the heartbreaking news that one of our TSD seniors, Kaylee Hottle, tragically passed away yesterday in a car accident in Frederick, Maryland,” the school wrote. The statement asked for privacy and respect for the family.

## What was Kaylee Hottle known for?

Hottle was best known for playing Jia, a deaf, orphaned girl who forms a unique bond with Kong through sign language in the MonsterVerse films. She made her feature film debut in 2021’s “Godzilla vs. Kong” at the age of 9 and reprised the role in the 2024 sequel. Her performance in the sequel earned her a Saturn Award nomination for Best Performance by a Younger Actor.

She was discovered for the role after appearing in a 2017 commercial for the video relay service Convo. An assistant director from “Kong: Skull Island” saw the ad and recommended her for the part. In a March 2024 interview with the Houston Chronicle, Hottle explained her process. “I would read the script, and [director] Adam [Wingard] would explain to me exactly what he wanted me to do. And I also had my parents’ support and translation of the script, and I just brought it to life.”

Her co-star Alexander Skarsgård praised her professionalism in a 2021 interview, noting her comfort in front of the camera and ability to quickly take direction. Hottle came from a multi-generational deaf family and was fluent in American Sign Language. In addition to her film work, she appeared in a 2021 episode of “Magnum P.I.” and was an athlete on her school’s track-and-field team.
