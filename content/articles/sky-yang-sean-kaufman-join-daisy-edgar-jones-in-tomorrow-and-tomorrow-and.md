Sky Yang and Sean Kaufman have joined the cast of Paramount’s upcoming film adaptation of Gabrielle Zevin’s novel “Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, and Tomorrow,” according to Variety. The actors will star opposite Daisy Edgar-Jones in the story about friendship, love, and video game creation.

Yang has been cast as Sam Masur, a video game designer who meets Sadie (Edgar-Jones) as a child and reunites with her in college. Kaufman will play Marx Watanabe, Sam’s roommate and friend. The trio forms a game studio called Unfair Games. The story unfolds across decades against the backdrop of the 1990s and 2000s video game industry.

## Who is directing the film?

Oscar winner Siân Heder is directing and writing the screenplay. The script is based on drafts by Mark Bomback and author Gabrielle Zevin. The film will be produced by Temple Hill’s Wyck Godfrey, Marty Bowen, and Isaac Klausner, with Zevin serving as an executive producer.

## When does the movie come out?

The novel, published in 2022, has sold over four million copies worldwide and was named one of the 100 best books of the 21st century by the New York Times.

## What other projects are the new cast members in?

Sky Yang’s recent credits include a starring role in Justin Lin’s Sundance drama “Last Days” and Zack Snyder’s “Rebel Moon.” He is also set to appear in Paul Greengrass’s historical drama “The Uprising.” Sean Kaufman is best known for his role in the Amazon Prime Video series “The Summer I Turned Pretty,” a part he will reprise in an upcoming film. He is also attached to star in Ron Howard’s war drama “Alone at Dawn” and an untitled Universal film from The Daniels.
