Is Lionel Richie ready to party all night again? The music icon just posted a reassuring update after a health scare sidelined his tour.

Richie, 77, took to Instagram over the weekend with a carousel of smiling, dancing concert photos. "Thank you for every message, every kind word, and for all your love," he wrote. "I’m doing well, and I’m grateful for all of you."

The post was his first direct message to fans since he felt dizzy during his tour-opener at the Grand Casino Arena on June 24, 2026. According to a fan video reported by the Minnesota Star Tribune, Richie paused and took a seat. He reportedly joked to the crowd, "When you’re feeling dizzy, sit your a– down." The show was ultimately canceled.

The St. Paul Fire Department confirmed to Fox News Digital that an individual was "transported from Grand Casino Arena late on June 24, 2026, to a local hospital by Saint Paul Fire-EMS." Following doctors' orders, Richie's next two shows in Chicago and Columbus, Ohio, were postponed. An announcement from the United Center read, "Under advisement by doctors to rest and return to full health, Lionel Richie has postponed his next two shows," adding, "Lionel is heartbroken to postpone these two shows and cannot wait to be back performing for his fans."

Richie's latest Instagram post suggests his recovery is on track. He fondly recalled recent shows, writing, "Pittsburgh and Detroit were an absolute joy. The energy, the dancing, the faces in the crowd… we made real memories together." He ended with a forward-looking promise: "See you in Toronto… let’s party all night long!"

While the "American Idol" judge is back to performing, the exact nature of his health scare remains private. No additional details about the incident have been released. For now, fans can take comfort in seeing the legend back on his feet and ready to dance.
