His tribute confirmed the actress's passing, as reported by TMZ. She was 82.

Shinkoda added the two will see each other again one day. Another former colleague, Judy Lei from the film "The World’s Greatest," shared her distress at Wai Ching's passing, sharing memories from the film they worked on together and noting Wai Ching never became frustrated with her lack of experience, jumping in with no inhibitions.

## A Career Spanning Decades

Wai Ching Ho landed her first film role back in 1990 as a minor character in the Robin Williams flick "Cadillac Man." Over the next quarter century, she built a steady resume with appearances in projects like "A Price Above Rubies," "Law & Order: Criminal Intent," and "Premium Rush."

Her breakout to a wider audience came with the Marvel Netflix universe. She played the enigmatic and villainous Madame Gao across three series: "Daredevil," "Iron Fist," and "The Defenders." That notoriety earned her bigger roles, like her appearances in Jennifer Lopez's stripper-thriller "Hustlers," Pixar's "Turning Red," and a five-episode stretch on "Awkwafina Is Nora from Queens." Her final project, a short film titled "Here and Again," has not yet been released.

Wai Ching Ho's career trajectory showcases the impact of a dedicated character actor. From a small part in a major studio comedy to becoming a memorable villain in a sprawling superhero saga, her work resonated across genres. Her role as Madame Gao was particularly notable for its quiet menace and depth, making her a standout even among the flashier antagonists of the Marvel shows. This performance opened doors to high-profile projects in both film and television, demonstrating her versatility. Her later work in an acclaimed animated feature like "Turning Red" and a popular comedy series like Awkwafina Is Nora from Queens further cemented her status as a reliable and talented presence on screen.

The news of her death was confirmed by several former costars, according to TMZ. No cause of death has been disclosed.
