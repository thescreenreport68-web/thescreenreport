What does a pop superstar use for her "something blue"? How about an entire skyscraper.

The Empire State Building lit up in a dazzling electric blue on Friday evening, a spectacle the building's Instagram account captioned, "Her something blue 🩵." According to Page Six, the display was to celebrate Taylor Swift's marriage to Kansas City Chiefs tight end Travis Kelce. The building's website noted the specific hue was a "light blue sparkle color."

The light show followed reports that the couple wed at Madison Square Garden earlier on Friday. The venue reportedly confirmed the news with signs reading, "JUST T MARRIED!" Page Six reported the star-studded ceremony was attended by 1,000 of their closest friends and family members.

Fans on social media were quick to connect the iconic gesture to Swift's New York legacy. "I 💙 this!!! So sweet! NYC loves its girl! Congratulations @taylorswift! So happy for you!" one commenter wrote on the Empire State Building's post. Another reacted, "why am i crying?! 😭😭🩵." A third gushed, "I AM SCREAMING 🥹😭🩵💙🩵💙🩵💙🩵💙🩵💙🩵💙🩵💙🩵💙," while a fourth declared, "YES. HER SOMETHING BLUE!! So so sweet of you to ring in her marriage with something blue on the most beautiful and iconic building in the world!!! 💙💙💙💙💙💙💙💙💙💙."

The blue tribute appears to be a nod to the couple's engagement announcement. Page Six also reported that a press release immediately following the ceremony revealed the bride and groom wore Christian Dior Haute Couture.

While the Empire State Building's tribute and the Madison Square Garden signs strongly suggest a wedding took place, official confirmation from Swift or Kelce's representatives is still pending. The details of the ceremony, including the officiant and specific guest list, remain unconfirmed by the couple.
