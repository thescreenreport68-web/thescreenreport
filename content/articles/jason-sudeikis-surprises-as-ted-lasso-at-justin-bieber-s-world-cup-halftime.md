Jason Sudeikis made a surprise cameo as Ted Lasso during Justin Bieber’s halftime performance at the 2026 FIFA World Cup on Sunday, July 19, according to Billboard. The actor appeared in character alongside Brendan Hunt as Coach Beard at New York New Jersey Stadium in East Rutherford, N.J., offering commentary on the show before introducing the pop star.

The bit featured the fictional coaches walking the stadium. “Coach, I gotta say, first half of halftime knocked my darn socks off,” Sudeikis said as Lasso. “How the heck is anyone gonna follow all that?” After suggesting the show needed “someone who can bring the love,” he called for Bieber.

Sudeikis then delivered a pep talk to the singer. “All right, young man,” he said. “Let’s go! It’s your big shot. OK. Listen to me. Go out there. Don’t be nervous. Nobody’s watching. Make us proud.” The segment led directly into Bieber’s performance of his song “Everything Hallelujah.”

## What was the rest of the halftime show?

The Ted Lasso cameo was part of an 11-minute halftime show that Billboard reports included high-energy musical performances from artists like Madonna, BTS, Shakira with Burna Boy, and Coldplay. Bieber’s acoustic moment provided a quieter contrast within the larger spectacle.

FOX, the network broadcasting the 2026 World Cup, shared the clip of the surprise appearance on social media. The full halftime show was also uploaded to YouTube.
