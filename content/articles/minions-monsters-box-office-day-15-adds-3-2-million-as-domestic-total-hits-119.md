**PART 1 — THE MOVIE**

Pierre Coffin's *[Minions & Monsters](/movies/minions-monsters-box-office-day-14-domestic-total-climbs-to-116-4-million/)* brings back the chaotic yellow crew for their most outlandish adventure yet. This 90-minute animated family comedy tells the rambunctious, ridiculous, and supposedly true story of how the Minions conquered Hollywood, became movie stars, lost everything, and then accidentally unleashed monsters onto the world. Their final mission? To band together and save the planet from the very mayhem they created.

Coffin pulls double duty, not only directing but also voicing the core Minion crew—James, Henry, Ed, and Dick. He's joined by a stellar voice cast: Trey Parker as Goomi, Allison Janney as Olivia, and Christoph Waltz as Max. Jeff Bridges voices both Frank and Elwood, while Jesse Eisenberg plays Dort, Zoey Deutch is Debbie, and Bobby Moynihan rounds out the cast as Phillips.

**PART 2 — THE BOX OFFICE**

The film's **Minions & Monsters opening weekend box office** story is a tale of two halves. Domestically, it's a comedown. The movie opened at No. That debut marks the lowest opening in the franchise's storied history.

Globally, however, the picture is far brighter.

## At the Box Office

Minions & Monsters has grossed $119,545,820 at the domestic box office, 15 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 4,244 theaters. The film added $3,160,435 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $280 million. It carries a reported production budget of $85 million.
