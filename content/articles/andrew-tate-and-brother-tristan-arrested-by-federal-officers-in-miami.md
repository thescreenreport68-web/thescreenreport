Controversial influencers Andrew Tate and his brother Tristan were arrested by federal officers in Miami on Saturday afternoon, according to a report from the Associated Press cited by Variety. The specific charges levied against the brothers have not yet been made public.

This marks the latest legal entanglement for the pair, who have faced a series of serious accusations in other countries in recent years. In 2022, they were arrested in Romania by anti-terrorism authorities on charges of human trafficking and rape. Those charges were formally filed by Romanian prosecutors in 2023.

Andrew Tate has consistently denied any wrongdoing in the Romanian case. After a period of house arrest, he told media in 2023, “We’ve been completely innocent since the beginning of this, and I have to give absolute faith to the Romanian judicial system for finally making the right decision and letting us free.”

## What other charges do the Tate brothers face?

Beyond the Romanian case, British prosecutors authorized 21 charges against Andrew and Tristan Tate in May 2025, according to the BBC. These include allegations of rape, actual bodily harm, and human trafficking. Andrew Tate faces 10 charges connected to three alleged victims, while Tristan Tate faces 11 charges tied to one alleged victim.

A Romanian court has since lifted all preventative judicial control measures against the Tates while authorities continue their investigation, as reported by Reuters. The Miami arrest represents a new and separate federal legal front for the brothers, whose initial rise to notoriety came after Andrew Tate's appearance on the U.K.'s "Big Brother" in 2016.
