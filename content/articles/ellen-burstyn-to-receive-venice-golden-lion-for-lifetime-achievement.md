Ellen Burstyn is set to receive a prestigious lifetime achievement honor from the Venice Film Festival. According to Deadline, the Oscar-winning actress will be awarded the Golden Lion for Lifetime Achievement later this year.

The festival will present the award to Burstyn at a special screening of the short film **"Flesh Impact."** The film is directed by Maggie Gyllenhaal and stars Burstyn alongside Dakota Johnson, Peter Sarsgaard, and Sepideh Moafi. The short is dedicated to Marilyn Monroe on the centenary of her birth, with Johnson portraying Monroe at the height of her fame.

## What is the short film about?

A synopsis provided by the festival describes the film as featuring "another extraordinary performance" from Burstyn. Her role is described as a version of Marilyn Monroe the world never got to see. The title "Flesh Impact" references a phrase once used to describe Monroe's on-screen presence, suggesting she looked so real viewers felt they could touch her through the screen.

## What did Ellen Burstyn say about the award?

In a statement, Burstyn expressed her joy and gratitude. "Wow! I not only get to travel to one of my top-most favorite cities in the whole world. I get to return home carrying a Golden Lion in my arms!" she said. "The Lifetime Achievement Award from the Venice Film Festival! I feel so honored— so happy — so filled with gratitude! Wow indeed!"

George Clooney will also receive an honorary award at this year's festival, which is scheduled to run from September 2 to September 12.
