A slate of new movies and TV shows has premiered this July, according to a report from Essence. The month's highlights include the arrival of a major studio epic, the final season of a long-running series, and the debut of a new crime drama.

## What major film has been released?

Christopher Nolan's adaptation of **The Odyssey** has reportedly arrived in theaters. The film from Universal Pictures features an all-star cast including Matt Damon as Odysseus, Tom Holland as Telemachus, and Lupita Nyong’o and [Zendaya](/movies/zendaya-the-odyssey-christopher-nolan/) in unspecified roles. According to verified data, the 173-minute epic carries an R rating and has a reported budget of $250 million.

## What TV shows are premiering?

The CW's football drama **All American** has returned for its eighth and final season. The farewell season picks up six months after the championship showdown between Beverly and Crenshaw, following the characters through a single eventful week.

On STARZ, the new series **Fightland** premieres July 31. The crime drama, executive produced by Curtis "50 Cent" Jackson, stars Howard Charles as a former boxing champion who returns to London after prison seeking revenge. He is convinced someone close to him destroyed his life.

## What else is new this month?

Other notable July releases include the second season of BET's mystery series *Diarra From Detroit*, premiering July 29, and Sony's *Spider-Man: Brand New Day*, which hits theaters July 31 and sees Zendaya return as MJ. STARZ also continues the final chapter of its prequel series, *Power Book III: Raising Kanan*.
