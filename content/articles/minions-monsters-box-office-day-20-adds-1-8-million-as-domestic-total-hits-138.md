*[Minions & Monsters](/movies/minions-monsters-box-office-day-19-adds-4-6-million-as-domestic-total-hits-137/)* brings the chaotic, yellow henchmen back to the big screen for another round of globe-trotting mayhem. Directed by longtime franchise voice Pierre Coffin, this 90-minute animated adventure-comedy tells the rambunctious, ridiculous, and supposedly true story of how the Minions conquered Hollywood, became movie stars, lost everything, and then unleashed monsters onto the world, forcing them to band together to save the planet from the chaos they created.

The voice cast is a blend of franchise stalwarts and new talent. Pierre Coffin returns as the voice of the Minions themselves, alongside characters James, Henry, Ed, and Dick. He's joined by *South Park* co-creator Trey Parker as Goomi, Oscar winner Allison Janney as Olivia, and two-time Oscar winner Christoph Waltz as Max. The ensemble is rounded out by Jeff Bridges (voicing Frank and Elwood), Jesse Eisenberg as Dort, Zoey Deutch as Debbie, and Bobby Moynihan as Phillips. It's a tale of hubris, havoc, and tiny yellow redemption.

## The Box Office: A Franchise First… For All the Wrong Reasons

The box office story for *Minions & Monsters* is one of historic underperformance for the powerhouse franchise.

The **Minions & Monsters franchise low debut** signals a potential cooling off for one of animation's most reliable brands, leaving Universal to ponder the future of its mischievous minions.

## At the Box Office

Minions & Monsters has grossed $138,208,155 at the domestic box office, 20 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 3,510 theaters. The film added $1,822,455 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $358 million. It carries a reported production budget of $85 million.
