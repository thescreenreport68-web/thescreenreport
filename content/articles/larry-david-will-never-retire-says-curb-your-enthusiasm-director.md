Larry David is already working on his next act, proving that talk of his retirement is greatly exaggerated. His longtime collaborator, director Jeff Schaffer, confirmed that the 77-year-old comedian is returning to television with a new HBO series just months after Curb Your Enthusiasm wrapped its 12th and final season.

David's career is a masterclass in turning personal neuroses into landmark comedy. He started as a stand-up in New York City, working odd jobs while living in Manhattan Plaza. His first major TV break came as a writer and performer on ABC's *Fridays* from 1980 to 1982. A brief, famously frustrating stint writing for *Saturday Night Live* followed, where he met future *Seinfeld* star Julia Louis-Dreyfus.

## What else has Larry David created?

David's true breakthrough arrived in 1989 when he teamed with Jerry Seinfeld to create *The Seinfeld Chronicles*. The show became a cultural phenomenon, defining 1990s television. David was the primary writer and the real-life inspiration for the character of George Costanza. He wrote 62 episodes, including the legendary "The Contest," which earned him a Primetime Emmy Award. After leaving the show amicably after season seven, he returned to write the polarizing series finale in 1998.

Following *Seinfeld*'s conclusion, David launched a new, even more personal project. In 1999, HBO aired a special titled *Larry David: Curb Your Enthusiasm*. Its success led to a series that premiered in 2000. The show featured David playing a fictionalized, more socially reckless version of himself. **It was largely improvised from brief outlines**, capturing the awkward minutiae of everyday life and coining the term "Larry David moment." The critically adored series ran for 12 seasons, earning 30 Primetime Emmy nominations and a Golden Globe win before concluding in 2024.

## What is Larry David's best movie?

While television is his dominant medium, David has appeared in several notable films. His most significant film role came in Woody Allen's 2009 comedy *Whatever Works*, where he played the lead opposite Evan Rachel Wood. He had earlier cameos in Allen's *Radio Days* (1987) and *New York Stories* (1989). David also wrote and starred in the 2013 HBO television film *Clear History* and made a memorable appearance in the 2012 reboot of *The Three Stooges* as Sister Mary-Mengele.

## Is Larry David really worth $400 million?

David's wealth stems overwhelmingly from the syndication of *Seinfeld*. Reports from 2013 estimated the show had generated [$3.1 billion in rerun fees](https://www.boxofficemojo.com/). David's personal share from that deal was estimated at $250 million in 1998 alone. While his 2007 divorce in California, a community property state, reportedly cut his net worth in half, subsequent estimates still place it between $400 and $900 million. He confirmed his wealth in a 2015 interview, though he called public figures "crazy."

## What's next for Larry David?

Retirement isn't on the agenda. In July 2025, David announced a new seven-episode sketch comedy limited series for HBO titled *Life, Larry and the Pursuit of Unhappiness*. Co-written and executive produced with Jeff Schaffer, who will also direct, the series will focus on overlooked figures in U.S. history. It is produced in collaboration with Barack and Michelle Obama's Higher Ground Productions and is slated to premiere on June 26, 2026. As Schaffer indicated, the end of *Curb* merely marked the start of a new chapter for an artist fundamentally defined by his work.
