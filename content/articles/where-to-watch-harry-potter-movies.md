Here's the current US picture at a glance, and how it changes on July 1.

- **HBO Max (Max):** Yes, **is Harry Potter on HBO Max** — all 8 films, but only until June 30, 2026.
- **Peacock:** Yes, **are Harry Potter movies on Peacock** — all 8, also only until June 30, 2026.

The dual HBO Max-and-Peacock window is unusual and temporary. Treat it as a closing door, not a permanent home.

## Why the Harry Potter movies are leaving streaming in July 2026

This isn't a routine licensing shuffle. **Why are the Harry Potter movies leaving streaming in 2026?** Warner Bros. owns the films through Warner Bros. Pictures, and it's deliberately pulling them from subscription tiers as it builds anticipation for its ambitious **HBO Harry Potter TV series reboot**, a faithful, season-per-book adaptation whose first season is expected to debut in December 2026.

Removing the movies makes the franchise feel fresh and scarce right before the show arrives — and nudges fans toward buying digital copies they'll keep. The same purge also affects the three **Fantastic Beasts** spin-offs on HBO Max. If you've been meaning to revisit the Wizarding World, this is your cue to do it before the shelves go bare. ## Can you watch Harry Potter for free in 2026?

Realistically, **how to watch Harry Potter for free** has no clean answer in the US. There's no legitimate ad-supported on-demand stream of the films. Your closest free route is catching them during **ad-supported cable marathons** — the franchise frequently airs in big blocks on linear TV, including around holidays, so a marathon can let you watch several films back-to-back at no extra cost if you have a TV package.

Avoid any website promising free full-movie streams of Harry Potter. Those are almost always pirated, riddled with malware, or geo-spoofed, and they vanish as fast as they appear. The small cost of a digital bundle buys you 4K quality, no ads, and zero risk.
