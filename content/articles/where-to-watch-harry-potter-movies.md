If you want the short answer: as of mid-2026 in the United States, all eight Harry Potter movies stream on **HBO Max** as part of a standard subscription. That makes it the easiest single place to watch the entire saga, from *Sorcerer's Stone* to the second half of *Deathly Hallows*, with no extra rental fees. If you don't subscribe, you can also rent or buy every film individually on digital storefronts. So when people ask **where to watch Harry Potter**, the cleanest route right now is one HBO Max login and a free weekend.

The films are owned by Warner Bros., which is why they cluster on HBO Max rather than scattering across rival services. That ownership is also why you won't find them on Netflix or Disney+ in the US. One caveat worth knowing: the movies' US streaming rights have rotated between HBO Max and Peacock over the years, so the subscription home isn't permanent. Below, we break down every way to watch, the correct viewing order, and how the broader Wizarding World fits in.

## Where to watch Harry Potter movies right now

Here are your main options in the US:

- **HBO Max (subscription):** All eight films are currently included with your plan. The best value if you want to marathon the series or rewatch on a whim.
- **Rent or buy (digital):** Each movie is available à la carte on platforms such as Prime Video, Apple TV, and Fandango at Home. Buying gives you permanent access regardless of streaming-licensing shuffles.
- **Physical media:** The films remain widely available on Blu-ray and 4K UHD, often bundled as a complete eight-film collection — the most future-proof option of all.

Streaming rights rotate over time, and the saga has previously lived on Peacock during certain windows. As of this writing, HBO Max is home base. If you're outside the US, check your regional Warner-affiliated service, since licensing varies by country.

## How to watch the Harry Potter movies in order

Good news: release order and chronological order are identical, so there's no puzzle here. Watch them like this:

1. **Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone** (2001)
2. **Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets** (2002)
3. **Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban** (2004)
4. **Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire** (2005)
5. **Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix** (2007)
6. **Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince** (2009)
7. **Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1** (2010)
8. **Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2** (2011)

One note for international viewers: the first film is titled *Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone* outside the US. It's the same movie, just a different name on the box.

## Should you watch the Fantastic Beasts films too?

The Wizarding World extends beyond the core eight with three *Fantastic Beasts* movies, set decades before Harry was born. They follow magizoologist Newt Scamander and the rise of the dark wizard Grindelwald, and they're best treated as a separate prequel arc. You do **not** need them to understand Harry's story.

If you want the full chronological experience, you could watch the *Fantastic Beasts* trilogy first, since it takes place earlier in the timeline. But most fans — and we'd agree — recommend doing the eight Harry Potter films first. They're the emotional heart of the franchise, and the prequels land better once you already love this world.

## Why the films aren't on Netflix or Disney+

This trips up a lot of viewers. Because the Harry Potter movies are Warner Bros. properties, they live inside Warner's own streaming ecosystem — HBO Max — rather than on competitors' platforms. Disney+ houses Disney, Pixar, Marvel, and Star Wars titles; Netflix licenses a rotating mix but doesn't hold the Potter rights in the US. So if you've been hunting for them on those apps, you're simply looking in the wrong shop.

This is the same studio-ownership logic that shapes a lot of modern streaming. It's why franchises like the [MCU movies in order](/movies/mcu-movies-in-order) gravitate toward Disney+, and why a Warner saga like this one anchors HBO Max. Knowing who owns what is half the battle when you're tracking down a film.

## The best way to marathon the saga

Eight movies is a serious commitment — roughly 20 hours of screen time across the set — so plan accordingly. A few tips:

- **Split it across a weekend.** Two to three films a day keeps the experience joyful rather than exhausting.
- **Watch the *Deathly Hallows* pair back-to-back.** Parts 1 and 2 are essentially one long finale split in half, and they hit hardest when viewed together.
- **Use HBO Max's resume feature.** If you're pausing mid-saga across several nights, picking up where you left off is seamless.

And if you prefer your fantasy in tighter doses, the [best limited series to stream](/tv/best-limited-series-to-stream) offer self-contained worlds you can finish in a sitting.

## What about the new Harry Potter TV series?

The movies aren't the only way to revisit Hogwarts anymore. HBO is producing a television adaptation of the Harry Potter books, conceived as a faithful, long-form retelling planned to span multiple seasons — roughly one per novel. It features an entirely new cast and is a wholly separate project from the original film series. The first season is scheduled to premiere on HBO Max on **December 25, 2026**.

That means the eight movies you can watch today are the definitive cinematic version, and they aren't going anywhere. The series is an expansion of the brand, not a replacement. If anything, it's a great excuse to revisit the films first so you can compare adaptations once the show arrives.

## A quick word on quality

On HBO Max, the films stream in high definition, and the platform has offered 4K presentations of select major library titles (availability can vary by film). For the absolute best picture and sound — particularly the sweeping Quidditch sequences and the rain-soaked Battle of Hogwarts — the 4K UHD Blu-rays remain the gold standard. But for sheer convenience, streaming in HD is more than enough to fall back under the spell.

## The bottom line

To recap the answer to **where to watch Harry Potter**: stream all eight films on HBO Max in the US right now, or rent and buy them individually anywhere digital movies are sold. Watch them in release order — which doubles as chronological order — and treat the *Fantastic Beasts* prequels as optional extra credit.

With a brand-new HBO series arriving on December 25, 2026, there's never been a better moment to revisit the saga that started it all. Brew some Butterbeer, dim the lights, and let the Sorting Hat decide what kind of weekend you're about to have.
