The spotlight hit the stage at the David H. Koch Theater in New York on Wednesday, July 15, as Marcello Hernández, the "Saturday Night Live" star, stepped up to host the ESPYs, ready to roast the sports world.

His 10-minute monologue, as reported by TMZ, landed its biggest laughs with jokes aimed at legends Tiger Woods and Bill Belichick. For Woods, Hernández quipped the golf superstar will be on the cover of the next "Grand Theft Auto" video game—a clear, playful nod to Woods's March DUI rollover crash and arrest.

Belichick wasn't spared either. Hernández cracked that when the New York Knicks won their 1973 NBA title, the former NFL coach was the same age his girlfriend, Jordon Hudson, is today. The math might be fuzzy, but the joke about their age gap landed.

## The Supporting Cast of Roasts

Hernández's targets extended beyond the legends. He teased Jake Paul for his penchant for fighting older opponents and gave Jalen Brunson a lighthearted "nepo baby" label. The entire WNBA also got a mention in the rapid-fire set.

Notably absent from the monologue was any mention of Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce's July 3 New York wedding.

## Background and Comedic Style

Marcello Hernández's comedy is deeply rooted in his personal life and heritage. According to People.com, his parents immigrated to the United States—his mother from Cuba and his father from the Dominican Republic—and they inspire much of his material. He has said, "A lot of the jokes I say and the stuff that I write has to do with our relationship growing up," speaking about his mother to Variety. Hosting a major event like the ESPYs represents a significant career milestone, allowing him to bring his culturally-informed, family-inspired humor to a massive sports audience.

## Event Context and Reception

The ESPYs, or Excellence in Sports Performance Yearly Awards, are an annual ceremony celebrating the past year's top athletic achievements and moments, often blending sports with entertainment and celebrity. Hosting duties are typically given to comedians or personalities known for their sharp wit and ability to engage a diverse audience. Hernández's selection as host aligns with this tradition, following his rise on Saturday Night Live and the success of his Netflix special. The July 15 monologue — covered by TMZ and Yahoo Sports — marked his first time hosting the ceremony.
