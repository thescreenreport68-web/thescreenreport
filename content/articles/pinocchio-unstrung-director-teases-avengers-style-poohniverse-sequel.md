Director Rhys Frake-Waterfield is expanding his horror franchise with an [Avengers](/movies/mcu-movies-in-order/)-style crossover film featuring his twisted childhood icons. riety, the filmmaker teased a new project titled 'Poohniverse: Monsters Assemble.' This follows the upcoming release of his latest slasher, *Pinocchio Unstrung*, which premieres globally this summer.

Frake-Waterfield's concept would unite the monstrous versions of characters he has previously reimagined. The planned crossover would bring together the bloodthirsty incarnations of Winnie the Pooh, Bambi, Peter Pan, and Pinocchio into a single narrative. The director described the potential project as "kooky, crazy and fun," aiming for a tone reminiscent of ensemble superhero films.

## What is Pinocchio Unstrung about?

*Pinocchio Unstrung* is a British independent slasher film serving as a horror retelling of Carlo Collodi's classic novel. The plot follows Geppetto's grandson, James, who introduces a magical doll named Pinocchio to the world. Pinocchio's naive nature leads him to embark on a violent crusade to eliminate all things he perceives as bad. The film is the fifth installment in Frake-Waterfield's Twisted Childhood Universe.

## Who stars in the new horror film?

The cast features horror icon Robert Englund, famed for portraying Freddy Krueger, as the voice of the Cricket. Richard Brake, known for his roles in *31* and *Barbarian*, plays Geppetto. Jack Art Gray provides the voice of Pinocchio, with Cameron Bell as James. The supporting cast includes Jessica Balmer and Peter DeSouza-Feighoney.

## When will Pinocchio Unstrung be released?

The film had its world premiere at the Brussels International Fantastic Film Festival on April 11, 2026. It is scheduled for a wide theatrical release in North America and the United Kingdom on July 24, 2026. Distribution is being handled by ITN Distribution, with Viva Pictures releasing it in North America and Altitude Film Distribution handling the UK release.

## What's next for the Twisted Childhood Universe?

The teased 'Poohniverse: [Monsters](/movies/minions-monsters-trailer-drops-promises-wild-hollywood-origin-story/) Assemble' represents the next logical step for Frake-Waterfield's growing horror franchise. The director first gained notoriety with 2023's *Winnie-the-Pooh: Blood and Honey*, which spawned a sequel in 2024. His production company, Jagged Edge Productions, has steadily built a shared universe of public-domain characters reimagined as horror antagonists. No official production start date or release window has been announced for the crossover project.
