But the path to that billion-dollar milestone is paved with a divided audience. The conversation online isn't a unified victory lap — it's a passionate debate between fans who found the film deeply moving and those who walked away underwhelmed. Social media had thoughts — a lot of them.

## The Tear-Jerkers Are Winning

For a large chunk of the audience, the sequel hit all the right notes. The emotional core, a franchise staple, landed powerfully. One viewer captured the collective theater sentiment, writing “As [Toy Story 5](/movies/toy-story-5-fans-are-crying-and-cringing-at-the-same-time/) began, the lady behind me turned to her friend, ‘You’re crying already?’ ‘Yup!’ her friend replied. Can confirm many tears were shed, by them…and all.”

That reaction wasn't a one-off.

Beyond the tears, many praised the film's narrative choices. One detailed positive review called it a “genuine home run,” highlighting “Jessie being the lead really gives it an infectious energy, and the toys vs. tech conflict is much more nuanced than expected.” This focus on Jessie and a more complex conflict provided fresh energy. Co-writer Andrew Stanton hinted at this new direction, explaining “Lilypad is built like a toy in the sense that she wants to help the kid go forward, but she’s got very different skills and zero experience, whereas Jessie has nothing but experience and is probably unprepared for what to do.”

## The 'Meh' Crowd Speaks Up

But not everyone is buying it. Despite the commercial success, a vocal group found the film lacking. The most-liked critical post framed disappointment in stark comparative terms: “The Odyssey” secured the best preview screening gross of the year so far, beating “[Toy Story](/movies/toy-story-5-is-a-box-office-juggernaut-but-not-everyone-is-buying-it/) 5.” That single fact became a focal point for those questioning the sequel's cultural impact versus its financial one.

The criticism wasn't just comparisons. Many expressed a simple, personal letdown—it was alright.

There's also a palpable sequel fatigue bubbling beneath the surface. A post referencing animation fans' past reactions noted “Reminds me of how animation fans felt about Toy Story 5, and look how that turned out for them,” implying a cycle of skepticism. Even searching for info prompted frustration, with one person posting “I type in Toy Story 5 into Google Search so I can look up some info and........ uuuugggggh.”

## The Great Toy Story Debate

The divide speaks to the immense weight of expectations a Toy Story sequel carries. For some, returning to this world with Jessie at the forefront is an automatic win—heartfelt storytelling they crave. For others, the bar is impossibly high, and any sequel is viewed through a lens of comparison—to the franchise's golden era or standout films from other studios.

The Toy Story 5 reactions show that in the era of endless franchises, a film can be a financial triumph and an emotional touchstone for many while still failing to win over everyone. The conversation isn't about whether the film is successful, but what kind of success it represents. As the box office climbs, the debate between those who cried twice and those who shrugged continues. One thing is certain: nobody is done talking about this.
