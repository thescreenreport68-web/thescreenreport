The first teaser for a new French adaptation of Victor Hugo's *Les Misérables* has arrived, promising a powerful, non-musical take on the classic novel. According to GeekTyrant, the trailer previews an epic historical drama directed by Fred Cavayé, set to open in French theaters on October 14, 2026.

## What is this new adaptation about?

This version is not another musical. Instead, it tells Hugo's story as a sweeping period drama centered on redemption, justice, sacrifice, and revolution. The official synopsis details the journey of Jean Valjean, who emerges from a 19-year prison sentence a hardened man. He seeks redemption by reinventing himself, promising a dying woman to rescue her daughter, Cosette, all while being relentlessly pursued by Inspector Javert. Their fates intertwine amidst the social unrest of a turbulent, revolutionary Paris.

## Who is in the cast?

Vincent Lindon, an award-winning actor, leads the film as Jean Valjean. He is joined by Tahar Rahim, who takes on the role of the dogged Inspector Javert. The supporting cast includes Camille Cottin as Madame Thénardier, Benjamin Lavernhe as Thénardier, Noémie Merlant as Fantine, and Megan Northam as Cosette.

## When does it come out?

The film is scheduled for a release in French theaters on October 14, 2026. As of July 2026, a release date for the United States has not been announced. The project is produced by Studiocanal, with Cavayé writing and directing the adaptation of Hugo's landmark 1862 novel.
