Ryan Reynolds confirmed another Deadpool movie is on the way.

The actor made the announcement during an appearance at Fanatics Fest 2026, according to The Hollywood Reporter. The news follows the massive success of 2024's *Deadpool & Wolverine*, which became the top-grossing R-rated movie of all time.

## What Did Ryan Reynolds Say?

On stage at the event, Reynolds spoke about his desire to explore more of the character's comic book roots. A video of his comments was shared on X.

“There’s a few really deep cuts that I think are missing behind the movies. I think there’s a few deep cuts missing from some of the comics. There’s certainly some stuff that, you know, I love that Fabian Nicieza [co-creator of Deadpool] wrote that is incredible. Gerry Duggan, who was a friend, was an incredible comic writer,” Reynolds said. “There’s stuff upcoming. There’s eventually, you know, remotely another Deadpool film. It’s going to be great.”

Reynolds did not specify whether the new film would be another solo vehicle for his chaotic antihero or if Deadpool would be featured in a larger ensemble piece.

## What Was Previously Reported?

This confirmation follows a previous report from The Hollywood Reporter last year that Reynolds was exploring ideas for another movie in the franchise. That report indicated the project would feature X-Men characters. Reynolds has also been open about his initial reluctance to return for a fourth solo outing.

In a 2024 interview with The Hollywood Reporter, he explained his past hesitation. “Centering Deadpool works best if you take everything away from him and put his back against the wall. I can’t really do that again,” Reynolds said. “A fourth time feels a little iterative and redundant.”

## What's Next for Deadpool?

The franchise, which began with 2016's *Deadpool* and continued with 2019's *Deadpool 2*, reached new heights with 2024's crossover event *Deadpool & Wolverine*.

No further details about the next Deadpool film have been announced. The Hollywood Reporter noted it has reached out to Marvel for comment.
