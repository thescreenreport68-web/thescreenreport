Yet amid the scorn, a small vocal contingent defends the film's visual splendor and casting choices.

## The 'Carbon Copy' Complaint That Has Everyone Talking

The most common refrain among critics is that the remake offers nothing new. One user summed it up with a sarcastic question: "Disney: Hey, want a visually less appealing version of the same exact story shot for shot?" That sentiment was echoed by another viewer who noted, "They practically showed us whole movie in one trailer. Oh wait we've already seen the whole movie haven't we?"

Many questioned the very existence of a live-action version. "With this amount of CGI, why wouldn't I just watch the animated version?" one user asked, pointing to the inherent contradiction of using computer effects to replicate a medium that was already animated. Another joked, "It's like they asked Chat how a live action [Moana](/movies/catherine-laga-aia-and-dwayne-johnson-lead-moana-live-action-cast/) movie would look like and spit this out."

But the most pointed criticism came from a user who blamed the audience itself: "That soulless Lion King remake made well over a billion dollars. In this rare occasion, I blame the audience for all these shitty live-action remakes." This comment struck a nerve, capturing the frustration that Disney's remake pipeline continues because viewers keep showing up.

## The Rock's Maui Mojo? Missing

[Dwayne Johnson](/celebrity/wil-wheaton-slams-coward-dwayne-the-rock-johnson-over-keeping-his-poli/)'s portrayal of Maui drew particular ire. Another mocked his acting range: ""The Rock" is a fitting title because it perfectly describes his acting." A more specific complaint pointed to his physical performance: "Did Dwayne not want to make exaggerated movements or something? They wrote. Looks like he's doing as little as possible lol."

Some viewers went further, calling the film "an abomination against human rights" and urging others to "GO WATCH THE ORIGINAL MOANA..."

## Wait, Some People Actually Liked It?

Not everyone is buying the backlash. A small but vocal group praised specific elements, particularly the return of original voice actors. "One thing I noticed is Jemaine Clement is back to voice Tamatoa in this live action too," one user noted, while another exclaimed, "You don't know how happy I am that Tamatoa is Jemaine Clement! 'Shiny' song is safe."

New lead Catherine Laga'aia also earned praise. "I really enjoyed this live action version of MOANA. Catherine Laga'aia was absolutely a joy! Perfect casting. Not going to replace the animated movie, but, not a bad movie," wrote one viewer. Another enjoyed the meta-humor: "'It's true, I have beautiful hair.' I see what you did there, Disney."

## Fan Fatigue: The Straw That Broke the Moana Back

In the lead-up, executive producer Auli'i Cravalho spoke of "passing the baton to Laga'aia," while critic Lindsay Ellis noted the amusing irony that "Rachel House who played Moana's grandma in Moana, a 12-year-old movie, is still too young now to play her grandma in the remake." But the context of the film's release matters: many fans expressed a desire for a third animated film rather than a live-action retread. "Analysts think Disney's live-action Moana has one big problem: Fans wanted Moana 3, not another trip through the same story," one user shared.

As the conversation continues, one thing is certain: the discourse isn't cooling off any time soon. this is a net positive for society."
