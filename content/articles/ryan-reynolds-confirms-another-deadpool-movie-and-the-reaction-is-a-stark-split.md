## The Backlash: 'Ryan Reynolds Fatigue' Sets In

While the promise of more Deadpool content would typically dominate the conversation, a significant wave of response quickly pivoted to criticism of [Ryan Reynolds](/movies/ryan-reynolds-confirms-another-deadpool-movie-is-in-the-works/) himself. The critique centers on a perceived lack of range, with many viewers stating they feel they've seen this performance before—in every performance.

One user wrote "i see his acting range is...himself for the last 25 years." Another viewer posted "I am having Ryan Reynolds as Ryan Reynolds fatigue." This sentiment was echoed repeatedly, with posts like "Ryan Renyolds plays the same role in every movie or show he is in" gaining traction. The criticism didn't stop at artistic critique; for some, it curdled into outright personal disdain and hostile speculation about his character. One particularly vitriolic post accused "I hope everyone can see what a manipulator Ryan is. Blake and Ryan is match made in hell. Both equally evil and narcissistic." Another simply stated "Ryan just grosses me out. I never liked him." This undercurrent of personal hostility became a notable, if ugly, part of the discourse, extending far beyond film criticism into attacks on his marriage and personality.

## The Optimists: Focusing on the Franchise Future

Amid the noise of personal criticism, a contingent of fans focused squarely on the news they'd been waiting for: more Deadpool. Their reactions were celebratory and forward-looking, embracing Reynolds' confirmation and his tease of deeper comic book lore. The announcement was framed as the long-awaited next step for a beloved character, with fans sharing the news positively. The excitement was for the world of Deadpool itself, with Reynolds' involvement seen as a necessary and welcome constant.

## Why the Announcement Landed This Way

The divided reaction to this Ryan Reynolds Deadpool movie announcement speaks to a broader phenomenon in modern fandom, where an actor's public persona can become inseparable from their roles. For over a decade, Reynolds has cultivated a specific, witty, self-aware brand both on-screen and off, through social media and business ventures like Aviation Gin and Mint Mobile. For supporters, this consistency is part of his charm and the key to Deadpool's authenticity. For detractors, it has become a repetitive script, making it difficult to separate the character from the man. The announcement also arrived amidst promotion for his upcoming Cold War thriller 'Mayday' with Kenneth Branagh, leading some to conflate the projects and critique his casting there as more of the same. As one mixed reaction put it "Kenneth as a tough guy, interesting, Ryan Reynolds literally being Ryan Reyonds Lol." The conversation became less about a specific film and more about a referendum on a celebrity's entire ecosystem.

## What Comes Next for Deadpool and His Detractors

The path forward is now a dual challenge. Creatively, the team must mine those "deep cuts" from Nicieza and Duggan to deliver a Deadpool story that feels fresh and justifies the wait, potentially satisfying fans who crave more than the standard formula. Culturally, the reaction is a clear signal that a segment of the audience has reached a saturation point with the central star's persona. Whether future marketing can navigate this divide—leaning into the meta-commentary that is Deadpool's trademark to address the fatigue, or simply letting the work speak for itself—remains to be seen. For now, the confirmation of another film has done more than announce a project; it has held up a mirror to the complicated relationship between a star, his signature role, and an increasingly skeptical online audience.
