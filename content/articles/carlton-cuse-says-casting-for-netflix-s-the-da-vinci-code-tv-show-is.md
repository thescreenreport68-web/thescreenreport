The search for a new Robert Langdon is almost over, according to the co-creator of Netflix's upcoming series. Carlton Cuse revealed that the team is finalizing the casting for the lead role in the adaptation of Dan Brown's novel "The Secret of Secrets."

Cuse, speaking with ScreenRant, said the announcement is imminent. "I think we're soon going to announce who's going to be taking over as Robert Langdon in that franchise," he said. He confirmed the show will be an eight-episode version of the latest Langdon story for Netflix, adding, "We're getting close [to confirming the lead]."

## What is the new series about?

The project is not a direct remake of the 2006 film *The Da Vinci Code*, but a fresh adaptation of the newest book in Dan Brown's series. The novel "The Secret of Secrets" follows the symbologist Robert Langdon in Prague as he searches for his missing girlfriend, Katherine Solomon. The book, released in 2025, debuted on The New York Times Best Sellers list.

## Who played Robert Langdon before?

[Tom Hanks](/celebrity/tom-hanks-attends-taylor-swift-travis-kelce-s-wedding/) portrayed the character of Robert Langdon across three films over a decade, beginning with 2006's *The Da Vinci Code*. The most recent screen adaptation was the 2021 Peacock series *The Lost Symbol*, which starred Ashley Zukerman as a younger Langdon.

The series currently does not have a release date. According to AOL.com, the project is a reboot of *The Da Vinci Code* franchise for Netflix.
