The first look at the definitive documentary chronicling the historic Oasis reunion has arrived. Disney released the official teaser for *Don't Look Back in Anger*, the film capturing the Gallagher brothers' monumental 2025 world tour, according to Deadline.

## What is the documentary about?
*Don't Look Back in Anger* is an epic documentary that tells the story of the Oasis reunion the world was waiting for. The official synopsis promises unprecedented on-stage and backstage footage from the Gallagher brothers' sell-out global stadium tour. The film is positioned as an unapologetically uplifting account of the biggest musical event of 2025, capturing the experience and emotions of the band and their fans worldwide.

Beyond the concert footage, the documentary's unique perspective includes rehearsal access and, most notably, the first joint interviews with Noel and Liam Gallagher in over 20 years. In a statement, producer Steven Knight framed the project as more than just a concert film. "The Oasis world tour united generations, cultures and countries and spoke to a broken world about reconciliation," Knight said. "*Don't Look Back In Anger* is not only your ticket to the show – it's a backstage pass and a seat at the table when Liam and Noel sit down together for the first time in 15 years and tell it how it is and how it was."

## When and where can I watch it?
The documentary will have a theatrical premiere this September. It is scheduled to open in IMAX and cinemas before arriving for streaming on Disney+ and Hulu later in the year. The release marks a major cinematic event for music fans, offering a big-screen experience for the band's first performance together in 16 years.

## Who is behind the camera?
The film is a creative powerhouse. It is written, directed, and produced by BAFTA and Oscar-nominated Steven Knight, the creator of *Peaky Blinders*. He is joined by co-directors Dylan Southern and Will Lovelace, known for music documentaries like *Shut Up and Play the Hits*. The documentary is presented by Disney+ and comes from Magna Studios and Sony Music Vision.

The production team is equally stacked. It includes Oscar-winning sound mixers James Mather and Tarn Willers, cinematographer Haris Zambarloukos, and editors George Cragg and Martina Zamolo. This level of craft suggests the film aims to be a sensory immersion into the tour's atmosphere, not just a straightforward recap.

The documentary arrives on the one-year anniversary of Oasis's first reunion show, which kicked off in Cardiff, Wales in July 2025. The band's subsequent sold-out tour spanned the globe, uniting generations of fans and creating a cultural moment the film seeks to crystallize. With its blend of raw concert energy and intimate, long-awaited brotherly dialogue, *Don't Look Back in Anger* is poised to be the definitive record of a landmark event in rock history.
