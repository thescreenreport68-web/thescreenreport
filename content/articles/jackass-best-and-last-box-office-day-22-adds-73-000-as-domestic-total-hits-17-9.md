A Franchise Finale Fizzles Out

Johnny Knoxville and the infamous 'Jackass' crew have returned for what they promise is their last round of self-inflicted chaos. 'Jackass: Best and Last' marks the fifth and final chapter in the long-running franchise of outrageous stunts and pranks, billed as one last insane crusade for the aging daredevils. Directed by series stalwart Jeff Tremaine, the 92-minute film blends action, comedy, and documentary elements as it follows the crew's final, painful hurrah.

The cast features the familiar faces fans have come to expect, all playing heightened versions of themselves. Johnny Knoxville leads the pack as the ringleader of mayhem, with Steve-O, Chris Pontius, and Jason 'Wee Man' Acuña returning for more punishment. They are joined by Dave England, Preston Lacy, Ehren McGhehey, and Bam Margera, completing the original team for a final series of stunts that test the limits of human endurance and common sense.

## A Steep Box Office Collapse

The film's box office performance tells a story of rapid decline.

The severe **Jackass Best and Last box office drop** highlights a fundamental disconnect; a project marketed as a celebratory farewell, but one where almost half the runtime reportedly consists of recycled old clips, has failed to lure audiences back for a final goodbye.

## At the Box Office

Jackass: Best and Last has grossed $17,852,585 at the domestic box office, 22 days into its theatrical run, while playing across 305 theaters. The film added $73,000 in its most recent day of release. Worldwide, it has taken in $25 million. It carries a reported production budget of $10 million.
