Director Scott Derrickson publicly criticized [Elon Musk](/movies/elon-musk-vows-to-make-ai-generated-odyssey-film-by-end-of-year/) on social media in July 2026. The filmmaker behind "Doctor Strange" and "The Black Phone" delivered a blunt assessment of the billionaire's artistic judgment.

Derrickson addressed Musk directly on X, the platform Musk owns. "His opinions on art are worthless."

## What prompted the criticism?

The director's comments followed a pledge from Musk about filmmaking. The Tesla mogul vowed that before the end of the year, his AI tool Grok "will make a full-length movie of [The Odyssey](/movies/christopher-nolan-s-oscar-odyssey-can-matt-damon-and-mythology-deliver/) that is historically accurate and true to the art of Homer." Musk also shared a three-minute AI-generated clip adapted from the ancient poem.

## Has Musk criticized other films?

Yes. "The Odyssey" is not the first film Musk has criticized on his platform. He similarly mocked "[Barbie](/movies/barbie-box-office-2023/)" and Disney's "Snow White" reboot. Despite the anti-woke backlash, Nolan's "The Odyssey" opened strongly worldwide and has continued to sell out screenings months after its release.
