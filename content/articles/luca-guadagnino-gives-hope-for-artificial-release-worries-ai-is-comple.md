Luca Guadagnino has expressed hope for the release of his film 'Artificial' while sharing serious concerns about artificial intelligence's effect on the world.

The Oscar-nominated director made the comments in a recent interview, according to a report from Deadline. He noted he couldn't say much as the project is still in flux.

"We are right in the middle of this situation," Guadagnino said, prefacing his remarks about the film's status.

## What is the status of 'Artificial'?

The film, which dramatizes the brief ousting of OpenAI CEO Sam Altman, was dropped by its original distributor, Amazon MGM Studios, in mid-2026. The move came just days after Amazon signed a massive $50 billion deal with OpenAI. The reasons for the withdrawal were not specified, but the timing drew scrutiny. Producers have since been seeking a new distributor for the project, which is reportedly in the final stages of post-production.

Guadagnino's latest directorial effort, *After the Hunt*, premiered last year and starred Julia Roberts. His previous film, the hit tennis drama *Challengers*, was a critical and commercial success.

## What did Guadagnino say about AI?

Beyond the fate of his movie, the director voiced broader, more philosophical worries. He described the current state of AI development as profoundly unsettling. Guadagnino worries AI is "completely changing the world" and that its impact is "more than just disturbing."

His film *Artificial* is a timely exploration of these themes, focusing on a real-world corporate drama at the heart of the AI industry. The cast includes Andrew Garfield, Jason Schwartzman, and Monica Barbaro. The project began filming in Italy in mid-2025 with a planned 2026 release, a timeline now thrown into uncertainty.

Guadagnino is no stranger to complex, emotionally charged material. His filmography, which includes *Call Me by Your Name* and *Bones and All*, often examines desire and human connection.

## What's next for the director?

Despite the distribution hurdle for *Artificial*, Guadagnino remains a prolific filmmaker with several prospective projects. He has been linked to a new adaptation of *American Psycho*, with Austin Butler reportedly in talks to star. He was also previously attached to direct a new *Scarface* and an adaptation of *Separate Rooms*, though his involvement in those has ended.

He is next set to preside over the jury at the Marrakech International Film Festival, taking over for Thomas Vinterberg. His production company, Frenesy Film Company, continues to develop new work.
