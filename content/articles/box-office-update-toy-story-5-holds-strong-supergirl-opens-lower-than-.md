Pixar’s latest sequel is proving its staying power while a new DC hero struggles to find her footing. According to Deadline, **Toy Story 5** is holding onto the number one spot at the domestic box office, with a second weekend estimate of $74 million. That strong hold will push its domestic total over the $300 million mark. Meanwhile, the DC Universe’s *Supergirl* is flying lower than expected, with a projected $40 million opening weekend.

## How big is Toy Story 5’s second weekend?

The $74 million second-weekend estimate for *Toy Story 5* represents a solid performance. It shows the film has strong word-of-mouth and audience retention. This follows a record-breaking opening. The film earned $159.7 million domestically and $150.5 million internationally in its first three days. That $310.2 million global debut is now the biggest opening weekend in Pixar history. It surpassed *Inside Out 2* ($294.2 million) and *Toy Story 4* ($244.5 million), figures not adjusted for inflation.

As of June 25, the film had grossed $227 million in the United States and Canada and $152 million internationally. Its worldwide total stands at $379 million. The film carries a hefty $250 million [production](/movies/mira-sorvino-marks-first-day-of-romy-michele-sequel-filming-one-day-ha/) budget, making it Pixar’s most expensive film to date.

## Why is Supergirl underperforming?

The $40 million opening for *Supergirl* is a disappointment for the new DC Universe. Directed by Craig Gillespie and starring Milly Alcock, the film faced an uphill battle against a beloved, family-friendly juggernaut. *Toy Story 5*’s continued dominance likely siphoned away a significant portion of the audience. The film’s darker, revenge-driven premise may also have limited its broader appeal compared to the sunny, nostalgic pull of Pixar’s toys.

This result puts early pressure on the rebooted DC cinematic universe, which launched earlier this year. While audience reception will determine its legs, the opening suggests the franchise still has work to do to win back general audiences. The performance stands in stark contrast to the Pixar sequel’s robust run.

## What records has Toy Story 5 broken?

The film has already secured a major milestone. Its $310.2 million global opening weekend is now the highest in Pixar’s storied history. It also set a new benchmark for the *Toy Story* franchise itself. The achievement is a testament to the enduring global love for these characters nearly three decades after their debut.

Critics have largely embraced the film, which holds a 93% score on Rotten Tomatoes. The consensus praises its humor and heart while acknowledging it successfully tackles modern themes of technology and play. Director Andrew Stanton, who co-wrote the first four films, returned to helm this installment, marking the first mainline film without involvement from series co-creator John Lasseter.

## Can Toy Story 5 keep winning?

The path ahead looks favorable for Woody and Buzz. With a projected $74 million second weekend, the film is displaying the kind of holding power that leads to long, profitable runs. Family films often have multiple strong weekends throughout the summer. There are no major animated challengers on the immediate horizon.

Its success reaffirms the immense value of Pixar’s core franchises. It also demonstrates that a well-executed sequel can still captivate audiences and break records, even in a fifth outing.
