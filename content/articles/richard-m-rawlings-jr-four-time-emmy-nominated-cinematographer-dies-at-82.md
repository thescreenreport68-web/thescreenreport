Richard M. Rawlings Jr., a four-time Emmy-nominated cinematographer, has died at 82, according to a report from The Hollywood Reporter. His family announced he died on June 30 in Flagstaff, Arizona.

Rawlings Jr. was best known for his prolific television work. He and his father, Richard L. Rawlings, famously split duties on the third season of **ABC's *Charlie's Angels*** in 1978-79, with each handling half of the season's 22 episodes.

## What was his career highlight?

In February 2014, he received the Television Career Achievement Award from the American Society of Cinematographers. At the ceremony, *Charlie's Angels* star Cheryl Ladd paid tribute. "This talented man worked his ass off for years to make me look good," she said.

## What other shows did he work on?

His extensive career spanned decades and numerous hit series. After his father left *Charlie's Angels* to work on *Dynasty*, Rawlings Jr. continued as cinematographer for the show's final two seasons. He later shot episodes of *Matt Houston*, *L.A. Law*, *Boston Public*, and *Desperate Housewives*. He earned his four Emmy nominations for his work on *Ohara*, *Paradise*, *Reasonable Doubts*, and the telefilm *Big Dreams & Broken Hearts: The Dottie West Story*.

Richard Maxwell Rawlings Jr. was born in Hollywood on Oct. 9, 1943. He started in the industry as a messenger at Warner Bros. before working as an assistant camera operator for his father on shows like *Gilligan's Island*. He made his cinematographer debut on *Charlie's Angels* in 1978, reportedly becoming one of Hollywood's youngest D.P.s at the time. He is survived by his children, grandchildren, and sisters.
