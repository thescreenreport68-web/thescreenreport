Assaad Bouab and Caroline Dhavernas have joined the cast of the CBC crime drama Saint-Pierre for its upcoming third season, according to Deadline. Production on the new season is currently underway in St. John’s, Newfoundland and Saint-Pierre-et-Miquelon, France.

The show, which follows exiled Inspector Donald Fitzpatrick (Allan Hawco) and Deputy Chief Geneviève Archambault (Joséphine Jobert) solving crimes on the French island territory, is CBC's most-watched drama series, per Numeris TV Meter data.

## Who are the new actors playing?

French-Moroccan actor Assaad Bouab, known for *Call My Agent!*, will play Pascal Lalonde, a highly-decorated officer from mainland France sent to Saint-Pierre because of his record in taking down criminal organizations. Caroline Dhavernas, from *Hannibal*, will portray Victoria Peters, a well-educated businesswoman who plans to put Saint-Pierre on the map as a world-class tourist destination and forms a strong connection with Fitz.

## When will Season 3 premiere?

The plan is for Season 3 to premiere in winter 2027. Executive producer and star Allan Hawco expressed gratitude for the show's support. “We’re thankful to CBC, Fifth Season and our global audience for supporting us from the very beginning,” Hawco said. “The opportunity to return for Season 3 gives us the chance to make the most of our experienced production team and bring even more to the show’s visuals.”

Hawco Productions is producing the season for CBC and its streaming service CBC Gem, with Fifth Season handling international distribution. The returning ensemble cast includes Benz Antoine, Erika Prevost, and Jean-Michel Le Gal.
