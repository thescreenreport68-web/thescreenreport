Actress Britt Lower has opened up about her role and the surprise villain in Netflix's hit mystery series *I Will Find You*. According to Variety, the eight-episode adaptation of Harlan Coben's novel became the streamer's top TV show launch of the year following its debut on June 18, 2026. It later scored Netflix's highest second week of viewers for an English-language scripted series launch in 2026.

## Who Does Britt Lower Play?

Lower stars as Rachel Mills, a Boston Globe journalist who becomes convinced her ex-brother-in-law, David Burroughs (Sam Worthington), was wrongfully imprisoned for his son's murder. Her investigation kicks off the entire story after she spots a boy who looks like the supposedly dead child in a social media photo. "The [Hayden] twist really is connected to why Rachel has this instinct in the first place and everything just makes more sense," Lower said in an interview. "Oh, she had this Spidey sense, and it was connected to this person who was supposed to be like her best friend."

## Why Was the Villain Casting 'Perfect'?

The series' major twist reveals Rachel's ex-boyfriend, Hayden Payne (Milo Ventimiglia), as the kidnapper and murderer. Lower praised the unexpected casting choice. "That’s really good casting because Milo is the total opposite," she said. "He’s the loveliest, most gentle human." The character's violent actions, which include killing a detective and his own mother, starkly contrast with the actor's real-life demeanor.

## Is There a Romance Between Rachel and David?

The finale shows Rachel and David holding hands, sparking speculation about a romantic future. Lower offered a definitive interpretation. "I think it feels so earned based on everything we’ve been through together," she said. "They have this unspoken understanding of what the other person has been through, and that connection felt stronger than any kind of romance could be." She described the gesture as one of quiet support, not the start of a relationship.
