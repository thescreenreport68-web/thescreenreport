Warner Bros. Animation announced a new Dark Shadows animated series at the Annecy International Animated Film Festival on Friday. According to a report by Variety, the studio unveiled the project as part of its showcase, confirming a push into adult animation genres beyond comedy.

The show is a reimagining of the 1960s cult television series. It is currently in very early development, with no creative team, voice cast, or release window attached. The announcement signals Warner Bros. Animation's intent to diversify its adult-oriented output, which has historically leaned heavily on comedy franchises.

## What is Dark Shadows?

*Dark Shadows* is an American gothic soap opera that aired on ABC from 1966 to 1971. The show followed the wealthy, mysterious Collins family in the fictional town of Collinsport, Maine, weaving a complex tapestry of supernatural drama. It famously introduced the vampire Barnabas Collins, played by Jonathan Frid, who became the show's breakout character and propelled it to massive popularity.

The original series amassed 1,225 episodes over six seasons. Its blend of gothic romance, horror, and serialized storytelling attracted a massive teenage audience and a dedicated cult following, making it ABC's highest-rated daytime series by 1969. The franchise has seen numerous revivals, including two feature films in the 1970s, a 1991 TV remake, a 2012 Tim Burton-directed film adaptation, and ongoing audio dramas produced by Big Finish Productions.

## Why does this announcement matter?

This move represents a significant genre shift for Warner Bros. Animation's adult slate. The studio, known for hits like *Harley Quinn* and *Teen Titans Go!*, is now exploring gothic horror and supernatural drama. The enduring appeal of the original *Dark Shadows* lies in its rich mythology and beloved characters, which could translate well to an animated format.

An animated series allows for creative freedom with the show's signature supernatural elements—ghosts, witches, time travel, and parallel universes. It also opens the door to potentially involving original cast members in voice roles, as the Big Finish audio dramas have done for years. The project joins other recent Warner Bros. Animation announcements at Annecy, including a sneak peek of *Get Jiro!* and a tease for *Gerry Anderson’s Hit Squad*.

## What happens next?

With the project in its earliest stages, fans can expect a long wait before seeing any footage. The next steps will involve assembling a writers' room, finding a showrunner with a vision for the property, and casting voice actors. The key challenge will be capturing the distinctive, melodramatic tone of the original while updating it for a modern audience.

The success of this venture could lead to further explorations of classic genre properties in animation. For now, the announcement has successfully revived interest in the Collins family's haunted legacy.
