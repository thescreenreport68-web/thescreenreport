Mikayla Nogueira just went back to brown. The 28-year-old TikTok star debuted a new dark brunette shade on July 1, a swift reversal from the blonde look she'd been sporting for just a few weeks. So, what prompted the sudden switch? She asked her followers.

In a TikTok posted on June 30, Nogueira revealed the results of a poll she put out to her audience. The verdict was clear: over 10,000 followers voted for her to go brunette, compared to just 1,500 who wanted her to keep the blonde. The influencer framed the decision with her signature self-deprecating humor. "The internet has successfully bullied me," Nogueira admitted in the video, "into getting rid of this hair."

She elaborated on the strong reaction, noting, "I have never received such a negative reaction to a hair color until this." While she maintained a personal fondness for the lighter look—"Personally, I think the blonde’s kind of cute, especially in the summer"—she conceded to the popular opinion. "I just know—and we all know—there are better hair colors for me."

The POV Beauty founder cited practical reasons for the change as well, mentioning there were "a lot of issues" with the blonde 'do and that she had "important s--t coming up that my hair needs to look good for." "So I gotta fix this," she concluded.

True to her word, she documented the transformation in a GRWM (Get Ready With Me) video, ultimately landing on a dark brown shade. In a follow-up TikTok on July 1, she debuted the final look, declaring herself "done touching" her hair. According to her video, she stated, "This is my final form," and reportedly said she loved it, thought it was very pretty, and that it wasn't as harsh as black but had enough dimension to feel like her, adding that her natural hair was fried.

Nogueira, who maintained a signature jet black look for over a decade before her recent blonde experiment, has promised to stick with this color for a while. The episode highlights the unique, and sometimes overwhelming, dynamic between influencers and their audiences, where even a personal style choice can become a community decision.
