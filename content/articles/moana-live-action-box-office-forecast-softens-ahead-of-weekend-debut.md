Disney’s live-action *Moana* is heading into its opening weekend with tempered expectations, now projected to earn between $60 million and $65 million domestically according to *The Hollywood Reporter*. This is a dial-back from last month’s tracking, which suggested the film could debut as high as $75 million.

## How much is Moana projected to make?

The new forecast puts the film’s domestic weekend start in the $60-$65 million range. With a reported production budget of $250 million, the studio will need the film to perform as a family-friendly summer option for several weeks to turn a profit.

## What is the film’s competition?

The remake enters a competitive landscape. Disney’s own *[Toy Story 5](/movies/minions-monsters-north-america-box-office-hits-25m-in-2-days-but-can-i/)* has already crossed $800 million globally and is in its fourth weekend. The live-action *Moana* also follows closely on the heels of *Moana 2*, the animated sequel that became a smash hit in November 2024.

## What has the response been?

Critical reception has been mixed. In his review for *The Hollywood Reporter*, chief film critic David Rooney wrote that “this charming new iteration stands confidently on its own.” Star [Dwayne Johnson](/celebrity/wil-wheaton-slams-coward-dwayne-the-rock-johnson-over-keeping-his-poli/) has addressed the timing of the remake, telling *THR* at the premiere, “To be honest with you, I never bought into this idea [of], ‘You have to wait 20 years. You have you wait 30 years. It’s too soon.’”
