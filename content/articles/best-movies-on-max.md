**The best movies on Max offer a perfect blend of timeless classics, award-winning epics, and groundbreaking modern animation, all curated from the service's highest-rated titles.** This isn't just a list of what's available; it's a ranked guide to the films that have earned universal acclaim from both audiences and critics. From the enchanting worlds of Studio Ghibli to the gritty streets of Gotham City, these are the films worth your subscription.

## What Are the Highest-Rated Movies on Max?

Max's library is deep, but its highest-rated films, according to major audience aggregators, cluster in a few key genres: acclaimed anime, monumental fantasy, and genre-defining superhero cinema. The top-rated titles all share scores above 8.3/10, indicating near-universal praise. This ranking prioritizes those ratings alongside cultural impact and rewatchability. It answers the question: if you only have time for a few films, which ones deliver the most profound, entertaining, and memorable experiences available on the platform right now?

## 12 Best Movies on Max, Ranked

Here is our definitive ranking of the best movies currently streaming on Max, based on their ratings and significance.

1.  **Spirited Away (2001)** – Hayao Miyazaki's Oscar-winning masterpiece is a breathtaking journey into a spirit world that balances wonder, fear, and profound growth. It's the perfect introduction to Studio Ghibli's magic.
2.  **The Dark Knight (2008)** – Christopher Nolan's crime epic transcends the superhero genre. Heath Ledger's legendary performance as the Joker anchors a tense, morally complex story about chaos versus order.
3.  **The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King (2003)** – The epic conclusion to Peter Jackson's trilogy, this Best Picture winner delivers monumental battles, emotional closure, and filmmaking on a breathtaking scale.
4.  **Your Name. (2016)** – Makoto Shinkai's body-swap romance is visually stunning and emotionally devastating, proving modern anime can achieve widespread, heartfelt appeal.
5.  **Seven Samurai (1954)** – Akira Kurosawa's seminal epic is the blueprint for the team-assembly action movie. Its three-hour runtime is a masterclass in pacing, character, and black-and-white cinematography.
6.  **The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)** – The first film in the trilogy masterfully establishes the world, the fellowship, and the monumental quest, setting a new standard for fantasy adaptation.
7.  **The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers (2002)** – This middle chapter expands the scope of the war for Middle-earth, featuring the iconic battle of Helm's Deep and deepening the saga's themes.
8.  **Howl's Moving Castle (2004)** – Another Miyazaki classic, this film blends a whimsical, ever-changing castle with a poignant anti-war message and a transformative love story.
9.  **Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse (2023)** – A visual revolution in animation, this sequel pushes stylistic boundaries while telling a compelling, multiversal story that expands on the beloved original.
10. **Princess Mononoke (1997)** – Miyazaki's environmentally conscious epic is a complex, violent, and beautifully animated tale about the clash between industrial progress and nature.
11. **Perfect Blue (1998)** – Satoshi Kon's psychological thriller blurs the line between reality and delusion for a pop idol, influencing countless filmmakers with its tense, paranoid narrative.
12. **Modern Times (1936)** – Charlie Chaplin's timeless satire of industrial society remains hilarious and deeply human, showcasing his iconic Little Tramp character navigating the modern world.

## Why Are Anime Films So Highly Rated on Max?

A striking feature of Max's top-rated list is the dominance of anime, particularly works from **Studio Ghibli** and director **Makoto Shinkai**. These films earn their high ratings because they universally combine exquisite, hand-drawn (or hand-crafted digital) artistry with powerful, often emotionally mature storytelling. They are not "just cartoons"; they are complete cinematic experiences that explore themes of environmentalism, love, loss, and personal identity with a depth that resonates with global audiences. Max has secured a robust collection of these modern classics, making it a premier streaming destination for animation fans. From the ecological fury of *Princess Mononoke* to the urban melancholy of *Your Name.*, the platform offers a tour through anime's greatest achievements.

## Is Max Worth It for Movie Fans?

Absolutely. While Max is known for its HBO-originated series, its movie library is anchored by an impressive array of **enduring, high-quality films** rather than a revolving door of recent blockbusters. The consistent presence of the entire *The Lord of the Rings* trilogy, a deep bench of Studio Ghibli films, and landmark titles like *The Dark Knight* and *Seven Samurai* provides tremendous value. This collection caters to viewers seeking substantial, acclaimed films that reward multiple viewings. For fans of fantasy, animation, and cinematic classics, Max's curated movie selection is arguably one of the strongest and most stable among major streamers.
