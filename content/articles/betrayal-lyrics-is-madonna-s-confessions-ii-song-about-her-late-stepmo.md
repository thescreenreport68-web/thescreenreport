Is Madonna using her new album to revisit a decades-old family wound? The release of her upcoming album 'Confessions II' has fans zeroing in on one track in particular. The deluxe edition features a song called "Betrayal," and its lyrics are setting tongues wagging about a possible connection to the late Joan Ciccone, Madonna's stepmother, who passed away in 2024 after a battle with cancer.

The connection feels plausible to many given Madonna's own past reflections on her complex family history. Joan married Madonna's father, Silvio Ciccone, after the death of Madonna's mother when she was a child. In a 2002 interview with Larry King, Madonna recalled, "I mean, my mother died when I was little, and that was difficult for me for a while." She added, "I mean, truthfully, I didn’t accept my stepmother when I was growing up. In that retrospect I think I was really hard on her…and I’m very close to my father, and I didn’t want to accept change."

She expanded on that childhood shock on The Howard Stern Radio Show in 2015, saying, "I was almost 9 when my dad remarried. We were really shocked by it because we were still mourning and grieving my mother’s death, which my father never spoke about." The singer has also noted the absence of a maternal figure in her life, telling Rolling Stone at one point, "I had a father, I had older brothers. I did have a stepmother, but I didn’t have any relationship with her. So there was no role model for me."

With 'Confessions II' arriving on July 3, the album's title itself suggests a theme of personal revelation.
