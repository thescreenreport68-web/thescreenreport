Lionel Richie is back in his groove. The music icon took to Instagram this week to share a message with fans, his first since a health scare cut a concert short and landed him in the hospital.

Richie posted performance photos from his recent Pittsburgh and Detroit concerts, telling fans he's "doing well" and thanking them for their support. He ended the post by confirming his next stop is Toronto, signaling a full return to his tour schedule.

The update comes after a concerning incident on June 24. During a performance at the Grand Casino Arena in St. Paul, Minnesota, Richie said he felt "dizzy" and "strange," according to TMZ. He cut the show short and was taken to a hospital for treatment. Doctors later advised him to rest, leading to the postponement of his Chicago and Columbus, Ohio tour dates.

His return to the stage in Pittsburgh on Tuesday night appears to mark the end of that brief hiatus. The Instagram post, filled with photos of a smiling Richie performing, seems designed to reassure concerned fans that the "All Night Long" hitmaker is back on his feet.

While the exact cause of the dizzy spell has not been publicly disclosed, the swift return to touring suggests it was a temporary issue. For a star of Richie's caliber, who has maintained a relentless touring pace for decades, any interruption is notable. The episode highlights the physical demands of live performance, even for legends.

Fans flooded his social media with relief and well-wishes upon seeing the update. The post effectively serves as both a thank-you note and a health bulletin, putting speculation to rest with a simple, direct message: he's doing well and the show goes on.
