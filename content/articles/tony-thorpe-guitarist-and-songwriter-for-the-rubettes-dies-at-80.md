Tony Thorpe, guitarist and vocalist for the glam-rock band The Rubettes, has died at the age of 80, according to Billboard.

His death was announced in a statement posted to his official Facebook page.

## What did the announcement say?

The Facebook statement remembered him as "a father to Clay, a husband to Shirle, a friend to many, and a mentor to countless musicians young and old." It added that despite his declining health in recent months, "his fierce intellect and sharp sense of humour" never faltered.

## What was his role in The Rubettes?

Thorpe joined the band's classic lineup in 1974, serving as lead guitarist and a songwriter.

Following the news, frontman Alan Williams shared a tribute on the band's official Facebook page, calling Thorpe's brilliance with the guitar and pen undeniable and saying "Rest now Cowboy." Thorpe left the band in 1979 but remained musically active, working with other artists and teaching music.
