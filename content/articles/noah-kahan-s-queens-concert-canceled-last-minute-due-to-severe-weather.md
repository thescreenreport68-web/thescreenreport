Noah Kahan's outdoor concert at Citi Field in Queens was canceled on Saturday (July 18) due to inclement weather, according to Rolling Stone.

With heavy rain, severe thunderstorms, and strong winds forecast for the area, the decision was made after a last-minute attempt to delay the show. The venue initially pushed back door times and removed opening acts from the bill, but the conditions proved too risky for the stadium and artist's teams.

## What happened with the show?

The show was canceled shortly before it was set to begin, after some fans had already arrived at the stadium. In a statement, Citi Field officials called it a "very difficult decision" but emphasized that "the first priority is always to keep everyone safe." Kahan echoed this sentiment on social media, saying his team "exhausted every option to make this happen."

"I am so incredibly sorry," the singer wrote.

## Is the Sunday show still happening?

As of now, Kahan's second sold-out show at Citi Field, scheduled for Sunday night (July 19), remains on the calendar. The performance is contingent on the weather clearing. With his tour already booked solid through December, the Saturday show was canceled outright rather than rescheduled for a distant future date.

## What about other concerts in the area?

Kahan was not the only artist affected by the severe weather in Queens. The electronic duo Zeds Dead also had their scheduled performance at the nearby Forest Hills Stadium postponed. In a statement, the group said, "The weather is just being too difficult," citing an incoming storm and production delays.

## How do I get a refund?

For ticket holders of the canceled July 18 show, refunds are available from the original point of purchase. Citi Field stated that if tickets were purchased through Ticketmaster, refunds would be processed automatically.
