Mara Wilson discussed the potential for a sequel to the 1996 film *Matilda* while attending the Fanboy Expo in Tennessee, according to Deadline.

The former child star, who played the titular telekinetic prodigy, told the audience she is often asked about a follow-up film. "People always ask me about doing a *Matilda 2*. And that could be possible," she said, as reported by People.

## What Are Mara Wilson's Concerns About a Sequel?

Wilson expressed reservations about a common fan idea for a sequel plot. "But one thing that people always say is, ‘Oh, maybe it could be Matilda’s daughter.’ And I have some issues with that, because I think Matilda’s powers come from her adversity," she explained. She argued that Matilda's abilities stemmed from overcoming hardship and that a good parent would shield their child from such trauma. Wilson also joked about who could possibly be worthy of Matilda as a partner, asking, "Who’s good enough for Matilda?"

## How Does She Feel About Being Called 'Matilda' Now?

Wilson also reflected on her past discomfort with being so closely linked to the character. "I used to be a little annoyed when people would call me Matilda because I was like, ‘No, I’m Mara,'" she recalled. She compared it to being called by a cooler sibling's name. Her perspective has since shifted, inspired by Carrie Fisher's relationship with Princess Leia. Wilson now sees Matilda as a role model, noting they share values like advocating for mental health and supporting public libraries. "I feel like Matilda has really taught me a lot of good lessons," she said.
