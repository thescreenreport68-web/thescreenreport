## The Coffin, the Snakes, and the Dark Humor

While the mood was largely light, the catalyst for the conversation was anything but. Elordi, who was absent for much of the series, returned to film a death scene for *Euphoria*'s final season. He described the experience with a sense of dark humor that both horrified and fascinated the audience.

The actor revealed the shoot was physically grueling from the start. "It was like, six weeks of me just getting beat by a seven-foot guy. I was just running the whole time,"

But the pinnacle of the ordeal was his character's death. In a line that quickly became the interview's standout soundbite, Elordi detailed, "I got locked in a coffin for two days straight with prosthetics and a fake tongue, and I couldn’t see out of my eyes. And then Sam put snakes into the coffin."

He framed the intense process as a form of deliberate torment by director Sam Levinson, albeit one he approached with a wry smile. The shocking description of being locked in a coffin with snakes served as the stark counterpoint to the interview's otherwise warm atmosphere, highlighting the extreme contrasts of an actor's job.

Despite the ordeal, or perhaps because of the way he contextualized it with humor, Elordi's overarching feeling about *Euphoria* remained one of profound appreciation. "It really changed my whole life. It’s an incredible thing," "I don’t know if I’ll ever fathom the sort of size of that show and what it did for me."
