The *Toy Story 5* soundtrack has made a major splash on the Billboard charts. According to Billboard, the album debuted in the top 10 across three of its album charts dated July 4, including a No. 1 start on the Kid Albums tally.

Led by [Taylor Swift](/music/stevie-nicks-performs-at-taylor-swift-and-travis-kelce-s-wedding/)'s new original song “I Knew It, I Knew You,” the set also entered at No. 2 on the Soundtracks chart and No. 9 on the Independent Albums ranking. The album, released via Pixar/Walt Disney Records and largely comprised of Randy Newman’s score, also entered at No. 67 on the overall Billboard 200 albums chart. That marks the highest-charting *Toy Story* album ever on that ranking.

## When was the album released?
The *Toy Story 5* soundtrack was released on June 19 and was initially available on streaming services and as a digital download. A CD and vinyl release for the set is scheduled for September 18.

## What about the lead single?
The album's arrival was preceded by the release of Swift's song, which achieved significant chart success. According to Billboard, “I Knew It, I Knew You” also became just the third Hot 100 No. 1 from an animated Disney movie, and the first from its Pixar studio.
