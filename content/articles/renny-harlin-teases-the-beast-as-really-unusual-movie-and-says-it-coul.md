Renny Harlin is betting his upcoming film *The Beast* will be a major moment for its star, Joel Kinnaman. In a wide-ranging interview for Fangoria's 'The Séance at Cannes,' the veteran director teased the project as a 'really unusual movie' that could redefine the actor's career, while also detailing the ambitious production of his *The Strangers* trilogy and reflecting on the hustle that launched his Hollywood journey.

## What did Renny Harlin say about 'The Beast'?
Harlin was bullish about the potential of his next film. While specific plot details remain under wraps, he positioned *The Beast* as a standout project for its lead. 'People are going to be stunned,' Harlin said, directly linking the film's impact to Kinnaman's trajectory. He framed it as the kind of role that can serve as a definitive career boost for an actor, suggesting it will showcase Kinnaman in a transformative light.

## What's the latest on 'The Strangers' trilogy?
The director revealed that all three films in the new *The Strangers* series were shot simultaneously, a logistical challenge he called a 'new experiment.' He admitted the first chapter was designed to closely echo the 2008 original, a 'calculated gamble' they knew would draw criticism from purists. The strategy, however, was to use that foundation to launch the story into uncharted territory with the sequels.

Harlin said the response to the first film directly influenced additional shooting for parts two and three. A key insight was the audience's deep curiosity about the masked antagonists' origins. 'We realized that the audience was very curious about the strangers and where they come from and what their backstory is,' he explained. This led to reshoots that added more depth and new angles to the later installments. The second film, *The Strangers: Chapter 2*, is scheduled for a wide theatrical release on September 26.

## How did Renny Harlin break into Hollywood?
The Finnish filmmaker's origin story is a classic tale of relentless hustle. After making the low-budget action film *Born American* as a calling card, he arrived in Los Angeles with little more than a VHS tape. His first stateside break came when producer Irvin Yablans, who had discovered John Carpenter, saw the tape and offered him the horror film *Prison*. That film also marked the big-screen debut of a then-unknown actor named Viggo Mortensen, whom Harlin fought to cast.

His persistence reached its peak with *A Nightmare on Elm Street 4: The Dream Master*. With a writers' strike halting production and no script or director, Harlin repeatedly showed up at New Line Cinema without an appointment. He eventually wore down president Bob Shaye by presenting detailed storyboards for the film's nightmare sequences. 'I went back six times and finally I was able to get into Bob's office,' Harlin recalled. The film's successful test screening was a career-defining moment of validation.

## Why is Harlin excited to be back in horror?
For Harlin, returning to the genre is a homecoming driven by creative freedom and audience connection. He draws a direct line between his work in horror and action, emphasizing that both are about visual storytelling, camera work, and crafting a visceral reaction. 'The best feeling for a filmmaker... is to see people react,' he said. He described the joy of manipulating an audience's expectations, whether by delivering a predictable scare or subverting them entirely, and praised the horror community as the most passionate fans.
