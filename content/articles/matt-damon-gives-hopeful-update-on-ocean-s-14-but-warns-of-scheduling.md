[Matt Damon](/movies/matt-damon-says-next-bourne-movie-is-taking-shape-with-director-edward/) says the long-awaited sequel Ocean's 14 is moving forward but faces a major scheduling challenge. The actor provided the update during a recent interview.

He stated the film is "very much in development" and that all the stars are excited to get started. Damon appeared on the Rich Eisen Show to discuss the project.

## What is the main problem with Ocean's 14?

The central issue is logistics. Damon explained that the difficulty lies in aligning the calendars of the film's extensive, high-profile ensemble cast. "Because it's just everybody's schedules," he said. "You got to get you got to get everything right and make and I mean I think they're they're probably close on that one."

This scheduling conflict means production will be delayed significantly, according to Damon's comments. Despite the hurdle, he offered a note of optimism, suggesting the team is closer than ever to making the film a reality.

## What did George Clooney say about the sequel?

Earlier this year, George Clooney confirmed that production for Ocean's 14 was expected to get underway in October. He also revealed plot details, stating the movie would draw inspiration from the 1979 film Going in Style, which revolves around aging bank robbers.

Clooney confirmed the latest installment would be a star-studded affair, with plans for Don Cheadle, Andy Garcia, Julia Roberts, Brad Pitt, and Matt Damon to all team up again. Damon's latest comments indicate the October start date may now be pushed back as the production works to solve its scheduling puzzle.

The status of Ocean's 14 remains in development, according to the report from Yahoo.
