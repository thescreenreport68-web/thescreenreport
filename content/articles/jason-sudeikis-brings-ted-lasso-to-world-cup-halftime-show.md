Jason Sudeikis made a surprise cameo as his beloved character Ted Lasso during the halftime show of the FIFA World Cup Final, according to a report from The Hollywood Reporter. The actor appeared in character on Sunday to offer encouragement to co-headliner [Justin Bieber](/celebrity/jason-sudeikis-surprises-as-ted-lasso-at-justin-bieber-s-world-cup-halftime/).

Sudeikis was joined by Brendan Hunt, who reprised his role as Coach Beard. In the segment, the fictional coaches discussed who could bring the right energy to the massive event. **"I think we need some heart, someone that has some soul, someone that can bring the love,"** Sudeikis said to Hunt. Bieber then joined them on the field.

## What did Ted Lasso say to Justin Bieber?

Turning to the pop star, Sudeikis, as Lasso, delivered a pep talk. "Alright, young man, it’s your big shot. Now listen to me. Go out there, don’t be nervous. Nobody’s watching. Make us proud," he said. Bieber then performed his song "Everything Hallelujah" for the global audience.

The cameo was part of the first-ever halftime show staged for a World Cup Final, an 11-minute production that also featured performances from Madonna, BTS, Coldplay, and Shakira with Burna Boy.

## When is 'Ted Lasso' coming back?

The surprise appearance arrives just ahead of the series' return.
