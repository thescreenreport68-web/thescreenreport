Charli XCX released the music video for her new single "Camera" on July 21.

The track is the fourth and final single from her upcoming album "Music, Fashion, Film", which is set for release on July 24. The British pop star co-directed the black-and-white visual with frequent collaborator Aidan Zamiri, who directed the film *The Moment*.

## Who stars in the 'Camera' music video?

The video stars French actor Vincent Cassel. In the clip, Cassel takes multiple hits to the chest, with fake blood oozing from his mouth, as Charli XCX directs the chaotic action from the sidelines of a film set. The visual is set in the aftermath of a fiery car crash, per Pitchfork.

## What happens in the video?

Charli moves around the set, giving orders to the cast and crew. She vocalizes a desire to break from a monotonous life, singing lyrics like "I'm always craving an experience that feels dangerous." The video completes the thematic rollout for "Music, Fashion, Film", which followed singles dedicated to music and fashion.

## When is the new album out?

"Music, Fashion, Film" arrives on Friday, July 24. The 11-track album will feature an appearance from director David Cronenberg. This project is her formal follow-up to 2024's *Brat*, which reached No. 3 on the Billboard 200 and won the Grammy for best dance/electronic album in 2025.

## What's next for Charli XCX?

The singer will hit the road for a North American arena tour in support of the album, slated to kick off in Philadelphia on Sept. 11. She will also appear in the film *I Want Your Sex*, which hits theaters on July 31.
