“I’m actually very happy, very just thrilled, and just my heart is so full to let you guys know that Hannah has been released from the hospital, which is the best news we could have right now,” Armando Rubio said in a three-part Instagram video on Monday. The “90 Day Fiancé” star emotionally announced that his 13-year-old daughter, Hannah, has been discharged from an Arizona hospital after suffering a brain bleed.

Her condition improved dramatically after Rubio was finally able to cross the border from Mexico to be at her side. His husband, Kenneth Neidermeier, noted the change. He added that the doctors were so pleased they felt Hannah would do better in a home setting, saying, “The doctors were so happy with her progress that they said, ‘Now, I think she would do better with you guys in a home setting.’”

## The Road Ahead

Hannah is not yet in the clear. She must remain in the Phoenix area for another month so doctors can supervise her care. The cause of her brain bleed remains unknown, a fact Rubio called “unsettling.” Hannah is now on anti-seizure medication and still has partial blindness in one eye. She will also need rehabilitation to strengthen the muscles in her face.

The couple profusely thanked fans and fellow “90 Day Fiancé” castmates for their support. Rubio also tearfully thanked his husband for caring for Hannah and being by her side when he couldn't.

The story is a confirmed health update from the family.
