The first full trailer for '[Avengers: Doomsday](/movies/robert-downey-jr-s-doctor-doom-mask-revealed-in-avengers-doomsday-concept-a/)' is imminent, according to a new audio teaser released by Marvel. The studio appended the brief, ominous clip to its regular Sunday night post on the official Fantastic Four social media account, signaling the marketing campaign is about to kick into high gear.

According to a report from Digital Spy, the teaser features a dialogue exchange between Reed Richards (Pedro Pascal) and Sue Storm (Vanessa Kirby). After Reed says "no matter what," Sue asks, "How long do we have?" He ominously replies, "Not long." The outlet suggests this could be a direct tease for the first full trailer, which is widely expected to debut this week, possibly timed with a Comic-Con panel or the start of ticket sales.

## What is 'Avengers: Doomsday' about?

The film, directed by Joe Russo, will bring together heroes from three distinct universes on a deadly collision course. The official synopsis promises they will face an existential threat unlike anything they've ever encountered. This positions the project as a massive, multiversal team-up event within the [Marvel](/movies/fans-embrace-a-darker-joking-spider-man-in-brand-new-day-but-some-are-tired-of/) Cinematic Universe.

## Who is in the cast?

The ensemble is one of the largest in Marvel history. Robert Downey Jr. returns as Victor von Doom, with Chris Evans back as Steve Rogers and Chris Hemsworth as Thor. They will be joined by the new Fantastic Four—Pedro Pascal as Reed Richards, Vanessa Kirby as Sue Storm, and Ebon Moss-Bachrach as Ben Grimm—alongside other heroes like Paul Rudd's Scott Lang, Anthony Mackie's Sam Wilson, and Florence Pugh's Yelena Belova.

## When does it come out?

With the first teaser now out, the full trailer is expected to arrive soon, giving fans a much clearer look at the scale of the impending conflict.
